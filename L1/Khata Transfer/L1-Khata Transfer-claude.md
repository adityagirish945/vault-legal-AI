## **1\. LEGAL DEFINITION — WHAT KHATA TRANSFER IS, WHEN TRIGGERED, AND LEGAL EFFECT**

**Definition:** A Khata (also spelled Khatha) is the official municipal property account maintained by BBMP (Bruhat Bengaluru Mahanagara Palike) for every property within its jurisdictional limits. The word "Khata" derives from the Kannada/Urdu term for "account." It is a municipal ledger entry — not a title document — that records: the property owner's name, property location and address, site dimensions, built-up area, Property Identification Number (PID), annual property tax liability, and tax payment history.

**Khata Transfer** is the formal administrative process by which BBMP updates its property account records to substitute the name of the previous owner with the name of the new owner following any legal change in property ownership. It is, in essence, a "mutation" of the municipal tax record.

**Triggering Events — Khata Transfer is compulsorily required upon:**

* Purchase of property through registered sale deed  
* Inheritance of property on the death of the registered owner (testate or intestate)  
* Receipt of property through a registered gift deed  
* Court-decreed transfer of ownership  
* Allotment of property by a government body (BDA, KHB, etc.) to a buyer  
* First transfer to a flat purchaser from a builder/developer in a new apartment complex  
* Property received through a government auction  
* Relinquishment deed or family settlement deed

**Legal Effect of Khata Transfer:** Khata Transfer does NOT confer ownership or title — that is the function of property registration. However, its legal effects are substantial:

1. The new owner becomes the assessed person for property tax purposes under BBMP.  
2. It is conclusive evidence of the owner's municipal identity for the property.  
3. It is a prerequisite for obtaining trade licenses, building plan approvals, and occupancy certificates from BBMP.  
4. Banks and financial institutions require a valid Khata (in the applicant's name) before sanctioning home loans, loan against property, or any mortgage.  
5. It is required for applying for water connections (BWSSB), electricity connections (BESCOM), and other civic utilities.  
6. Without Khata Transfer, accumulated property tax continues to be assessed in the previous owner's name, creating ambiguity and potential arrears liability.  
7. It constitutes the primary municipal record for dispute resolution, property identification, and land record reconciliation.

**Types of Khata under BBMP:**

* **A Khata (Form A):** Issued to properties that have all requisite approvals, comply with BBMP building bylaws, zoning regulations, and sanctioned layouts. A Khata holders can avail home loans, apply for building plan approvals, trade licenses, and occupancy certificates.  
* **B Khata (Form B / Register B):** Issued under Section 108A of the Karnataka Municipal Corporations Act, 1976 (inserted by amendment in 2009\) to properties with irregularities — unapproved layouts, properties on revenue/agricultural land not converted for non-agricultural use, or deviations from sanctioned plans. A Karnataka High Court order of December 2014 classified B Khata properties as effectively illegal for transactional purposes. B Khata holders face severe restrictions on loans, building approvals, and resale.  
* **E-Khata:** The digitised, electronic version of the Khata record. As of 2024–2025, BBMP has mandated E-Khata for all properties in BBMP limits. E-Khata is issued through the BBMP E-Aasthi portal (https://bbmpeaasthi.karnataka.gov.in) and is the current operational format replacing physical Khata certificates.

## **2\. HOW KHATA TRANSFER DIFFERS FROM PROPERTY REGISTRATION — WHY BOTH ARE SEPARATELY NEEDED**

**Property Registration** (under the Registration Act, 1908 and the Indian Stamp Act, 1899\) is the process of recording a conveyance instrument (sale deed, gift deed, will probate, etc.) in the official Sub-Registrar's Office (SRO) records under the Department of Stamps and Registration, Government of Karnataka. Registration:

* Establishes legal title and ownership in the eyes of law  
* Is governed by the Sub-Registrar under the Inspector General of Registration (IGR)  
* Creates an immovable public record of the conveyance transaction  
* Involves payment of stamp duty (currently 5%–6.7% of property value in Karnataka, depending on category and gender of buyer) and registration fee (1% of property value)  
* Gives the new owner a registered sale deed — the foundational title document

**Khata Transfer** is a separate, subsequent process under the BBMP's Revenue Department:

* It updates the municipal tax account — it does NOT establish legal title  
* It is governed by BBMP under the Karnataka Municipal Corporations Act, 1976 (KMC Act)  
* It involves a separate application to BBMP (not the Sub-Registrar)  
* It involves a separate fee (2% of stamp duty paid, plus other charges)  
* It is administered by the Assistant Revenue Officer (ARO) of the relevant BBMP zone

**Why Both are Needed:** The Sub-Registrar's Office and BBMP are completely separate government bodies with no automatic data synchronisation (despite ongoing e-governance initiatives). Completing property registration does not automatically trigger Khata Transfer. The legal ownership record (at IGR/SRO) and the municipal account record (at BBMP) must each be separately updated. Failure to complete Khata Transfer means:

* The new owner cannot legally pay property tax in their own name  
* The property remains on the municipal rolls under the previous owner  
* The new owner cannot access BBMP services tied to the property  
* Banks will not process home loans since Khata and title must match

A common analogy: Property registration is like purchasing a vehicle — Khata Transfer is like getting the RC Book transferred to the new owner's name. Both are legally necessary and neither substitutes for the other.

## **4\. STEP-BY-STEP OFFICIAL PROCESS — THREE SCENARIOS**

### **4(a) TRANSFER AFTER SALE (Purchase via Registered Sale Deed)**

**Pre-requisites:**

* Sale deed must be registered at Sub-Registrar's Office  
* All property taxes must be cleared (no arrears in the seller's name)  
* Encumbrance Certificate (EC) must show no pending dues or charges

**Step 1 — Obtain and Verify Documents:** Collect all required documents (see Section 5 below). Verify that the property details (survey number, PID, address, dimensions) are consistent across the sale deed, Encumbrance Certificate, previous Khata, and tax receipts.

**Step 2 — Pay Pending Property Tax:** Confirm all property tax dues are cleared up to the current financial year. Pay any outstanding amount through the BBMP Property Tax portal (https://bbmptax.karnataka.gov.in) or at any BBMP office or Bangalore One centre. Obtain payment receipt.

**Step 3 — Access E-Aasthi / Sakala Portal:** Visit https://bbmpeaasthi.karnataka.gov.in or https://sakala.kar.nic.in. Register as a new user with mobile number and email. Log in and navigate to "Khata Transfer" under property services.

**Step 4 — Fill Application Form (Form 11 / Online Form):** Enter: new owner's full name, address, Aadhaar number, contact details; property details (PID, survey number, ward, zone, area); nature of transfer (sale); previous owner's details.

**Step 5 — Upload Documents:** Scan and upload all required documents in the specified format (typically PDF, max size per document). Ensure clarity and completeness of each uploaded file.

**Step 6 — Pay Transfer Fee:** Pay the Khata transfer fee online (2% of stamp duty paid on the registered sale deed, minimum ₹500). Payment can be made via credit/debit card, net banking, or UPI. Retain the digital payment receipt. If applying via Bangalore One centre, payment can be made in person using the Sakala application number.

**Step 7 — Obtain Acknowledgement / Sakala Number:** On successful submission, an acknowledgement receipt with a unique Sakala application number is generated. This number is used for tracking the application status (via SMS alerts and the Sakala portal). Retain this receipt.

**Step 8 — BBMP Verification and Site Inspection:** The ARO or designated revenue inspector reviews the application and documents. A physical site inspection may be conducted to verify property dimensions, construction status, and identity of the occupant. The inspector cross-checks details with BBMP's existing records.

**Step 9 — Approval and E-Khata Issuance:** Once verification is complete and no discrepancies are found, the ARO approves the transfer. A new E-Khata certificate in the new owner's name is issued digitally. The new owner can download the E-Khata certificate from the BBMP E-Aasthi portal.

**Step 10 — Obtain Khata Certificate and Khata Extract:** The Khata Certificate (₹25 per copy) confirms that the property tax records are in the new owner's name. The Khata Extract (₹100 per copy) contains detailed property information. Both are available for download or collection at a BBMP office / Bangalore One centre.

### **4(b) TRANSFER AFTER DEATH / INHERITANCE**

Inheritance Khata Transfer applies when the registered property owner dies and the property passes to legal heirs — either through a registered will (testate succession) or without a will (intestate succession, governed by personal law — Hindu Succession Act 1956, Indian Succession Act 1925, Muslim Personal Law, etc.).

**Step 1 — Establish Legal Heirship:** Obtain a Legal Heir Certificate (issued by the Tahsildar of the relevant taluk, on application to the Revenue Department) OR obtain a Succession Certificate (issued by a civil court under the Indian Succession Act, 1925\) OR obtain a Probate of Will (if death was testate and property is immovable, court probate may be required in Karnataka).

**Step 2 — Obtain Death Certificate:** Obtain the registered death certificate of the deceased owner from the relevant municipal authority or hospital.

**Step 3 — Prepare Affidavit / No-Objection:** If there are multiple legal heirs, an affidavit from all heirs on stamp paper (typically ₹200 stamp paper) declaring consent for Khata to be transferred to one heir, or to be jointly transferred, is required. A No-Objection Certificate (NOC) from other heirs is also typically required.

**Step 4 — Clear Property Tax:** Verify all property tax is current. Pay any arrears accrued during the deceased owner's period. Obtain latest tax paid receipt.

**Step 5 — Apply via E-Aasthi / Sakala Portal or BBMP Office:** Submit application form with full details — the applicant is the legal heir or all legal heirs jointly. Select "Inheritance / Death" as the reason for transfer.

**Step 6 — Upload All Required Documents:** Upload the complete document checklist (see Section 5(b) below).

**Step 7 — Pay Transfer Fee:** Transfer fee applies: 2% of the property's stamp duty value (as per the last registered transaction), minimum ₹500. Note: for inherited properties that were never the subject of a registered sale deed (e.g., ancestral properties), a reasonable assessed value is used for fee computation.

**Step 8 — BBMP Verification:** BBMP verifies documents, confirms the death, legal heirship, and property details. A physical inspection may be conducted. The ARO reviews any competing claims or litigation flags.

**Step 9 — Approval and E-Khata Issuance:** After successful verification, E-Khata is issued in the heir's name. All heirs' names may be recorded if jointly inherited.

---

### **4(c) TRANSFER AFTER GIFT DEED**

A gift deed (registered under Section 17 of the Registration Act, 1908 read with Transfer of Property Act, 1882, Sections 122–129) transfers property by way of voluntary, irrevocable gift without consideration. It is typically used between family members.

**Step 1 — Ensure Gift Deed is Registered:** The gift deed must be compulsorily registered at the Sub-Registrar's Office. An unregistered gift deed has no legal validity for immovable property and will be rejected by BBMP.

**Step 2 — Clear All Property Tax:** All outstanding property tax on the property must be cleared before the transfer application is filed.

**Step 3 — Apply via E-Aasthi / Sakala Portal:** Log into the portal, fill the Khata Transfer application, and select "Gift Deed" as the reason for transfer.

**Step 4 — Upload Documents:** Upload all required documents (see Section 5(c) below), including the original registered gift deed and all supporting documents.

**Step 5 — Pay Transfer Fee:** Transfer fee: 2% of the stamp duty value reflected on the gift deed (stamp duty paid at the time of gift deed registration is the base). Minimum fee: ₹500.

**Step 6 — Verification and Approval:** BBMP verifies the gift deed, property records, and tax payment status. A site inspection may be conducted. On approval, the Donee (recipient of gift) is entered as the new Khata holder.

**Step 7 — Download E-Khata:** New E-Khata is issued in the Donee's name and can be downloaded from the E-Aasthi portal.

## **8\. LEGAL PROVISIONS**

### **8.1 Karnataka Municipal Corporations Act, 1976 (KMC Act)**

* **Section 108** — Provides for assessment of properties within the corporation limits and the maintenance of the property tax register, which is the basis for the Khata record.  
* **Section 108A** — (Inserted by amendment in 2009\) Provides for the Register B, which is the legal basis for B Khata. Properties that do not comply with all approvals but are paying property tax are entered into this register.  
* **Section 114** — Deals with the liability of owners and occupiers to pay property tax and their responsibilities in notifying the corporation of changes in ownership.  
* **Sections 144–147** — Deal with assessment and reassessment of property tax, including the obligation to notify the corporation upon transfer of ownership.

### **8.2 Bruhat Bengaluru Mahanagara Palike Act / BBMP Act**

* The **BBMP Act, 2020** (Karnataka Act 32 of 2020\) governs the Bruhat Bengaluru Mahanagara Palike. While it largely mirrors the KMC Act framework for property tax and Khata administration, it establishes BBMP-specific procedures for property assessment and municipal records.

### **8.3 Karnataka Town and Country Planning Act, 1961**

* Governs the legal basis for **betterment charges** collected in connection with land development, conversion, and layout approvals.  
* Sections providing for improvement schemes and betterment levy empower BBMP to collect betterment charges before issuing A Khata for converted/regularised properties.

### **8.4 Karnataka Sakala Services Act, 2011 (Karnataka Act 33 of 2011\)**

* Mandates time-bound delivery of Khata Transfer (and other specified services) and provides for grievance redressal and officer accountability for delays.

### **8.5 Registration Act, 1908 (Central Act)**

* Section 17 mandates compulsory registration of instruments of sale, gift, and exchange of immovable property valued above ₹100. Only a registered instrument forms the legal basis for Khata Transfer.

### **8.6 Transfer of Property Act, 1882 (Central Act)**

* Governs the underlying legal validity of sale deeds (Sections 54–57), gift deeds (Sections 122–129), and other modes of transfer. BBMP Khata Transfer is consequential to valid transfer under this Act.

### **8.7 Relevant Government Orders (GOs)**

* **GO authorising E-Khata (2024):** The Government of Karnataka, through its Urban Development Department, issued a GO mandating E-Khata for all BBMP properties, with the E-Aasthi platform as the official digital repository.  
* **Greater Bengaluru Governance Act (GBG Act), 2024:** Enacted to transition BBMP to the Greater Bengaluru Authority (GBA). Includes provisions for rationalisation of Khata records and the B Khata to A Khata conversion scheme. Properties registered on or before 30 September 2024 are eligible for B-to-A Khata conversion under this scheme.

## **9\. COMMON REJECTION REASONS AND HOW TO RESOLVE THEM**

### **9.1 Incomplete Documentation**

**Cause:** Missing any of the required documents (e.g., EC not covering the full period, missing latest tax receipt, no photographs, absent NOC from co-heirs). **Resolution:** Carefully prepare a document checklist before applying. Ensure EC covers the period from the date of last registration up to the current date. Collect the latest tax receipt for the current assessment year. Re-submit the complete application.

### **9.2 Pending Property Tax Dues**

**Cause:** Outstanding property tax in the previous owner's name. BBMP will not process Khata Transfer if there are arrears. **Resolution:** Pay all outstanding property tax dues (including interest and penalties if any) through BBMP's Property Tax portal (https://bbmptax.karnataka.gov.in) or at a BBMP office. Obtain official payment receipt. Re-submit with updated receipts.

### **9.3 Name / Detail Mismatch Across Documents**

**Cause:** Discrepancy in the owner's name (spelling variations), property dimensions, survey number, or address between the sale deed, EC, Khata, and tax records. **Resolution:**

* For name discrepancies: Obtain an affidavit confirming the two names belong to the same individual. For major discrepancies, rectification deed at the Sub-Registrar's Office may be necessary.  
* For property detail discrepancies: File a correction petition with the BBMP ARO with supporting survey records, or obtain a surveyor's report confirming the identity of the property.

### **9.4 Unregistered Sale Deed / Instrument**

**Cause:** Submitting an unregistered agreement of sale, MOU, or will (not probated) as the transfer instrument. **Resolution:** Property registration is a legal prerequisite. Ensure the sale deed, gift deed, or other instrument is compulsorily registered at the Sub-Registrar's Office. For inheritance via will, obtain probate from a civil court if required.

### **9.5 Encumbrances on Title**

**Cause:** EC reveals a mortgage, charge, court attachment, or lien on the property. **Resolution:** Obtain a mortgage release/discharge deed from the bank or financier and register it at the Sub-Registrar's Office. Obtain a clear EC thereafter. For court attachments, the court order must be resolved before proceeding.

### **9.6 Property Under Legal Dispute / Litigation**

**Cause:** BBMP's records flag the property as subject to a pending civil suit, court injunction, or title dispute. **Resolution:** Produce a copy of the court order confirming that no injunction prohibits mutation/transfer. If litigation is ongoing, Khata Transfer will typically be stayed until the suit is disposed of. Engage a property lawyer to resolve the underlying dispute.

### **9.7 B Khata Status / Unapproved Layout**

**Cause:** The property is in B Khata (unapproved layout or deviated construction), and the applicant seeks A Khata directly. **Resolution:** Under the GBG Act 2024 scheme, pay the applicable betterment charges (₹200–₹250/sq.m.) and conversion fee (2% of stamp duty value) to convert B Khata to A Khata. Alternatively, obtain a DC Order (conversion order from the Deputy Commissioner for agricultural land) if the land conversion is pending, and then apply.

### **9.8 PID Number Not Assigned / Property Not in BBMP Records**

**Cause:** Newly constructed properties or recently added BBMP peripheral areas may not yet have a PID assigned in BBMP's database. **Resolution:** File for a fresh Khata Registration (rather than transfer) with the ARO, along with a site plan, approved building plan, and tax assessment application. Once a PID is assigned and assessment is completed, Khata Transfer can proceed.

### **9.9 No Objection Certificate Missing (For Apartments)**

**Cause:** For flats and apartments, the Residents' Welfare Association (RWA) or builder's NOC is required in some BBMP zones but not submitted. **Resolution:** Obtain NOC from the RWA/builder and include in the re-submission. Also ensure the Occupancy Certificate (OC) is obtained from BBMP for the building — BBMP may not process Khata Transfer for apartments in buildings without a valid OC.

## **10\. LEGAL CONSEQUENCES OF NOT COMPLETING KHATA TRANSFER AFTER PROPERTY PURCHASE**

Failure to complete Khata Transfer after purchase of property in Bangalore carries significant legal, financial, and practical consequences:

### **10.1 Inability to Pay Property Tax in New Owner's Name**

Property tax continues to be assessed in the previous owner's name. The new owner cannot legally pay property tax in their own name. This creates ambiguity in tax records, potential for accumulated arrears, and risk of duplicate tax demands.

### **10.2 No Access to Home Loans or Mortgage Finance**

All scheduled banks and most NBFCs require a valid Khata (in the borrower's name) as a prerequisite for home loan sanction, loan against property, or any mortgage. Without Khata Transfer, the new owner cannot leverage the property for institutional credit. This is a practical impossibility for most property owners.

### **10.3 Ineligibility for Building Plan Approvals**

BBMP will not sanction building plans, renovation approvals, or extensions on a property unless the applicant's name appears in the Khata records. New construction or even authorised modifications cannot begin without this.

### **10.4 Denial of Occupancy Certificate**

If a new apartment is under construction or recently completed, the OC (Occupancy Certificate) will not be issued if the Khata is not in the buyer's name.

### **10.5 Inability to Obtain Utility Connections**

BWSSB (water supply), BESCOM (electricity), and BBMP (sanitation) connections require a valid Khata in the applicant's name. Without Khata Transfer, the new owner cannot independently apply for utility connections.

### **10.6 Risk of Property Being Treated as Ownerless / Disputed in Municipal Records**

If the previous owner dies, becomes insolvent, or is untraceable, and the Khata has not been transferred, the new owner may face administrative and legal difficulties asserting their rights in BBMP records. The property may be flagged as a disputed asset.

### **10.7 Complications in Future Resale**

When the new owner attempts to sell the property, the buyer's lawyer and bank will scrutinise the Khata. If the Khata is still in the name of the seller's predecessor, the chain of title in municipal records is broken, making the resale extremely difficult and potentially unmarketable. Buyers and banks will insist on a clear Khata before proceeding.

### **10.8 Trade License and Commercial Permissions Unavailable**

For commercial properties, a trade license from BBMP is linked to the Khata. Without Khata Transfer, the new owner cannot obtain or renew a trade license.

### **10.9 No Legal Penalty for Delay Per Se — But Practical Bar**

The KMC Act and BBMP regulations do not prescribe a specific penalty amount (such as a fine) for delay in completing Khata Transfer. However, the practical consequences enumerated above act as a comprehensive bar to exercising full ownership rights. Furthermore, Section 114 of the KMC Act places an obligation on the new owner to notify BBMP of the change in ownership; prolonged non-compliance may attract regulatory scrutiny.

### **10.10 Risk in Legal Disputes**

In property litigation, courts and revenue authorities give significant weight to Khata records as evidence of possession and municipal recognition. A property owner who has not completed Khata Transfer is in a weaker evidentiary position compared to one whose name appears in municipal records.
