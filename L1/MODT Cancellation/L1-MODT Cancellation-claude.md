## **1\. LEGAL DEFINITION**

### **What is MODT?**

A Memorandum of Deposit of Title Deed (MODT) — also written as MoDT or referred to in sub-registrar records as DTD (Deposit of Title Deeds) — is a written, registered document executed by a borrower in favour of a lending institution (bank or NBFC) at the time of disbursement of a home loan or loan against property. It records and gives formal legal effect to the act of the borrower depositing the original title deed(s) of the mortgaged property with the lender as security for the loan.

In Indian law, this transaction creates an **equitable mortgage** by deposit of title deeds, as governed by Section 58(f) of the Transfer of Property Act, 1882\. An equitable mortgage is distinguished from a registered mortgage: it arises from the mere act of depositing title documents with an intent to create a security, without requiring a formal registered instrument. However, Karnataka, like certain other states, mandates the **registration of the MODT document** at the jurisdictional Sub-Registrar Office (SRO) under the Registration Act, 1908 in order for the equitable mortgage to be reflected in the Encumbrance Certificate (EC) of the property and to provide public notice of the lender's security interest.

The MODT document typically contains:

* Full details of the borrower(s) and co-borrowers  
* Full particulars of the lending institution (bank/NBFC name, branch, authorised signatory)  
* A complete description of the secured property (survey number, site/flat number, extent, CTS/khata details, registered document reference)  
* Loan account number, sanctioned loan amount, and date of sanction  
* A declaration by the borrower confirming voluntary deposit of enumerated original title documents  
* A statement that the deposit is intended to create a security by way of equitable mortgage  
* Signatures of both borrower and authorised bank representative, along with witnesses

### **What does MODT mean for Property Title?**

Once the MODT is registered, it appears as a **charge/encumbrance** in the Encumbrance Certificate (EC) of the property issued by the Department of Stamps and Registration, Government of Karnataka. The EC is the primary document used by lawyers, buyers, and subsequent lenders to assess whether a property has any liabilities against it. The MODT entry in the EC signals to all third parties that:

* The property's original title documents are pledged with a specific lender  
* The property cannot be freely sold, transferred, or re-mortgaged without the lender's knowledge and concurrence  
* The lender holds a prior security interest over the property

Importantly, while legal **ownership** of the property remains with the borrower throughout the loan tenure, the borrower's ability to deal freely with the property is practically constrained by the lender's security interest.

### **What does MODT Cancellation Legally Achieve?**

MODT Cancellation (also called Discharge of Mortgage, Reconveyance, or Discharge Deed registration) is the formal legal process by which the equitable mortgage created by the MODT is extinguished upon full repayment of the secured loan. Legally, it achieves the following:

1. **Removes the encumbrance from the EC:** After cancellation is registered, the Sub-Registrar makes an entry in the property's EC record reflecting the discharge, resulting in a clean EC.  
2. **Restores unencumbered title to the borrower:** The borrower regains the right to sell, mortgage, or otherwise deal with the property without any lender's charge.  
3. **Extinguishes the lender's legal claim:** The bank or NBFC's security interest over the property is formally nullified and cannot be revived on the basis of the original MODT.  
4. **Creates a public record of discharge:** Anyone searching the SRO records or obtaining an EC will find the original MODT entry and the subsequent cancellation/discharge, demonstrating clean title progression.

Without MODT cancellation, the encumbrance remains visible on the EC indefinitely, even though the loan is repaid — a serious practical problem for property sales and subsequent financing.

## **2\. WHEN MODT CANCELLATION IS MANDATORY**

MODT cancellation is legally required and practically essential in the following circumstances:

**2.1 Full Loan Repayment (Regular Closure)** When a borrower completes all EMI payments and the loan account reaches a zero outstanding balance through the normal repayment schedule, the loan is "closed" by the bank. This is the most common trigger. As soon as the bank issues the loan closure certificate/No Objection Certificate (NOC), the borrower is obligated to initiate MODT cancellation to clear the EC.

**2.2 Foreclosure / Prepayment** When a borrower repays the entire outstanding principal, accrued interest, and any applicable prepayment charges in a single lump-sum payment before the original loan tenure ends (foreclosure), the bank closes the loan account. MODT cancellation must follow. Per RBI Circular RBI/2023-24/60 dated September 13, 2023 (effective December 1, 2023), the lender is required to return all original property documents and remove charges registered with any registry within **30 days** of full repayment, including in foreclosure scenarios.

**2.3 Loan Transfer / Balance Transfer to Another Bank** When a borrower transfers an outstanding home loan from one bank to another (balance transfer), the original lending bank receives full settlement of its dues from the incoming bank. The original bank's MODT must be cancelled, and a fresh MODT is created in favour of the new lending bank. This is critical: if the original MODT is not cancelled, the incoming bank's new MODT will reflect as a second charge on the EC, creating a title complication.

**2.4 Sale of Property (Where Loan Is Outstanding)** If a borrower sells the property and uses the sale proceeds to repay the outstanding loan in full, the MODT must be cancelled as part of the sale transaction documentation. Buyers and their lawyers invariably demand a clean EC, and the MODT cancellation is a condition precedent to the sale.

**2.5 Inheritance / Succession After Borrower's Death** Where the sole borrower or all joint borrowers are deceased and the loan is repaid by legal heirs, the MODT cancellation process must be completed by the legal heirs, subject to producing succession certificate, legal heir certificate, or probate, as applicable.

## **4\. STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS — MODT CANCELLATION IN BANGALORE/KARNATAKA**

### **PHASE 1: Loan Closure and Bank-Side Actions (Responsible Party: Borrower \+ Bank)**

**Step 1: Final EMI Payment / Foreclosure Payment** *Responsible Party: Borrower* The borrower ensures the last EMI is paid or makes a foreclosure payment covering the entire outstanding principal, interest, and applicable charges. The borrower should request a statement of account from the bank confirming zero outstanding.

**Step 2: Collect Loan Closure / No Objection Certificate (NOC)** *Responsible Party: Borrower (initiates), Bank (issues)* After confirming zero balance, the borrower formally requests the loan closure certificate and NOC from the bank's home loan / mortgage department. Under RBI Circular RBI/2023-24/60 (effective December 1, 2023), the bank is mandated to issue the NOC and return original title documents within **30 days** of loan closure. The bank may charge a nominal administrative fee for issuing the NOC (see Section 6). The borrower should submit a written/email request and maintain proof of the request date.

**Step 3: Collect Original Title Documents from Bank** *Responsible Party: Borrower (collects)* Along with or following the NOC, the borrower must collect the complete set of original title documents that were deposited with the bank at the time of MODT execution. These typically include: the registered sale deed / parent documents, the original MODT document (certified copy is usually retained by SRO), the khata certificate, tax paid receipts, and any other documents as listed in the MODT schedule. The bank must provide a receipt/acknowledgment of all documents returned, and the borrower must cross-verify this against the original MODT deposit schedule.

**Step 4: Verify Original MODT Document Details** *Responsible Party: Borrower / Advocate* The borrower (preferably assisted by a property advocate) verifies the MODT document number, registration date, SRO where registered, loan account number, and party names. All these details are needed to execute and register the Discharge Deed / Cancellation Deed.

### **PHASE 2: Preparation of Discharge Deed (Responsible Party: Advocate)**

**Step 5: Engage a Property Advocate** *Responsible Party: Borrower* The borrower engages a locally experienced property advocate (typically from the Bangalore district bar / Karnataka State Bar Council). The advocate drafts the Discharge Deed (also called Reconveyance Deed or Cancellation of Mortgage). While some borrowers attempt this independently, advocate assistance is strongly recommended for accuracy.

**Step 6: Draft the Discharge Deed / Cancellation Deed** *Responsible Party: Advocate* The advocate drafts a Discharge Deed that contains:

* Names and details of the executing parties: the bank (as Mortgagee / Depositor of funds) and the borrower (as Mortgagor)  
* Reference to the original MODT: document number, registration date, book number, volume, page, SRO  
* Loan account number and confirmation that the loan has been repaid in full  
* Declaration by the bank (through its authorised signatory) that it releases all claims over the property and relinquishes the security created by the MODT  
* Schedule of the property (same description as the original MODT)  
* Request to the Sub-Registrar to make appropriate entries in the registers

**Step 7: Obtain Bank Authorisation / Signature on Discharge Deed** *Responsible Party: Borrower (coordinates) \+ Bank (signs)* This is a critical step. The Discharge Deed must be signed by an **authorised signatory of the bank** (not just any bank employee — typically a branch manager or designated legal officer holding a Power of Attorney from the bank for this purpose). The borrower must coordinate with the bank's legal/mortgage department to schedule execution. Many banks in Bangalore (SBI, HDFC, ICICI, Axis, etc.) have designated legal departments for MODT discharge. The borrower may need to provide the drafted Discharge Deed to the bank in advance for their review and internal approval. The bank representative will sign the deed in the presence of two witnesses and the borrower.

### **PHASE 3: Fee Payment and Registration (Responsible Party: Borrower \+ Advocate)**

**Step 8: Calculate Stamp Duty and Registration Fee for Discharge Deed** *Responsible Party: Advocate / Borrower* For MODT cancellation/discharge in Karnataka, the applicable charges are nominal fixed-amount stamp duty (not a percentage of loan amount). See Section 6 for exact fee details.

**Step 9: Pay Stamp Duty via Kaveri Online Services / Khajane II** *Responsible Party: Borrower / Advocate* Stamp duty for the Discharge Deed is paid online through the Kaveri Online Services portal (https://kaverionline.karnataka.gov.in) or the Khajane II portal (https://k2.karnataka.gov.in). Upon payment, a challan (e-stamp) is generated. The challan is valid for 90 days and must be used within this period. Alternatively, impressed stamp paper may be purchased from licensed vendors for smaller amounts.

**Step 10: Book Appointment at Sub-Registrar Office (SRO)** *Responsible Party: Borrower / Advocate* Online appointment booking for document registration is available through the Kaveri Online Services 2.0 portal. The borrower selects the SRO having jurisdiction over the property and books an available slot. Walk-in registration may also be available depending on the SRO's queue.

**Step 11: Appear at SRO for Registration** *Responsible Party: Borrower \+ Bank Representative \+ Advocate* On the appointment date, the following must appear at the SRO:

* The borrower (and co-borrowers, if any)  
* The authorised bank representative (with their original identity proof and authority letter/PoA from the bank)  
* The property advocate (optional but recommended)

Documents to be presented at SRO (see Section 5 for full list). The SRO staff will verify all documents, confirm identity of parties via biometric/Aadhaar authentication, scan the documents, and accept the registration. The registered Discharge Deed is returned (either immediately or within a few working days, depending on the SRO).

**Step 12: Collect Registered Discharge Deed** *Responsible Party: Borrower* After registration, the borrower receives the registered Discharge Deed stamped with the SRO seal, document number, and date. This is the primary evidence that the MODT has been cancelled.

### **PHASE 4: Post-Registration Verification (Responsible Party: Borrower / Advocate)**

**Step 13: Download Updated Encumbrance Certificate (EC)** *Responsible Party: Borrower / Advocate* After 7–15 working days from registration, the borrower should download the EC from the Kaveri Online Services portal (https://kaverionline.karnataka.gov.in) for the relevant period covering both the MODT registration and the Discharge Deed registration. The EC should now reflect both entries — the original MODT and the subsequent discharge — confirming clean title. EC can be downloaded online for a nominal fee.

**Step 14: Update Khata Records (if required)** *Responsible Party: Borrower* If the BBMP or local authority's khata records reflect the bank's lien (some local bodies maintain their own records of mortgage), the borrower may need to submit the registered Discharge Deed to the relevant BBMP ward office / panchayat to have records updated. This step is property-specific.

## **8\. LEGAL PROVISIONS**

### **8.1 Transfer of Property Act, 1882**

**Section 58(f):** Defines mortgage by deposit of title deeds (equitable mortgage). A borrower in specified cities (originally Calcutta, Bombay, Madras — extended by state notification to include Bangalore and other Karnataka cities) may create a mortgage by delivering title deeds to the creditor with intent to create a security. No registered instrument is required for the mortgage itself, though Karnataka mandates MODT registration for public notice purposes.

**Section 60:** Right of mortgagor to redeem. Upon payment of the mortgage money, the mortgagor has the right to require the mortgagee to deliver the mortgage deed and all documents relating to the mortgaged property, and to reconvey the property.

**Section 62:** Right of usufructuary mortgagor to recover possession. On redemption, the mortgagor is entitled to recover possession.

### **8.2 Registration Act, 1908**

**Section 17:** Compulsorily registrable documents. While an equitable mortgage by deposit of title deeds does not strictly require registration under Section 17 (unlike a registered mortgage), Karnataka's practice and Sub-Registrar circulars treat the MODT as a registrable document for public notice purposes. The Discharge Deed/Cancellation Deed is also presented for registration at the SRO.

**Section 23:** Documents must be presented for registration within four months of execution. The Discharge Deed must be registered within four months of its execution date.

### **8.3 Karnataka Stamp Act, 1957**

Governs stamp duty on all instruments executed in Karnataka, including MODT and the Discharge Deed. Article-specific stamp duty rates are specified in the Schedule to the Act.

### **8.4 SARFAESI Act, 2002 (Securitisation and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest Act)**

While SARFAESI is primarily relevant to the bank's enforcement rights in case of default (the bank can enforce the security without court intervention under SARFAESI), its relevance to MODT cancellation arises in two contexts:

* **Section 13(8):** If the borrower tenders full payment of the outstanding dues including costs, at any time before the date fixed for sale/auction, the bank must return the property documents. This provision is relevant in foreclosure-under-SARFAESI scenarios where the bank has issued a Section 13(2) notice.  
* **Central Registry of Securitisation Asset Reconstruction and Security Interest (CERSAI):** Under the SARFAESI Act, banks are required to register their security interest (including equitable mortgages) with CERSAI (https://www.cersai.org.in). Upon MODT cancellation, the bank must also file for **satisfaction of charge on CERSAI** to remove the lien from that registry. Failure to do so means the property will still show a charge on CERSAI, which is checked by lawyers and incoming lenders in due diligence. The borrower must specifically confirm with the bank that CERSAI charge satisfaction has been filed.

### **8.5 RBI Circular — Release of Property Documents (RBI/2023-24/60)**

**Date:** September 13, 2023 **Effective Date:** December 1, 2023 **URL:** https://www.rbi.org.in/Scripts/NotificationUser.aspx?Id=12535\&Mode=0

This is the most critical recent regulation affecting MODT cancellation practice. Key provisions:

* All Regulated Entities (commercial banks, SFBs, RRBs, Urban Co-operative Banks, State Co-operative Banks, NBFCs including HFCs, ARCs) must release all original movable and immovable property documents and remove charges registered with any registry within **30 days** of full repayment or settlement.  
* Borrowers may collect documents from the branch where the loan was serviced or any other office designated by the lender.  
* Timeline and collection location must be specified in the loan sanction letter for loans sanctioned after December 1, 2023\.  
* If delay is attributable to the lender: **₹5,000 per day compensation** until documents are returned.  
* If documents are lost/damaged: bank must assist in obtaining certified/duplicate copies (at bank's cost) and pay ₹5,000/day after 60 days (30 days for return \+ 30 days for obtaining duplicates).  
* Issued under Sections 21, 35A, 56 of the Banking Regulation Act, 1949; Sections 45JA, 45L of the RBI Act, 1934; and Section 30A of the National Housing Bank Act, 1987\.

### **8.6 RBI Fair Practices Code (Master Circular)**

The RBI's master circulars on Fair Practices Code (issued periodically to banks and NBFCs since 2003\) require lenders to release property documents upon receiving full repayment. The 2023 circular codified this into a more specific, time-bound, and penalty-backed obligation.

## **9\. COMMON PROBLEMS AND PRACTICAL ISSUES**

### **9.1 Banks Delaying NOC Issuance**

**Problem:** Despite the RBI's 30-day mandate (effective December 1, 2023), many borrowers in Bangalore experience delays of 2–6 months in receiving the NOC and original documents. Banks cite internal processing, branch to head office coordination, legal department backlogs, or verification delays.

**Remedies:**

* Submit a written request (email \+ hard copy letter) to the bank's branch manager and home loan department on the date of final payment, creating a documented record.  
* If no response within 30 days, send a formal legal notice through an advocate.  
* Lodge a complaint with the bank's Nodal Officer / Principal Nodal Officer (contact available on the bank's website).  
* File a complaint with the Banking Ombudsman under RBI's Integrated Ombudsman Scheme (https://cms.rbi.org.in) — escalation is particularly effective post-December 2023 given the ₹5,000/day penalty provision.  
* File a consumer complaint before the Consumer Disputes Redressal Forum (District Consumer Forum, Bangalore).

### **9.2 Lost Title Deed — Bank Loses Original Documents**

**Problem:** Banks occasionally misplace or lose original title documents during the loan tenure (due to branch relocations, fires, digitisation errors, or administrative lapses). This is a severe issue as the original sale deed cannot be easily replaced.

**Legal position:** Under RBI Circular RBI/2023-24/60, if original documents are lost by the bank, the bank must assist the borrower in obtaining duplicate/certified copies from the registry office and bear all associated costs. Additionally, the ₹5,000/day penalty applies after 60 days.

**Karnataka-specific remedies:**

* Obtain certified copies of the registered sale deed from the SRO where it was originally registered (available through the Kaveri Online Services portal or by visiting the SRO physically).  
* Obtain a true copy of the EC and all linked documents from the SRO.  
* For old documents where originals are not traceable in SRO records, file an FIR with the police regarding the lost document.  
* Publish a newspaper notice (in an English and Kannada daily) regarding the loss of the original document — this is standard practice recommended by advocates to protect against misuse of the lost document.  
* The MODT cancellation can still proceed on the basis of certified copies, with appropriate affidavits and indemnity bonds, subject to the SRO's acceptance.

### **9.3 MODT Not Cancelled Before Resale**

**Problem:** A seller enters into a sale agreement or sale deed execution without having obtained MODT cancellation first. The buyer's advocate will invariably identify the MODT entry in the EC during due diligence and raise a title objection.

**Consequences:** The transaction will be stalled or may collapse. Even if the loan is repaid, an uncancelled MODT on the EC is a "red flag" in due diligence. Buyers and their banks refuse to proceed without a clean EC or at minimum a registered Discharge Deed.

**Solution:** The seller must insist on completing MODT cancellation before entering into a sale agreement, or alternatively, the sale can be structured with an undertaking by the seller to complete MODT cancellation within a specific period, with a portion of the sale consideration held in escrow pending the clean EC. In practice, experienced property lawyers in Bangalore structure the transaction with sufficient time for MODT cancellation prior to registration of the sale deed.

### **9.4 Multiple MODTs on the Same Property**

**Problem:** In exceptional cases, a property may have more than one MODT on its EC. This can happen if:

* The borrower took a first loan (MODT registered), then a top-up loan (second MODT registered separately by some banks)  
* A loan was transferred between banks and the original MODT was not cancelled before the new bank registered a fresh MODT  
* Administrative errors

**Remedy:** Each MODT must be independently cancelled with a separate Discharge Deed from the respective lender. All MODT entries must appear discharged on the EC before the property can be sold or re-mortgaged with a clean title. Borrowers must obtain separate NOCs from each lender corresponding to each MODT entry.

### **9.5 Bank Representative Unavailability for SRO Appearance**

**Problem:** Many banks require their authorised branch manager or legal officer to physically appear at the SRO to execute and register the Discharge Deed. Scheduling this can be difficult, particularly if the bank has centralised its legal operations in a different city or has an overburdened legal team.

**Solution:** Insist that the bank's legal/mortgage department provides a specifically authorised representative with a valid Power of Attorney from the bank entity. Many major banks in Bangalore now pre-execute standard form Discharge Deeds at their legal departments and have the authorised representative sign in advance, facilitating SRO registration without requiring the branch manager's personal attendance. Borrowers should confirm this process with their specific bank's home loan department.

### **9.6 Old MODT Records Not Digitised in Kaveri System**

**Problem:** For home loans taken before approximately 2000–2005, the original MODT may not appear in the Kaveri Online Services EC database. The EC downloaded online may show "no transactions" for the period when the MODT was registered, making it difficult for the borrower to obtain the exact document reference number.

**Solution:** Visit the SRO physically and request an EC search for the relevant period from the manually maintained registers. The SRO staff can provide a handwritten EC extract or direct access to ledger books for older records.

### **9.7 CERSAI Charge Not Removed by Bank**

**Problem:** Even after MODT cancellation at the SRO, the bank may not file satisfaction of charge on CERSAI. An incoming buyer's lender will check CERSAI and find the charge still active.

**Solution:** Specifically request the bank (in writing) to file CERSAI charge satisfaction within the 30-day RBI-mandated period. Obtain a written confirmation from the bank (with a CERSAI acknowledgment/reference number) that the charge has been removed. Check CERSAI independently at https://www.cersai.org.in for your property.

## **10\. CONSEQUENCES OF NOT CANCELLING MODT**

### **10.1 Impact on Property Resale**

An uncancelled MODT is an encumbrance that appears on the property's Encumbrance Certificate (EC). No prudent buyer will purchase a property with an active encumbrance unless they are certain the underlying obligation has been discharged. In practice, even if the loan is fully repaid, an uncancelled MODT will:

* Prevent or significantly delay the registration of the sale deed (most SROs in Bangalore will proceed with registration of the sale deed even with an encumbrance on record, since they do not verify EC before registering a sale deed — but the buyer's bank certainly will)  
* Cause the buyer's home loan bank to reject the loan application for the property (see 10.2)  
* Result in the sale transaction collapsing or requiring an expensive escrow arrangement  
* Create legal disputes if the buyer later discovers the undisclosed encumbrance and claims the seller fraudulently suppressed it (the seller has a duty to disclose all encumbrances under Section 55 of the Transfer of Property Act)

### **10.2 Impact on New Loan Application (for Buyer or Seller)**

If the owner of the property (after full loan repayment) wishes to take a fresh home loan against the same property, or if the property is to be offered as collateral for any loan, the incoming bank will conduct a title search and obtain the EC. An active MODT entry on the EC will:

* Cause the bank to reject the loan application outright on the basis of existing encumbrance  
* Or require the applicant to first obtain MODT cancellation and submit the clean EC before the loan can be sanctioned  
* Prevent the execution of a fresh MODT in favour of the new lender (since the property is already encumbered in records, creating an apparent "second charge" situation)

### **10.3 Impact on Title Clarity and Marketability**

Uncancelled MODTs accumulate on the EC over time, particularly where a property has changed hands or had multiple loans over the years. Each unresolved MODT reduces the property's "title clarity" and marketability. Properties with multiple uncancelled MODT entries require detailed reconciliation — obtaining NOCs and Discharge Deeds from possibly defunct or merged banks, which may be extremely difficult. Banks that have been merged (e.g., Vijaya Bank, Dena Bank — now merged into Bank of Baroda; Syndicate Bank — now merged into Canara Bank) present particular challenges in obtaining authorised signatories for old Discharge Deeds.

### **10.4 Impact on Buyer Due Diligence**

A property advocate conducting due diligence for a buyer will categorically flag an uncancelled MODT as a "title defect." Buyers financing their purchase through a home loan (the vast majority of Bangalore property buyers) cannot proceed without their bank's approval, and the bank's legal department will refuse to accept a property with an active encumbrance. Even cash buyers are advised against purchasing a property with an active MODT without concurrent execution of the Discharge Deed.

### **10.5 Risk of Double Sale / Fraud**

An uncancelled MODT combined with the original title documents not yet returned by the bank creates a risk scenario: if the bank (hypothetically in a rogue act) or if the MODT documents were misused, a fraudulent claim or mortgage could theoretically be created against the property. While such fraud is rare, the legal protection of a registered Discharge Deed is complete. The registered Discharge Deed, once in the SRO records, serves as an absolute bar against any subsequent claim by the lender.

### **10.6 Impact on Inheritance and Succession**

Legal heirs who inherit a property with an uncancelled MODT (where the loan was in fact repaid) must undergo the MODT cancellation process themselves, often years after the original borrower's death. This requires establishing legal heirship, locating the original loan records, and convincing the bank's records department to issue a retrospective NOC — all of which is considerably more difficult than timely cancellation during the borrower's lifetime.
