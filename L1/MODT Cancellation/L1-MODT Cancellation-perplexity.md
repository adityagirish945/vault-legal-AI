1. **Legal definition and effect of MODT and its cancellation**

    1.1. **Mortgage by deposit of title deeds (equitable mortgage)**

   * Under section 58(f) of the Transfer of Property Act 1882, a “mortgage by deposit of title deeds” is created when the borrower delivers title documents of immovable property to a creditor with the intention of creating security for a loan; no formal mortgage deed is required for the validity of the mortgage itself.bajajfinserv+1

   * Courts repeatedly treat the mere deposit of title deeds with the requisite intention as creating a valid equitable mortgage, even though a separate memorandum may or may not be registered.prepp+1

2. 1.2. **What the MODT document is in Karnataka practice**

   * In Karnataka, lenders commonly record this equitable mortgage through a written “Memorandum of Deposit of Title Deeds” (MODT) which is registered at the jurisdictional Sub‑Registrar Office (SRO) via the Kaveri system.nobroker+1

   * The MODT recites that the borrower has deposited specific title documents, identifies the property and loan, and is registered as a mortgage/charge entry so that the lender’s interest appears in the Encumbrance Certificate (EC) and other registration records.web.landeed+2

3. 1.3. **Why banks insist on MODT when issuing home loans**

   * For home and LAP (loan against property) in Karnataka, banks and housing finance companies routinely insist on MODT registration because it makes the equitable mortgage traceable in public records and enforceable like other registered mortgages.vaultproptech+2

   * Karnataka practice treats MODT registration as compulsory for most institutional home loans so that the bank’s charge is visible in EC searches and can be enforced under the SARFAESI Act as a “security interest”.cleartax+2

4. 1.4. **Effect of MODT on property title**

   * The borrower remains the owner; the MODT does not transfer ownership but creates a charge/limited interest in favour of the lender over the property, which can be enforced on default.bajajfinserv+2

   * In the Encumbrance Certificate for a property where MODT is registered, the bank typically appears in the “purchaser/claimant” column (column 6\) for the mortgage transaction, showing that the property is encumbered by a registered charge.pgnpropertymanagement+1

5. 1.5. **What MODT cancellation legally achieves**

   * After full repayment or other discharge of the secured debt, the mortgage must be redeemed; in Karnataka this is done through registration of a Discharge / Reconveyance Deed (also described as MODT cancellation), which explicitly releases the lender’s charge.vaultproptech+2

   * Once the discharge/reconveyance is registered at the SRO, subsequent ECs show the mortgage entry as discharged and the bank’s name is removed from column 6, restoring an unencumbered title in the public records.cleartax+2

   * Legally, MODT cancellation/extinction of the mortgage means the lender no longer has a “security interest” under SARFAESI or any right to sell or otherwise proceed against the property for that loan.ibclaw+1

6. **When MODT cancellation is mandatory (repayment, foreclosure, loan transfer)**

    2.1. **Full repayment / scheduled closure of home loan**

   * On full settlement of a home loan backed by MODT, Karnataka practice treats discharge deed registration as mandatory; simply closing the loan in the bank’s system or holding a closure letter is not enough to clear the mortgage from government records.vaultproptech+1

   * Specialist Bangalore/Karnataka guides emphasise that until the discharge or reconveyance deed is registered in the same SRO where the MODT was registered, the EC will continue to show an active mortgage, and the property will not be regarded as loan‑free for due‑diligence purposes.pgnpropertymanagement+2

7. 2.2. **Prepayment / foreclosure initiated by the borrower**

   * Whether the loan is repaid as per schedule or foreclosed early, once the entire outstanding is cleared the bank is obliged to release original documents and remove all registered charges within 30 days under the RBI’s 2023 directions.taxmann+2

   * For MODT‑backed loans in Karnataka, this obligation is practically implemented by issuing closure documents (loan closure letter, NOC/discharge deed, exemption letter) and enabling registration of the discharge deed to cancel the MODT entry.gpparsik.bank+2

8. 2.3. **Balance transfer / loan transfer to another bank**

   * In balance transfer cases, the new bank typically repays the old bank in full; as soon as the old loan is fully settled, the old bank must release property documents and remove the registered charge/MODT entry within 30 days, even though a new mortgage is created in favour of the new lender.probe42+2

   * Operationally, either (a) the outgoing bank executes a discharge/reconveyance deed and the incoming bank simultaneously registers a fresh MODT, or (b) MODT is directly substituted in favour of the new lender, but in all cases the earlier MODT in favour of the old bank must be extinguished through appropriate registration to avoid dual charges.nobroker+2

9. 2.4. **After enforcement sale / foreclosure by the bank**

   * Where the bank enforces its security under SARFAESI and sells the property, the mortgage is extinguished by the registered sale certificate/transfer in favour of the auction purchaser; the MODT entry effectively terminates when the secured asset is transferred free from the bank’s prior charge.taxmann+1

   * The SRO records the SARFAESI sale as a subsequent registered transaction, and updated ECs will show the bank’s mortgage entries followed by the sale/auction entry, with the purchaser holding title free of the earlier charge once sale is completed in accordance with the Act and rules.cleartax+1

10. **Governing body, Sub‑Registrar offices, and portals**

     3.1. **Regulatory and administrative authority (IGR Karnataka)**

    * The Inspector General of Registration and Commissioner of Stamps, Karnataka (IGR Karnataka), under the Department of Stamps and Registration, is the apex authority for document registration and stamp duty across the state.sobha+1

    * IGR oversees all Sub‑Registrar Offices (SROs), collection of stamp duty and registration fees, maintenance of property records, and digital initiatives such as the Kaveri Online Services portal used for MODT and discharge registrations.igr.karnataka+2

11. 3.2. **Sub‑Registrar Offices (SROs)**

    * Each property in Bangalore/Karnataka falls under the jurisdiction of a specific SRO; both the original MODT and the subsequent discharge/reconveyance deed must be registered at the same SRO.vaultproptech+2

    * SROs are responsible for verifying documents, collecting biometric data, ensuring proper stamp duty/fees are paid, registering the discharge documents, and ensuring encumbrance entries correctly reflect creation and cancellation of mortgages.sobha+1

13. **Step‑by‑step process: from loan closure to MODT cancellation registration**

     (The sequence below reflects typical practice in Bangalore/Karnataka; specific bank procedures may add internal steps.)

     4.1. **Stage 1 – Loan closure (Bank‑led, Borrower‑initiated)**

    * The borrower requests a foreclosure/closure statement and pays all outstanding principal, interest, charges, and any prepayment penalty as per the loan agreement.basichomeloan+1

    * After full repayment, the bank marks the loan closed in its system and triggers internal approval for release of security and documents, which under RBI’s directions must culminate in release and charge removal within 30 days.zerodha+2

14. 4.2. **Stage 2 – Bank issues closure and discharge documents (Bank responsibility)**

    * Within its internal turnaround time (often 15–30 working days in practice), the bank’s central processing unit (e.g., RACPC for PSU banks) prepares and issues: loan closure certificate, NOC/No‑Dues letter, Discharge/Reconveyance Deed draft, and an exemption letter under section 88 of the Registration Act (for scheduled banks) authorising registration without personal appearance of a bank officer.basichomeloan+2

    * Banks must also release all original title documents (sale deed, prior link documents, MODT copy, EC, khata, tax receipts, etc.) to the borrower, and RBI has mandated that they do so and remove charges from registries within 30 days of full repayment.taxmann+3

15. 4.3. **Stage 3 – Borrower collects documents and verifies bundle (Borrower responsibility)**

    * The borrower (or authorised attorney) visits the branch/RACPC to collect the original sale deed and other title documents along with the discharge/reconveyance deed and exemption letter, checking that property description, loan details, and names match the registered MODT and original sale deed.web.landeed+1

    * The borrower should carefully count and list all original documents returned and obtain a receipt copy from the bank, keeping this together with the loan closure letter and NOC; this bundle will be required at the SRO and in any future due‑diligence.bajajfinserv+2

16. 4.4. **Stage 4 – Online data entry and fee payment on Kaveri (Borrower / advocate responsibility)**

    * Before visiting the SRO, the discharge/reconveyance deed must be initiated on Kaveri Online Services: the user registers/logs in, chooses “Document Registration”, selects **Nature of Document: Reconveyance of mortgaged property**, and fills property/party details referencing the earlier MODT registration number.pgnpropertymanagement+2

    * The portal calculates stamp duty and registration fee for the reconveyance/discharge deed, and the borrower pays these (plus scanning fees) online through e‑payment/Khajane‑II and generates the payment challan and application summary.pgnpropertymanagement+2

17. 4.5. **Stage 5 – Appointment booking with jurisdictional SRO (Borrower / advocate responsibility)**

    * Using Kaveri Online, the user books a registration slot with the jurisdictional SRO (the same office where the MODT was originally registered), selecting date and time from available slots; an appointment ID is generated.sobha+1

    * Many practitioners in Bangalore engage an advocate or registration service to handle data entry and slot booking, but legally the borrower can complete these steps directly as a registered Kaveri user.brigadegroup+1

18. 4.6. **Stage 6 – SRO visit and registration of discharge/reconveyance (Borrower \+ witnesses; bank via exemption letter)**

    * On the appointment date, the borrower attends the SRO with two witnesses and carries: Kaveri application summary, payment receipts, original discharge/reconveyance deed, exemption letter (if bank official is not attending), original title documents, Aadhaar/PAN for all signatories, and witness IDs.pgnpropertymanagement+2

    * The SRO verifies documents, captures biometrics (photo and thumb impression) of the borrower and witnesses, verifies OTP, and registers the discharge/reconveyance deed, returning the original title documents and providing a registered copy/acknowledgement; the bank’s physical presence is generally not required when a valid section‑88 exemption letter is produced.igr.karnataka+2

19. 4.7. **Stage 7 – Post‑registration: EC and records update (Borrower responsibility)**

    * After registration, the Kaveri system automatically updates the property’s encumbrance data, but in practice property owners are advised to apply for a fresh EC (Form 15/16) covering the relevant period to confirm that the mortgage entry is marked discharged and the bank’s name no longer shows as claimant.cleartax+2

    * The owner should safely file the registered discharge/reconveyance deed with the original sale deed and maintain soft copies (downloaded from Kaveri) for future due‑diligence and loan/resale processes.bajajfinserv+1

20. **Complete document checklist (with NOC format expectations)**

     5.1. **From the bank / lender**

    * **Loan Closure Certificate / No Dues Letter** – on bank letterhead, stating loan account number, borrower name(s), property address, date of full settlement, and that no amounts are outstanding; most lender guides treat this as essential proof of closure.vaultproptech+2

    * **NOC / “No Objection” statement** – often merged with the closure letter, confirming the bank has no objection to release of title documents and has no subsisting charge over the property after full repayment; typical contents include loan details, property description, and confirmation that the mortgage has been fully satisfied.vaultproptech+2

    * **Discharge Deed / Reconveyance Deed (Bank‑drafted)** – formal document by which the bank (mortgagee) acknowledges receipt of full mortgage money and releases/reconveys the mortgaged property to the borrower; Karnataka‑specific guides emphasise that this deed *must* be registered to effectively discharge the MODT.vaultproptech+2

    * **Exemption Letter under section 88 of Registration Act (for scheduled banks)** – states that the bank is a “public officer” or “corporation” entitled to exemption from personal appearance, authorises execution/registration of the discharge deed through specified signatories, and is accepted by Karnataka SROs as sufficient in lieu of physical presence of bank’s officials.igr.karnataka+1

21. 5.2. **Property title and prior registration documents**

    * **Original registered sale deed and prior link documents** deposited at the time of MODT creation; these are returned by the bank and should be carried to the SRO to prove title and match property details in the discharge deed.web.landeed+2

    * **Copy of registered MODT deed / earlier mortgage registration details** – Kaveri application requires entry of document number/year and SRO of the existing MODT; practitioners often attach a copy of the registered MODT or at least its registration details to the discharge file.nobroker+2

    * **Latest Encumbrance Certificate (EC)** – while not always mandatory, many advocates obtain a current EC (showing the existing MODT) so that post‑registration EC can be compared to confirm removal of the mortgage entry.bajajfinserv+2

    * **Khata/e‑Khata and latest tax‑paid receipts** – commonly asked to corroborate property identity and ownership in Bangalore SROs, though technically discharge deed focuses on the mortgage entry; some local guidance in Karnataka treats e‑Khata as effectively mandatory for discharge registration.pgnpropertymanagement+1

22. 5.3. **Kaveri‑related documents**

    * **Kaveri Online Services application summary** for the discharge/reconveyance article, generated after online data entry.housing+1

    * **E‑payment receipts / Khajane‑II challan** for stamp duty, registration fee, and scanning fee for the discharge deed.pgnpropertymanagement+2

23. 5.4. **Identification and execution documents**

    * **Borrower’s ID and address proof** – Aadhaar is standard in Karnataka SROs; PAN may also be required to align with earlier registrations and loan records.vaultproptech+2

    * **Two witnesses’ ID proofs (typically Aadhaar)** – required for registration of the discharge deed; witnesses’ biometrics and signatures are taken at the SRO.pgnpropertymanagement+1

    * **Power of Attorney (if applicable)** – if the borrower is abroad or unable to attend, a properly executed and, where relevant, adjudicated power of attorney authorising an agent to sign the discharge deed and attend registration is used in practice.pgnpropertymanagement+1

24. 5.5. **Indicative contents/format points for NOC and discharge deed**

    * Karnataka practice (reflected in sample discharge deeds) requires precise recitals: original loan amount, date, MODT registration number, description of property, statement that mortgage money is fully paid, and declaration that the mortgage is redeemed and bank’s rights extinguished.web.landeed+2

    * The NOC/closure letter usually mirrors these details but is *not* itself a registrable instrument extinguishing rights; it is evidentiary and must be supported by the registered discharge/reconveyance deed for complete title clarity.prepp+2

34. **Key legal provisions: Registration Act, SARFAESI, RBI guidelines**

     8.1. **Transfer of Property Act, 1882 (TPA)**

    * Section 58(f) TPA defines “mortgage by deposit of title‑deeds” (equitable mortgage) as delivery of title documents in specified towns/areas with intent to create security; this is the legal foundation for MODT‑based mortgages used by banks in Bangalore.bajajfinserv+1

    * Section 60 TPA (right of redemption), read with case‑law, underpins the borrower’s right to redeem the mortgage upon payment of mortgage money and to demand return of documents and execution/registration of an instrument acknowledging extinguishment of the mortgage (i.e., the discharge/reconveyance deed).elplaw+1

35. 8.2. **Registration Act, 1908**

    * Section 17 of the Registration Act makes compulsory the registration of non‑testamentary instruments which create, declare, assign, limit, or extinguish any right, title or interest in immovable property of value ₹100 or more; a registered discharge/reconveyance deed extinguishing a mortgage thus must be registered to effectively remove the bank’s interest from title records.blog.ipleaders+1

    * Section 88 deals with exemption from appearance for certain public officers and is used by scheduled/commercial banks to issue exemption letters permitting registration of discharge deeds without physical appearance of bank officials at the SRO in Karnataka.igr.karnataka+1

36. 8.3. **Karnataka stamp and registration framework**

    * Karnataka applies state‑specific stamp duty rates under its adaptation of the Indian Stamp Act and schedules for MODT creation and reconveyance; MODT charges around 0.5% (stamp) \+ 0.1% (registration) of loan amount and nominal flat amounts for reconveyance are reported in current practice.vaultproptech+2

    * The Department of Stamps and Registration provides an online calculator on Kaveri for computing stamp duty and registration fees for different document types, including “Mortgage deed (MODT)” and “Reconveyance of mortgaged property”.housing+1

37. 8.4. **SARFAESI Act, 2002 – relevance to MODT**

    * Under section 2(1)(zb) SARFAESI, “security agreement” includes “the creation of mortgage by deposit of title deed with the secured creditor”, explicitly recognising MODT‑style equitable mortgages as security agreements under the Act.ibclaw+1

    * Section 2(1)(zf) defines “security interest” to include “any mortgage, charge, hypothecation, assignment or any right, title or interest of any kind” over property created in favour of a secured creditor, covering mortgages created through MODT in Karnataka.vinodkothari+2

    * SARFAESI thus allows enforcement of MODT‑backed home loans without court intervention (subject to thresholds and exclusions), making correct registration and later discharge of MODT critical to both banks and borrowers.luzenta+2

38. 8.5. **RBI guidelines on release of title deeds after loan closure**

    * RBI’s 2023 directions on “Responsible Lending Conduct – Release of Movable / Immovable Property Documents on Repayment / Settlement of Personal Loans” require regulated entities to: (i) release original property documents and remove charges within 30 days of full repayment, (ii) specify timeline and place of document return in sanction letters, and (iii) put in place procedures for returning documents to legal heirs.taxmann+2

    * Where delay beyond 30 days is attributable to the lender, compensation of ₹5,000 per day must be paid, and where documents are lost or damaged the lender must obtain duplicate or certified copies at its own cost in addition to compensation.taxmann+2

    * These directions apply nationwide, including to banks and HFCs operating in Karnataka, and support borrowers pressing for timely MODT cancellation and return of documents in Bangalore.zerodha+2

39. **Common problems in practice and how they arise**

     9.1. **Banks delaying NOC/discharge or returning documents**

    * Despite RBI’s framework, borrowers frequently report delays of weeks or months in getting NOCs, discharge deeds, and original documents even after loan closure, as illustrated in various banking and consumer commentaries.gpparsik.bank+1

    * Causes cited include centralised custody of title deeds (RACPC), internal audit/clearance delays, pending small charges or procedural issues, or banks waiting for borrowers to initiate requests rather than proactively releasing documents within 30 days.probe42+2

40. 9.2. **Lost title deeds and impact on MODT cancellation**

    * In some cases, either the bank or the borrower misplaces original sale deeds or other title documents; Karnataka‑focused practitioner guides set out steps such as obtaining certified copies from Kaveri, drafting an affidavit, filing a police complaint, and publishing newspaper notices before relying on certified copies in transactions.pgnpropertymanagement+2

    * RBI directions now require lenders who lose or damage documents to assist borrowers in obtaining duplicate/certified copies and to bear associated costs, which is directly relevant when MODT cancellation is stalled due to missing originals.taxmann+2

41. 9.3. **MODT not cancelled before resale**

    * Many property owners in Bangalore assume that loan closure and return of documents automatically clear records; however, without registration of a discharge/reconveyance deed, EC continues to show the mortgage, leading to issues when selling.vaultproptech+2

    * Buyers and their banks typically insist on a clean EC (Form 16/“Nil EC”) or at least an EC where any mortgage appears as “discharged”, so absence of a registered discharge deed can derail or significantly delay a resale transaction.bajajfinserv+2

42. 9.4. **Multiple MODTs on the same property (top‑ups / enhancements)**

    * It is common for borrowers to take additional loan amounts (top‑ups) secured by the same property, resulting in multiple MODTs registered for different amounts and dates at the same SRO.vaultproptech+1

    * Unless each of these mortgage entries is separately discharged (or a comprehensive discharge covers all loans), EC will show multiple encumbrances, and leaving one active can cause confusion over whether the property is fully debt‑free.pgnpropertymanagement+2

43. 9.5. **Loan transfer delays due to incomplete MODT discharge**

    * In balance transfer situations, the old bank may delay issuing NOCs and discharge deeds, which in turn prevents the new bank from perfecting its security and may lead to temporary uncertainty over which institution has a registered charge.nobroker+1

    * RBI’s timeline and compensation framework can be invoked by borrowers in such situations to pressure the outgoing bank to release documents and execute appropriate discharge documentation in time for re‑mortgage registration in Karnataka.taxmann+2

44. 9.6. **Procedural mistakes in Kaveri applications / SRO filings**

    * Practitioners report errors such as selecting wrong document article (e.g., generic “release deed” instead of “reconveyance of mortgaged property”), incorrect SRO selection, or mismatched property details, which force re‑filing and can delay MODT cancellation.brigadegroup+1

    * Inadequate witness presence, missing IDs, or lack of e‑Khata/tax receipts at the SRO can also result in adjournments and additional visits in Bangalore.vaultproptech+1

45. 9.7. **Bank‑initiated enforcement overshadowing borrower’s right to redeem**

    * If defaults persist and the bank initiates SARFAESI actions, the borrower’s focus shifts to redemption and settlement rather than simple MODT cancellation; until dues are cleared or property is sold, the mortgage remains and EC will show enforcement entries in addition to the original MODT.cleartax+1

    * Case‑law emphasising the borrower’s right of redemption up to certain stages of SARFAESI sale underscores that proper discharge and registration remain central even in contested enforcement scenarios.nishithdesai+1

    * **Consequences of not cancelling MODT in Karnataka**

46. 10.1. **Impact on property resale in Bangalore**

    * EC in Karnataka is the primary due‑diligence document for buyers; if it shows an active mortgage entry without a corresponding discharge/reconveyance, the property is treated as encumbered and many buyers will refuse to proceed.bajajfinserv+2

    * Even where the seller can show bank closure letters, prudent buyers and their advocates typically insist that the discharge deed be registered and EC updated before or contemporaneously with the sale, to avoid risk of hidden liabilities.vaultproptech+2

47. 10.2. **Effect on new loan applications (refinance or LAP)**

    * When applying for a fresh home loan or loan against property, lenders in Karnataka routinely obtain EC; an existing uncancelled MODT in favour of another bank will either result in rejection or require the earlier mortgage to be discharged before a new MODT can be registered.nobroker+2

    * MODT cancellation is therefore practically a precondition to using the property again as collateral, unless the earlier mortgage is being refinanced in a controlled balance‑transfer transaction where banks coordinate discharge and new MODT registration.cleartax+2

48. 10.3. **Title clarity and marketability of the property**

    * From a title‑verification standpoint, a property with uncancelled MODT reflects incomplete exercise of the right of redemption and leaves ambiguity over whether the lender’s rights are truly extinguished, undermining “clear and marketable title”.elplaw+2

    * This can show up in professional title reports and legal opinions for high‑value Bangalore transactions, prompting recommendations that discharge/reconveyance be registered and EC re‑run before any major sale or development.mypatta+2

49. 10.4. **Buyer and lender due‑diligence red flags**

    * Buyers, banks, and institutional investors treat unresolved MODT entries on EC as red flags signaling either unpaid dues, incomplete documentation, or procedural non‑compliance, and may demand price discounts, escrow arrangements, or additional warranties if forced to proceed.cleartax+2

    * For apartments in large projects, one unsatisfied MODT on an individual flat can also complicate association/NOC issues and hinder collective refinancing or redevelopment transactions.mypatta+1

50. 10.5. **Risk of future disputes and enforcement**

    * If a bank has not formally discharged the mortgage in registries, there remains at least a theoretical risk that enforcement or mistaken action may be attempted later, requiring the owner to prove closure and seek rectification.luzenta+1

    * RBI’s compensation regime mitigates some of this risk by incentivising lenders to remove charges promptly, but as long as MODT cancellation is not completed in the SRO and reflected in EC, the owner bears avoidable uncertainty.probe42+2

51. 10.6. **Regulatory non‑compliance risk for lenders**

    * For banks and HFCs operating in Karnataka, failure to ensure timely MODT cancellation and charge removal, as required by RBI’s release‑of‑documents directions, can expose them to customer complaints, ombudsman proceedings, and potential supervisory scrutiny.gpparsik.bank+2

    * Institutions therefore have both legal and regulatory incentives to coordinate with borrowers and their representatives to ensure that discharge/reconveyance deeds are executed and registered without undue delay once loans are closed.zerodha+2
