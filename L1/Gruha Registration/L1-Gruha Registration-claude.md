CONTENT:  
**1\. Legal Definition**

**Scheme Name:** Gruha Jyothi Scheme (also spelled "Gruha Jyoti Yojana"; Kannada: ಗೃಹ ಜ್ಯೋತಿ ಯೋಜನೆ)  
**Nature:** One of the five "Guarantee Schemes" (Pancha Guarantee) promised by the Indian National Congress in its 2023 Karnataka Legislative Assembly election manifesto. Upon winning the May 2023 elections, the Congress government under Chief Minister Siddaramaiah implemented the scheme.  
**Formal Announcement:** Chief Minister Siddaramaiah formally announced the scheme on 2 June 2023\. The state government issued guidelines on 5 June 2023, specifying the conditions and eligibility criteria for availing benefits.  
**Registration Opening Date:** Registration of the Gruha Jyothi Scheme started from 18 June 2023\.  
**Commencement of Benefit:** The scheme came into effect from July 2023 and was reflected in the August bill. Subsidised electricity bills were generated to eligible households from 1 August 2023 (for July 2023 consumption).  
**Core Benefit:** The scheme provides free electricity up to 200 units per month to all eligible residential households in Karnataka. Once approved, electricity bills with less than 200 units consumption are marked as 'ZERO Bills' and the consumer does not have to pay any bill.  
**Scheme Consolidation:** Existing schemes — Bhagya Jyothi, Kuteera Jyothi, and Amrutha Jyothi — were subsumed under the Gruha Jyothi scheme.  
**Government Order / Notification:** No single universally published GO number has been made available in public-facing documentation. The scheme was operationalised through guidelines issued by the Energy Department, Government of Karnataka, on 5 June 2023, and subsequently through ESCOM-level circulars. The scheme is administered under the Karnataka Electricity (Supply) Act framework and KERC (Karnataka Electricity Regulatory Commission) tariff orders. Practitioners requiring the exact GO number should submit an RTI request to the Karnataka Energy Department or access the official Seva Sindhu scheme notification at sevasindhugs.karnataka.gov.in.  
**Revised Norms (October 2025):** The Karnataka Cabinet approved revised eligibility norms: consumers using under 90 units now get 10 extra free units, replacing the earlier 10% increment rule. Electricity distribution companies (ESCOMs) have simplified average consumption calculations for transparency.  
**2\. Eligibility Criteria**

**Who Qualifies:**  
The person must be a resident of Karnataka state. The person must have an Aadhaar Card issued by the Government of India. The average monthly electricity consumption must be equal to or less than 200 units in the previous 12 months to be eligible. Only residential or domestic household electricity meter connections are eligible to receive benefits.  
**One-Connection Rule:** The scheme is extended only to one meter per person. If an individual has multiple RR numbers (consumer accounts) linked to their name, they can avail the benefit for only one meter.  
**Tenants:** To ensure that even tenants can benefit, they can register the RR number of the house they are residing in by providing their Aadhaar number and a copy of the rental agreement on the Seva Sindhu portal.  
**APL/BPL Distinction:** Under the Gruha Jyothi scheme, all citizens are equally eligible to get free energy up to 200 units, irrespective of caste, religion, family income, or kind of ration card. There is no income ceiling.  
**Out-of-State Aadhaar:** Applicants can apply for the Gruha Jyothi scheme if they reside in Karnataka but the address on their Aadhaar is in a different state, as long as they have a valid address proof for the residence they are applying for.  
**Apartment Residents:** Applicants can apply for the scheme if a separate meter has been installed.  
**New House / No Prior Consumption History:** When a consumer moves into a newly built house or shifts to a new place, the government allocates 50 units of free electricity per month, plus an additional 10%, bringing the total to approximately 55 free units per month. After completing one year in the new house, the government calculates the consumer's actual average electricity consumption for the past 12 months.  
**Who Is Explicitly Excluded:**

* Those with non-domestic and industrial connections do not qualify; the scheme is applicable only for domestic connections.  
* Consumers whose average monthly consumption for FY 2022–23 exceeded 200 units are not eligible for zero billing.  
* Consumers who use more than 200 units of electricity on average during FY 2022–23 will not be eligible for free electricity.  
* Consumers with commercial connections registered under their name cannot apply on the basis of that connection.  
* If monthly electricity consumption exceeds 200 units, the consumer is not eligible for the zero bill that month and must pay the entire bill amount.

**Pending Bill Condition:** All balances on electricity bills till 30 June 2023 had to be cleared within three months. Failing to do so would lead to disconnection of electricity. Ongoing: applicants with pending dues should clear arrears before or shortly after applying.  
**Entitlement Units Calculation (Original Formula):** Entitlement Units \= Monthly average units consumed from April 2022 to March 2023 \+ 10% increase. For example, if average units consumed is 52, 10% increase is 5.2, total entitlement units \= 57.2, rounded to 57 units.  
**Revised Formula (Post-October 2025):** The Karnataka Cabinet revised the formula: families consuming less than 50 units of electricity per month now receive an additional flat 10 units of free power, replacing the earlier percentage-based increment.  
**Three-Tier Billing Logic:**

1. Monthly usage ≤ Entitlement Units → ZERO Bill.  
2. Monthly usage \> Entitlement Units but \< 200 units → Pay only for units exceeding entitlement.  
3. Monthly usage \> 200 units → Pay the entire bill for that month.

**4\. Step-by-Step Registration Process**

**Online Process**

Step 1: Visit https://sevasindhugs.karnataka.gov.in/  
Step 2: Click on the yellow bulb icon.  
Step 3: Click on the link to register for the Gruha Jyoti Yojana.  
Step 4: Choose your preferred language (Kannada or English). Enter the details as required in the form. After entering your mobile number, you will receive an OTP via SMS. Enter the OTP to verify your number. Accept the declaration and complete the captcha code. Click on the submit button to finish the registration process.  
Step 5: Review the application and click on the Submit button. You will receive an acknowledgment via SMS/email.  
**Fields required during online registration:**

1. Account ID/Connection ID; 2\. Account holder name as in ESCOM; 3\. Account holder address as per ESCOM; 4\. Type of occupancy (Owner / Tenant / Family Member); 5\. Aadhaar Number; 6\. Applicant Name as in Aadhaar; 7\. Mobile number for communication.

**Acknowledgement:** After successfully submitting the application, you will receive an acknowledgement receipt through your registered mobile number (SMS) or email. If you didn't receive it or lost it, you can re-download the Gruha Jyoti acknowledgement slip by visiting the Gruha Jyoti Scheme Acknowledgement Reprint Portal, choosing your ESCOM, and entering your Account ID.  
**Status Check:** Step 1: Visit https://sevasindhugs.karnataka.gov.in/. Step 2: Click on the button to 'Know the status of your application'. Step 3: Select your ESCOM name from the drop-down menu and enter your Account ID. Click on the Check Status button.  
**Offline Process**

Applicants can apply for the Karnataka Gruha Jyothi Scheme offline by visiting Bangalore One, Karnataka One, or Grama One centres.  
Procedure: Visit the nearest service centre, request the Gruha Jyothi application form, fill in all personal and electricity connection details, attach the required documents, and submit the form at the same centre. A service charge may apply at offline centres depending on the centre's rules.  
**De-linking (Change of Residence)**

Step 1: Visit https://sevasindhugs.karnataka.gov.in/. Step 2: Click on the yellow bulb icon. Step 3: Click on the second option: 'Click here to de-link from the Gruha Jyoti Yojana'. Step 4: Enter your Aadhaar number and click on 'Get Details'. On the next page, enter your Aadhaar number, tick the consent, select OTP, and click 'Generate OTP'. Enter the OTP and click 'Submit'. Check the details, select the reason for de-link, tick the consent, and click 'Submit'. The acknowledgement will be displayed on the screen.  
After de-linking, the consumer must submit a fresh application for the new address.  
**5\. Complete Document Checklist**

The following documents are required at the time of application:

1. **Electricity Bill** — Recent bill showing the Account ID / Connection ID / RR Number (mandatory for identifying the ESCOM account).  
2. **Aadhaar Card** — Of the applicant (mandatory; Aadhaar number must be linked).  
3. **Mobile Number** — Registered and active (for OTP verification and SMS acknowledgement).  
4. **Email ID** — Optional but recommended for acknowledgement.  
5. **Rental / Tenancy Agreement** — Required if the applicant is a tenant registering an RR number where the meter is in the owner's name.  
6. **Valid Address Proof** — Required if the applicant's Aadhaar address differs from the Karnataka residence address (e.g., rental agreement, utility bill at current address).

**Note:** No charges are levied when applying for the Karnataka Gruha Jyothi Scheme. There is no requirement to submit income proof, caste certificate, ration card, or property documents as part of the standard application.  
**6\. Cost**

**Application Fee:** Free. There are no charges or fees to apply for the Gruha Jyothi scheme. Offline service centres (Bangalore One, Grama One, Karnataka One) may charge a nominal convenience fee per their standard service tariffs, but the scheme registration itself carries no government fee.  
**Effect on Existing Electricity Bills:**

* Once approved, electricity bills with less than 200 units consumption will be marked as 'ZERO Bills' and the consumer does not have to pay any bill.  
* If the electricity bill for any particular month exceeds 200 units, the consumer is not eligible for the zero bill that month and must pay the entire bill amount.  
* If monthly electricity consumption is more than the average consumption units but below 200 units, consumers pay only for the difference as the net bill.  
* The government reimburses the ESCOMs directly for the subsidised amount. Every month, the concerned ESCOM issues a bill to every customer; however, the government pays the bill amount.  
* Fixed charges and tariff hikes: Despite recent hikes in fixed charges and tariffs for electricity, Gruha Jyothi beneficiaries are to remain unaffected; their costs are to be covered by the government.

**Subsidy Scale:** From August 2023 to June 2025, an amount of ₹1,995.2 crore was released under the Gruha Jyothi subsidy in five districts under CESC alone. For about two years, the government spent approximately ₹18,900 crore on Gruha Jyothi statewide.  
**7\. Processing Timeline**

* **Registration opens:** 18 June 2023 (ongoing; no deadline has been announced for new registrations).  
* **Benefit commencement:** Subsidised bills were generated from 1 August 2023\. Applicants who applied after the initial window receive the benefit from the next billing cycle following verification.  
* **Application verification:** The approval process typically takes around 7 to 14 working days after successful submission. It may vary depending on the ESCOM and verification speed.  
* **After approval:** The next generated electricity bill will reflect the zero or reduced billing.  
* **New residents (no prior data):** Receive default 55 free units per month until one full year of consumption history is accumulated, after which entitlement is recalculated.  
* **Annual recalculation:** Entitlement units are reviewed annually based on the previous financial year's consumption data. As of 2026, the base year remains FY 2022–23 for entitlement calculation per revised guidelines.

**9\. Common Rejection Reasons and Eligibility Disputes**

**Common Rejection Reasons:**

1. Name mismatch — the name on the Aadhaar card does not match the name on the electricity connection.  
2. Incorrect ESCOM selection — applicant selected the wrong electricity supply company.  
3. Wrong or mistyped Account ID / Connection ID.  
4. Average monthly consumption in FY 2022–23 exceeded 200 units — the household is categorically ineligible.  
5. Connection is non-domestic (commercial or industrial) — only domestic connections qualify.  
6. Account ID already linked to another Aadhaar card under the Gruha Jyothi Scheme — the existing Aadhaar must be delinked first before reapplying.  
7. Multiple meters registered under one Aadhaar — only one meter per person is permitted.  
8. Unpaid electricity dues exceeding the prescribed period.

**Eligibility Disputes:**

* Tenancy disputes: If the meter is in the owner's name and the owner has already registered, the tenant cannot register the same RR number without the owner first delinking. The resolution involves the owner delinking, after which the tenant can apply using their own Aadhaar and a rental agreement.  
* Deceased account holder: Where the registered account holder is deceased and the electricity connection has not been transferred, the heir may face difficulty registering. The practical resolution is to first apply for a name transfer at the respective ESCOM before registering for Gruha Jyothi.  
* Average consumption disputes: If the ESCOM's database reflects incorrect historical consumption (e.g., estimated readings), consumers should file a rectification request at the concerned ESCOM divisional office before applying.  
* Energy Minister KJ George has indicated that the state cabinet will review the inclusion of new beneficiaries, especially those who have shifted homes or whose usage has changed, with a promise to reassess free unit allotments.

**Reapplication:** If the application is rejected, the applicant can reapply through the Seva Sindhu portal by correcting previous mistakes such as name mismatch, incorrect ESCOM selection, or wrong account ID.  
**10\. Interaction with Other Services**

**E-Khata:** Registration for Gruha Jyothi does NOT require an E-Khata or Khata certificate. The scheme is based solely on the ESCOM electricity connection record, not on property ownership records. Tenants without any property registration may freely apply.  
**BESCOM / ESCOM Name Change:** The scheme can be registered even if the electricity connection is in the owner's name; tenants can register the RR number of the house they are residing in by providing their Aadhaar number and a copy of the rental agreement. However, if a name change on the ESCOM account is desired for other administrative reasons (e.g., inheritance), this should be done separately with the ESCOM. A name change on the ESCOM account is not a prerequisite for Gruha Jyothi registration, but in cases of deceased account holders, a name transfer at the ESCOM is practically advisable to avoid future linking conflicts.  
**Property Tax Records:** Property tax records being in the applicant's name are NOT a prerequisite for Gruha Jyothi registration. The scheme relies entirely on the ESCOM consumer database and Aadhaar linkage.  
**Seva Sindhu Portal:** Gruha Jyothi registration is channelled exclusively through the Seva Sindhu portal (https://sevasindhugs.karnataka.gov.in/) or offline citizen service centres (Bangalore One, Grama One, Karnataka One). It does not interact with BBMP, BDA, or revenue department portals.  
**Gruha Lakshmi Scheme:** Gruha Jyothi and Gruha Lakshmi are separate guarantee schemes administered independently. Eligibility for one does not affect eligibility for the other. Applicants may register for both schemes simultaneously using the same Aadhaar.  
**Apartment / Common Area Meters:** Residents in apartments can apply for the scheme only if a separate meter has been installed for their individual unit. Common-area meters or bulk supply meters used for entire buildings are not eligible for domestic Gruha Jyothi benefits.  
**Delink Requirement on House Shift:** Karnataka residents can register only one electricity connection at a time. If a beneficiary moves from one house to another, they must delink their account from Gruha Jyothi on the Seva Sindhu portal and submit a new application for the new address.
