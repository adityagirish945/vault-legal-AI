1. **Legal definition, scheme notification and benefit**  
   1.1. The Gruha Jyoti (often spelt “Gruha Jyothi”) Scheme is described by the Government of Karnataka and its ESCOMs as a flagship program that provides free electricity up to 200 units per month to every eligible residential household in Karnataka. BESCOM’s official FAQ defines it as a program “which provides free electricity up to 200 units for every residential household of Karnataka.”bescom.karnataka.gov+1  
   1.2. The Energy Minister and government guidelines state that the scheme gives a maximum entitlement of 200 free units per month, calculated from a consumer’s average monthly usage in financial year 2022–23 plus a 10% buffer, subject to the 200‑unit cap. Press reports on the guidelines note that this model is distinct from other states and that the scheme is expected to cost the state around ₹13,000 crore per year in subsidy to ESCOMs.wirecable+3  
   1.3. The scheme was announced as one of the “guarantee” programmes of the newly elected government in 2023 and formally implemented from July 2023 consumption (benefit applying from meter readings on or after 1 August 2023). Existing power subsidy schemes Kuteera Jyothi, Amrutha Jyothi and Bhagya Jyothi have been brought under or merged into Gruha Jyoti.housing+3  
   1.4. The Energy Department’s official “Year 2022‑23” page lists a reform item titled “Providing free electricity up to 200 units per month to each household under Gruha Jyoti Scheme”, with a file reference beginning “ENERGY/154/PSR/…”, indicating the internal file / order under which the scheme is recorded in departmental proceedings.\[[energy.karnataka.gov](https://energy.karnataka.gov.in/70/year-2022-23/en)\]​  
   1.5. The HESCOM Gruha Jyothi page lists two specific “Gruha Jyothi Government Order” documents, dated 05‑06‑2023 and 15‑06‑2023, issued by the Government of Karnataka and hosted as scheme Government Orders, which constitute the primary administrative notifications for Gruha Jyoti. Although the full G.O. numbers are not visible in the snippet accessible here, those two Energy Department Government Orders, read with the department’s scheme entry in “Year‑2022‑23”, form the core legal notifications creating and governing the scheme.hescom.karnataka+1  
2. **Eligibility criteria (who qualifies / who is excluded)**  
   2.1. **Basic eligibility**  
   * All “residential consumers within the State of Karnataka” are in principle eligible for the scheme, according to the official BESCOM FAQ.\[[bescom.karnataka.gov](https://bescom.karnataka.gov.in/storage/pdf-files/aetomd/GruhaJyothiFAQ.pdf)\]​  
   * The scheme applies only to domestic / residential household electricity connections (LT‑1 and LT‑2 categories), and is explicitly not available for commercial or industrial establishments.newindianexpress+3  
   * Tenants in rented premises are eligible, even if the meter is in the landlord’s or builder’s name, provided they furnish acceptable address proof (such as lease/rent agreement or voter ID) along with Aadhaar.nobroker+3  
3. 2.2. **Unit‑based benefit condition**  
   * Free units are not a flat 200 units for everyone: the benefit is calculated as the household’s average monthly consumption for FY 2022‑23 plus 10%, with a hard cap of 200 units.schemealerts+3  
   * If the average plus 10% figure is ≤ 200 units, that number becomes the consumer’s “entitlement units”; consumption up to that level in a month results in a zero bill, while usage above that entitlement (but under 200 units) is billed only for the excess.vaultproptech+3  
   * If monthly consumption exceeds 200 units, the consumer must pay the entire bill for that month without Gruha Jyoti subsidy.wirecable+3  
4. 2.3. **Aadhaar and account linkage**  
   * Registration on the Seva Sindhu portal and linking of Aadhaar with the consumer’s Account ID / Consumer ID is mandatory to avail the scheme.bankbazaar+3  
   * BESCOM FAQ clarifies that Aadhaar registered outside Karnataka is acceptable, provided the consumer is residing in Karnataka and can provide Karnataka address proof.bangaloremirror.indiatimes+2  
5. 2.4. **Meter / account conditions**  
   * Only one meter per household can receive Gruha Jyoti benefits; if a consumer has more than one electricity connection, only one connection will be eligible.housing+2  
   * Guidelines clarify that eligibility is based on RR (Revenue Register) numbers for individual meters, not on property Khata categories; Minister K.J. George has stated that tenants or owners are eligible so long as there is a unique RR number for the residential connection.newindianexpress+1  
   * Apartment residents with individual sub‑meters in the builder’s name can apply if they provide proof of residence corresponding to the RR number.bangaloremirror.indiatimes+1  
6. 2.5. **Arrears and payment behaviour**  
   * Consumers with arrears are not automatically ineligible, but arrears outstanding up to 30 June 2023 (including the bill issued in July 2023 for June consumption) must be paid within three months; otherwise, the connection is liable to be disconnected.bescom.karnataka.gov+3  
   * If, after joining the scheme, the consumer fails to pay net payable amounts (for months where consumption exceeds entitlement/200 units), the supply can be disconnected; once arrears are cleared, Gruha Jyoti benefits resume.bangaloremirror.indiatimes+1  
7. 2.6. **Explicit exclusions**  
   * Commercial and industrial connections and shops, offices and business establishments are outside the scope of the scheme.govtschemes+3  
   * Households whose average FY 2022‑23 consumption is above 200 units are not eligible for free units; they continue to pay normal bills.newindianexpress+2  
   * If the connection is currently in the name of a deceased person (for example, a late parent), the FAQ states that the connection must first be transferred into the applicant’s name and only then can they apply for the scheme.bescom.karnataka.gov+1  
8. 2.7. **Income criteria**  
   * Neither the BESCOM FAQ nor the published guidelines and explanatory articles specify any income limit; multiple summaries describe the scheme as open to all domestic households meeting the unit and connection criteria, regardless of income.sevasindhukarnataka+3  
9. **Governing body and official portal**  
   3.1. The Gruha Jyoti Scheme is implemented by the Government of Karnataka through its **Energy Department**, which is listed as the “Concerned Department” in multiple scheme summaries and departmental materials.energy.karnataka+2  
   3.2. Operational implementation is through the state’s Electricity Supply Companies (ESCOMs) — BESCOM, MESCOM, HESCOM, GESCOM and CESC — which issue bills, compute entitlement units and apply the Gruha Jyoti subsidy line item. CESC’s official page, for example, provides direct links to “Apply for Gruha Jyothi Scheme” and “De‑register from Gruha Jyothi Scheme”.cescmysore.karnataka+2  
   3.3. The **official citizen portal** for registration and status tracking is the **Seva Sindhu Guarantee Schemes portal** at:  
   * Main portal: [https://sevasindhugs.karnataka.gov.in](https://sevasindhugs.karnataka.gov.in/)sevasindhugs.karnataka+1  
   * Application status tracking: [https://sevasindhugs.karnataka.gov.in/StatucTrack/Track\_Status](https://sevasindhugs.karnataka.gov.in/StatucTrack/Track_Status)\[[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BtkPYs_lM2w)\]​\[[nobroker](https://www.nobroker.in/blog/gruha-jyothi-scheme/)\]​  
10. 3.4. ESCOM‑specific information and FAQs are available on their respective official websites (e.g., BESCOM’s Gruha Jyothi FAQ PDF, HESCOM’s Gruhajyothi page, CESC’s scheme page), but all registrations ultimately flow through the Seva Sindhu Guarantee Schemes interface.hescom.karnataka+2  
11. **Step‑by‑step registration process (online and offline)**  
    4.1. **Prerequisites (for both modes)**  
    * Latest electricity bill to obtain Account ID / RR number and ESCOM name.govtschemes+1  
    * Aadhaar number and mobile number linked to Aadhaar for OTP verification.bankbazaar+2  
    * Address proof; for tenants, rental/lease agreement or voter ID or equivalent proof of residing at the supply address.newindianexpress+3  
12. 4.2. **Online registration via Seva Sindhu Guarantee Schemes**  
    * Visit the Seva Sindhu Guarantee Schemes portal at [https://sevasindhugs.karnataka.gov.in](https://sevasindhugs.karnataka.gov.in/) and click on the Gruha Jyothi scheme icon or link.testbook+3  
    * Choose the preferred language (Kannada/English) on the Gruha Jyoti application page.zencitizen+1  
    * Select your ESCOM (BESCOM, MESCOM, HESCOM, GESCOM, CESC) from the drop‑down and enter your Account ID / Connection ID or RR number exactly as printed on the bill; the system auto‑populates the account holder’s name and address.\[[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zlGjEiavmII)\]​zencitizen+2  
    * Confirm the auto‑filled details and select the type of occupancy (Owner / Tenant) as required by the form.youtube+1\[[govtschemes](https://www.govtschemes.in/karnataka-gruha-jyothi-scheme)\]​  
    * Enter the applicant’s Aadhaar number and click “Get Details” / “Get Aadhaar Details”; an OTP is sent to the Aadhaar‑linked mobile, which must be entered to complete Aadhaar authentication.reddit+2  
    * Provide the active mobile number (for SMS alerts) and, where asked, email ID; verify that the applicant name matches the Aadhaar details.\[[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ikW1VzO6PBA)\]​\[[bankbazaar](https://www.bankbazaar.com/electricity-bill/karnataka-gruha-jyothi-scheme.html)\]​  
    * Tick the mandatory declaration confirming that the Account ID/connection is used only for domestic purposes (not commercial) and that information given is true, then enter the captcha code.\[[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zlGjEiavmII)\]​\[[govtschemes](https://www.govtschemes.in/karnataka-gruha-jyothi-scheme)\]​  
    * Click “Submit”. A draft view of the application may appear; after reviewing, confirm submission again if prompted.\[[zencitizen](https://zencitizen.in/2025/09/08/how-to-apply-for-the-gruha-jyothi-scheme/)\]​\[[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ikW1VzO6PBA)\]​  
    * Upon successful submission, an acknowledgement page and Application Number are generated; applicants are advised to note or download this acknowledgement for future reference and status tracking.vaultproptech+2  
    * Application status can later be checked on the Seva Sindhu “Track Status” page by entering the Application Number or Account ID and selecting Gruha Jyothi as the service.gruhajyothischeme+1\[[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BtkPYs_lM2w)\]​  
13. 4.3. **Offline / assisted registration**  
    * Visit any **Grama One, Karnataka One or Bangalore One** centre, which are expressly authorised to provide assisted Gruha Jyoti registration.gpdp+2  
    * Obtain the Gruha Jyothi registration form if a paper form is being used, or request the operator to select the Gruha Jyothi service on the Seva Sindhu portal in assisted mode.gpdp+1  
    * Provide the ESCOM name, Account ID/RR number, account holder name and address as per the bill, and indicate whether the applicant is owner or tenant.govtschemes+1  
    * Provide Aadhaar number and the Aadhaar‑linked mobile number so that the operator can complete OTP‑based Aadhaar authentication on the portal.govtschemes+1  
    * Submit photocopies (or scanned copies where the centre uploads documents) of Aadhaar, the latest electricity bill, and tenancy proof / voter ID or similar address proof if applying as a tenant or where bill name does not match current occupant.newindianexpress+3  
    * The operator fills and submits the Seva Sindhu application on behalf of the applicant; a printed or SMS acknowledgement / Application Number should be provided to the applicant for their records.gruhajyothischeme+2  
    * Officially, no fee is to be charged to citizens for uploading guarantee‑scheme applications; any collection of fees by Grama One centres for Gruha Jyoti registration has been explicitly criticised by district officials, with instructions that registration must be free and that the government reimburses centres per application.timesofindia.indiatimes+1  
14. **Complete document checklist**  
    5.1. **Mandatory documents**  
    * **Aadhaar Card** of the applicant (owner or tenant) — used for identity, residency and mandatory Aadhaar‑to‑Account ID linkage.sevasindhukarnataka+2  
    * **Electricity bill** for the relevant connection showing ESCOM name, Account ID / Consumer ID / RR number, and address; any recent month’s bill is acceptable.housing+2  
    * **Mobile number linked to Aadhaar** — required to receive OTP for Aadhaar authentication on Seva Sindhu.bankbazaar+2  
15. 5.2. **Address / occupancy proof**  
    * For **owners** whose Aadhaar address matches the supply address, Aadhaar itself generally serves as address proof.housing+1  
    * For **tenants** or occupants whose Aadhaar address does not match the supply address, any of the following are accepted:  
      * Rental / lease agreement (registered or notarised not strictly required, per minister’s clarifications).bangaloremirror.indiatimes+2  
      * Voter ID showing the supply address.bescom.karnataka.gov+2  
      * Other address proofs such as driving licence, passport, or ration card showing the supply address (as indicated in explanatory coverage).\[[bangaloremirror.indiatimes](https://bangaloremirror.indiatimes.com/bangalore/others/a-to-z-of-gruha-jyoti-scheme/articleshow/100830508.cms)\]​  
16. 5.3. **Special situations**  
    * **Tenants**: tenancy document plus Aadhaar are required; NoBroker and other guides emphasise Aadhaar and proof of residency (rental agreement) as the main documents.nobroker+3  
    * **Connection in deceased person’s name**: BESCOM FAQ and related Q\&A state that the connection must first be transferred to the legal heir’s name via standard name‑change procedure at Jana Snehi Vidyuth service counters; this process itself may require death certificate and ownership proof, though such supporting documents are under general ESCOM rules, not Gruha Jyoti‑specific.bescom.karnataka.gov+1  
    * **New connections / newly occupied houses**: policy notes that a separate method would be announced for new connections; explanatory articles indicate that new consumers initially get a provisional free‑units entitlement (e.g., about 53 units \+ 10%) until a full year’s consumption history is available.schemealerts+3  
17. 5.4. **Not required**  
    * None of the official FAQs or ministerial explanations require **e‑Khata**, A‑Khata/B‑Khata certificates, or **property tax receipts** as part of the Gruha Jyoti registration documents; instead, eligibility is explicitly stated to be based on RR number and nature of connection, not on property Khata classification.newindianexpress+2  
18. **Cost, fees and impact on existing electricity bills**  
    6.1. **Registration and processing fees**  
    * BESCOM’s official FAQ explicitly states: “No fees has to be paid while registering on Seva Sindhu website.”\[[bescom.karnataka.gov](https://bescom.karnataka.gov.in/storage/pdf-files/aetomd/GruhaJyothiFAQ.pdf)\]​  
    * Government and media reports on Grama One operations confirm that registration for the guarantee schemes, including Gruha Jyoti, is supposed to be free for applicants; where centres were found charging ₹50 per application, district authorities clarified that such fees are not permitted and that the government instead pays a per‑application amount to Grama One centres.\[[timesofindia.indiatimes](https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/mangaluru/grama-one-centres-that-collect-fee-from-people-will-be-blacklisted-dc/articleshow/101250402.cms)\]​  
19. 6.2. **Treatment of pre‑scheme and existing bills**  
    * Consumers must pay their regular bills for June 2023; the scheme benefits apply from meter readings on or after 1 August 2023, corresponding to July 2023 consumption.newindianexpress+1  
    * Arrears for consumption up to 30 June 2023 (including the bill issued in July 2023 for June usage) must be cleared within three months; failure to pay makes the connection liable for disconnection, though Gruha Jyoti eligibility resumes after arrears are cleared.bangaloremirror.indiatimes+2  
20. 6.3. **How “free” electricity is applied on the bill**  
    * Under the guidelines, ESCOMs generate the bill as usual, then compute the number of free entitlement units (average 2022–23 consumption \+ 10% buffer, capped at 200\) and apply a Gruha Jyoti subsidy line item to offset charges for those units.\[[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SyDMADyaT7c)\]​newindianexpress+1  
    * BESCOM FAQ clarifies that if a consumer’s monthly consumption is less than their entitled units, they will receive a **zero bill** for that month.newindianexpress+1  
    * If consumption exceeds entitlement but remains ≤ 200 units, the consumer pays only for the excess units; if consumption exceeds 200 units, they pay the full bill for that month.wirecable+3  
    * While some early commentary suggested that fixed charges might still be payable even within 200 units, the final model described by BESCOM and the Energy Minister is that eligible consumers whose usage is within their entitlement receive zero‑amount bills, with the state budget providing subsidy to the ESCOMs to cover both energy charges and associated costs.newindianexpress+2  
21. **Processing timeline (application to benefit activation)**  
    7.1. **Scheme‑level effective date**  
    * For initial roll‑out, the Government directed that Gruha Jyoti benefits would apply from the meter reading date on or after 1 August 2023, for consumption in July 2023, irrespective of when in June the consumer registered (as long as it was within the notified registration window).testbook+2  
22. 7.2. **Current processing practice for new applicants**  
    * Official ESCOM and Seva Sindhu documents do not state a rigid statutory processing timeframe, but practice‑oriented guides based on portal behaviour indicate that applications typically move from “Application Received” to “Application Approved” status in about **7–14 working days**, though this may vary with ESCOM workload.\[[gruhajyothischeme](https://gruhajyothischeme.com/gruha-jyothi-scheme-status-check/)\]​  
    * Status descriptions commonly used on the Seva Sindhu tracking page include “Application Received” (submitted but not yet processed), “Application Approved”, and “Application Rejected”; applicants are advised to re‑check status after a few days and to reapply with corrected details in case of rejection.sevaasindhukarnataka+2  
23. 7.3. **Activation on bills**  
    * Once an application is approved, the entitlement units and Gruha Jyoti subsidy begin reflecting from the next applicable billing cycle, i.e., from the next meter reading date when the bill is generated after approval.vaultproptech+2  
    * In practice, some consumers have reported delays of one or more billing cycles due to high application volumes or billing‑system updates; government clarifications acknowledge that there were initial delays in bill issuance when the scheme was introduced, but billing was expected to normalise once implementation stabilised.bangaloremirror.indiatimes+2  
24. **Government Orders, notification dates and legal basis**  
    8.1. **Government Orders listed by ESCOMs**  
    * The HESCOM Gruhajyothi page lists two specific “Gruha Jyothi Government Order” documents dated **05‑06‑2023** and **15‑06‑2023**, available in Kannada and described as “Government Order” for the scheme; these are Energy Department GOs that set out the detailed scheme conditions.\[[hescom.karnataka.gov](https://hescom.karnataka.gov.in/156/Gruhajyothi/en)\]​  
    * These GOs give effect to the budget / manifesto announcement to provide free electricity up to 200 units per month to domestic households and lay down operational parameters such as calculation of average consumption, treatment of multiple meters, and ESCOM implementation responsibilities, as reflected in later FAQs and ministerial explanations.wirecable+2  
25. 8.2. **Energy Department file reference**  
    * The Energy Department’s “Year‑2022‑23” page shows a reform entry explicitly titled “Providing free electricity up to 200 units per month to each household under Gruha Jyoti Scheme”, with an associated file reference beginning **“ENERGY/154/PSR/…”**, evidencing that the scheme is processed and recorded as a formal Energy Department file / order.\[[energy.karnataka.gov](https://energy.karnataka.gov.in/70/year-2022-23/en)\]​  
    * This departmental entry indicates that Gruha Jyoti is anchored in Energy Department administrative powers and implemented via subsidy support to ESCOMs in line with standard electricity‑subsidy practice under the Electricity Act and state‑budget provisions, though the exact Act sections are not spelled out on the public page.energy.karnataka+1  
26. 8.3. **Ministerial guidelines and clarifications**  
    * On 6 June 2023, the Karnataka Government publicly released Gruha Jyoti guidelines; press coverage quotes Energy Minister K.J. George explaining the average‑plus‑10% rule, the 200‑unit cap, mandatory registration on Seva Sindhu, and the estimated annual subsidy burden of about ₹13,000 crore.newindianexpress+1  
    * A detailed article in The New Indian Express dated 5 June 2023 further clarifies that tenants are eligible, that only one connection per consumer is eligible, that the guarantee applies from August billings for July consumption, and that arrears up to 30 June 2023 must be cleared within three months — all of which mirror the subsequent BESCOM FAQ content and confirm the legal conditions issued in the official guidelines / GOs.bescom.karnataka.gov+1  
27. **Common rejection reasons and eligibility disputes**  
    9.1. **Typical rejection reasons (as observed on Seva Sindhu and scheme guides)**  
    * Scheme‑status guides indicate that “Application Rejected” statuses often arise from **errors or mismatches in submitted details**, such as incorrect Account ID, wrong ESCOM selection, or name mismatches between Aadhaar and the electricity account.sevaasindhukarnataka+1  
    * Missing or invalid documents — for example, absence of required tenancy proof where the applicant is not the named account holder — are also cited as causes for rejection.sevaasindhukarnataka+2  
    * Technical or portal errors (server overload, session timeouts, Aadhaar‑authentication failures) can lead to incomplete submissions that later appear as rejected or “No Record Found” in status checks; applicants are advised to re‑submit in such cases.reddit+2\[[youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ikW1VzO6PBA)\]​  
28. 9.2. **Eligibility‑related disputes**  
    * **Consumption above 200 units**: Consumers whose monthly usage occasionally exceeds 200 units sometimes dispute losing the benefit for that month; however, both the guidelines and FAQ are explicit that if consumption crosses 200 units, the entire month’s bill must be paid without subsidy.newindianexpress+3  
    * **Average‑consumption calculation**: Some disputes arise over how the FY 2022‑23 average is computed, especially for consumers in newly occupied premises or with incomplete history; explanatory materials note that for such cases, provisional free‑unit entitlements are based on limited historical data and may be lower, leading to confusion.reddit+3  
    * **Tenants vs. owners**: Despite clear statements that tenants are eligible, there have been misconceptions and occasional resistance from landlords; Energy Department clarifications emphasise that eligibility is tied to the RR number and domestic use, not to ownership, and tenants with proper address proof are entitled to apply.nobroker+2  
    * **Arrears and disconnection**: Some consumers interpret arrears as making them permanently ineligible; the FAQ clarifies instead that arrears must be cleared within three months to avoid disconnection, and that Gruha Jyoti benefits resume once arrears are paid and supply is restored.bangaloremirror.indiatimes+2  
29. 9.3. **Procedural issues**  
    * Users have reported applications being “stuck in processing” for several months; informal guidance from helplines and user experiences suggests repeatedly following up with ESCOM (dialling 1912\) and checking whether entitlement units are actually being displayed on bills, as bill systems may update ahead of visible status.reddit+1  
    * Grama One centres charging unofficial fees for registration have led to complaints; district officials have clarified that such charges are not authorised and threatened blacklisting of centres that collect money for Gruha Jyoti enrolment.\[[timesofindia.indiatimes](https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/mangaluru/grama-one-centres-that-collect-fee-from-people-will-be-blacklisted-dc/articleshow/101250402.cms)\]​  
30. **Interaction with other services (e‑Khata, BESCOM name change, property tax records, etc.)**

10.1. **Khata / property records**

* A detailed “A to Z of Gruha Jyoti Scheme” article, based on official clarifications, explicitly states that the **eligibility criteria are based on RR numbers rather than on whether residents possess an A‑Khata or B‑Khata**, confirming that property‑title documentation (including e‑Khata) is not a condition for Gruha Jyoti eligibility.\[[bangaloremirror.indiatimes](https://bangaloremirror.indiatimes.com/bangalore/others/a-to-z-of-gruha-jyoti-scheme/articleshow/100830508.cms)\]​  
* Neither the BESCOM FAQ nor Energy Department or ESCOM pages list property tax receipts, Khata extracts or similar land‑revenue documents in the Gruha Jyoti document checklist; instead, address and occupancy are established via Aadhaar, voter ID, lease agreement and similar proofs.housing+3

10.2. **BESCOM / ESCOM name change**

* For cases where the connection is in a deceased relative’s name (e.g., “my late father’s name”), the FAQ and related Q\&A clearly require that the **connection be transferred into the applicant’s name** before they apply; name change is handled at Jana Snehi Vidyuth service counters in ESCOM sub‑divisions under standard electricity‑supply procedures.bescom.karnataka.gov+1  
* However, this requirement does **not** apply to tenants generally: tenants can obtain Gruha Jyoti benefits on a meter that remains in the owner’s or builder’s name, as long as they provide appropriate tenancy / address proof and Aadhaar, so there is no requirement that meter name match the tenant’s name in such cases.nobroker+3

10.3. **Property tax and municipal records**

* No official scheme material indicates any requirement that property tax records be in the applicant’s name, nor that municipal property‑tax payment status affects Gruha Jyoti eligibility; eligibility is tied to the electricity connection category, RR number, consumption history and successful Aadhaar‑Account ID linkage.housing+3

10.4. **Interaction with other welfare and legacy electricity schemes**

* Existing beneficiaries of Bhagya Jyoti, Kuteera Jyoti and Amrutha Jyoti are migrated under Gruha Jyoti; Gruha Jyoti effectively consolidates these previous domestic‑power subsidy schemes rather than operating alongside them.housing+1  
* There is no indication in official or explanatory materials that availing Gruha Jyoti disqualifies a household from other non‑electricity welfare schemes; interaction is primarily within the electricity domain, where Gruha Jyoti becomes the umbrella domestic‑subsidy framework in place of older programmes.sevasindhukarnataka+2
