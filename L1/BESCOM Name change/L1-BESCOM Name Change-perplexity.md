1. Legal definition and legal/practical importance

1.1 Official legal concept and terminology

* Under the Karnataka Electricity Regulatory Commission (KERC) “Conditions of Supply of Electricity of Distribution Licensees in the State of Karnataka”, a BESCOM ownership/name change is legally treated as a **“Transfer of Installation (Change in the name of Consumer)” under Clause 36.00**.bescom.karnataka+1

* Form 13 issued by BESCOM is titled “TRANSFER OF INSTALLATION UNDER CLAUSE 36.00 OF KERC CONDITIONS OF SUPPLY” and is the prescribed application for change of name of the registered consumer for a specific Revenue Register (RR) number / Account ID.bescom.karnataka.gov+1

1.2 What changes in the electricity records

* When a transfer of installation is approved, BESCOM updates its Revenue Register and customer database to substitute the existing “registered consumer” with the transferee for the concerned RR No./Account ID, including linked details such as name, contact details, and often the linked security deposit holder.onlineservices.bescom+1

* A fresh Low-Tension (LT) or High-Tension (HT) **Power Supply Agreement** (Annexure‑7 to the Conditions of Supply) is executed in the name of the new consumer, and any required **indemnity bond (Annexure‑9)** is taken, thereby novating the contractual relationship from BESCOM to the new consumer.bescom.karnataka+1

1.3 Legal consequences of the change

* Clause 36.02 of the Conditions of Supply provides that the original registered consumer’s power supply agreement is **deemed terminated** from the date of the order of transfer or seven days after the transferee completes formalities, whichever is earlier, so contractual liability for ongoing consumption shifts entirely to the new consumer from that point.\[[anyflip](https://anyflip.com/vbtb/uljq/basic/51-100)\]​

* Under Clause 36.01 the transferee must undertake to pay any short claims or dues that may be detected later, even for periods prior to the transfer, which makes the new consumer potentially liable for historic under‑billing or supplementary assessments linked to that installation.\[[anyflip](https://anyflip.com/vbtb/uljq/basic/51-100)\]​

1.4 Practical importance in property and utility law

* Numerous practitioner guides emphasise that BESCOM bills in the buyer’s name function as **de facto proof of possession and address**, used routinely for KYC, bank loans, and other service connections, and are often requested in due diligence for secondary transfers.alltechinformation+3

* Specialist services stress that if the BESCOM name is not updated, the previous owner may continue to receive demands and the new occupier can face disconnection, difficulty claiming deposit refunds, and disputes during resale or in inheritance proceedings.docupro+2

2. Scenarios where BESCOM name change is legally/practically required

2.1 Sale or purchase of property (outright transfer)

* KERC Clause 36.01(a) explicitly recognises transfer where the **premises are now owned by the transferee**, and requires proof of ownership such as a registered sale deed, partition deed, or khata.bescom.karnataka.gov+1

* Practitioner guides on BESCOM name transfer uniformly state that every time a flat/house/plot in Bengaluru changes hands by sale, the buyer should have the BESCOM installation transferred to avoid future liability and to align utilities with title.ezylegal+3

2.2 Death of account holder (succession)

* Form 13 and Clause 36.01 refer to “succession” and accept a **succession or heirship certificate or deed of last will** as proof to support a transfer of installation to a legal heir.anyflip+1

* Consumer‑facing guidance for cases where a father or account holder dies requires production of the death certificate, legal‑heir/succession certificate, property ownership documents, and often NOCs from other legal heirs to transfer the BESCOM bill to the heir actually in possession.nobroker+2

2.3 Inheritance, partition or bequest without an immediate sale

* KERC’s Conditions of Supply expressly provide for transfer where ownership changes by **partition** (partition deed) or **succession/heirship certificate / deed of last will**, so legal heirs or beneficiaries under a will can regularise the BESCOM records even if there is no sale.bescom.karnataka.gov+1

* Guides on Bangalore name transfer note that BESCOM treats such cases similarly to sale, but the ownership proof is a partition deed, succession certificate or probated will, sometimes along with mutation of khata into the heir’s name.zencitizen+2

2.4 Gift deed and family transfers

* Property documentation guides for Bengaluru state that BESCOM recognises **registered gift deeds** and other title instruments (sale deed, will, settlement) as ownership proof for name change, and recommend updating BESCOM immediately after such intra‑family transfers.bescom.trm.ieasybill+1

* In gift scenarios, BESCOM generally expects a gift deed plus the donor’s consent/NOC, and follows the same transfer‑of‑installation framework under Clause 36.ezylegal+1

2.5 Tenancy and other occupancy‑based changes

* Clause 36.01 allows transfer based on **proof of occupancy** such as a valid power of attorney, latest rent paid receipt or lease deed for non‑commercial lighting and combined lighting & heating installations, enabling tenants or non‑owner occupiers to become the registered consumer with the owner’s consent.anyflip+1

* Practice‑oriented articles explain that for tenants BESCOM often requires an indemnity bond and NOC from the owner/builder, so that liabilities and rights (like disconnection requests) are clearly mapped despite ownership remaining with the landlord.alltechinformation+1

3. Governing body and official portals

3.1 Competent authority and internal jurisdiction

* BESCOM (Bangalore Electricity Supply Company Limited), a wholly owned Government of Karnataka undertaking, is the **distribution licensee** for Bengaluru and nearby districts under the KERC Conditions of Supply and is therefore the competent body for name change/transfer of installation.bescom.karnataka+1

* Form 13 and its instructions specify that the completed transfer application is to be submitted to the **Assistant Executive Engineer (Electrical), Sub‑Division, BESCOM** for the concerned area, with inspection by the Assistant/Junior Engineer of the O\&M unit.\[[bescom.karnataka.gov](https://bescom.karnataka.gov.in/storage/pdf-files/Download%20Forms-English/FORM%2013%20TRANSFER%20OF%20INSTALLATION.pdf)\]​

3.2 Core official portals

* BESCOM’s main organisational website is [https://bescom.karnataka.gov.in/english](https://bescom.karnataka.gov.in/english), which hosts regulations, forms, and general information.\[[bescom.karnataka.gov](https://bescom.karnataka.gov.in/english)\]​

* The principal **consumer portal** for bills and account management is the “Consumer Portal – BESCOM” at [https://www.bescom.co.in/SCP/Myhome.aspx.](https://www.bescom.co.in/SCP/Myhome.aspx.)\[[thecurrentindia](https://www.thecurrentindia.com/bill-payment/bescom-electricity-bill-payment-online/)\]​

3.3 Name‑change specific online interfaces

* BESCOM runs an Online Services site at [https://onlineservices.bescom.org](https://onlineservices.bescom.org/), which includes a **Fast Track Name Change** workflow that requires prior consumer registration and login.onlineservices.bescom+1

* The Fast Track Name Change page (FastTrackNameChangeDemo.aspx) allows the consumer to submit a “Name Change Request”, specify whether the security deposit is to be transferred, select the **reason for seeking transfer**, and upload identity proof, registered sale deed, tax receipt/khata, NOC, and indemnity bond as per Annexure‑9.\[[onlineservices.bescom](https://onlineservices.bescom.org/\(S\(3mhaue4is22tpfecqjckttqj\)\)/FastTrackNameChangeDemo.aspx)\]​

3.4 Helpline and escalation channels

* BESCOM operates a 24x7 helpline from its corporate office in Bengaluru on **1912**, staffed by customer support executives and team leaders, which consumers are directed to contact for status updates or issues with applications including name change.ezylegal+2

4. Step‑by‑step process – scenario‑wise

4.1 Common preliminary steps (all scenarios)

* In all transfer scenarios, the applicant must collect prescribed documents, fill **Form 13 – Transfer of Installation**, arrange execution of a fresh LT/HT power supply agreement and indemnity bond where required, and ensure there are **no arrears** outstanding on the installation.ezylegal+2

* BESCOM typically insists that the latest BESCOM bill be paid up to date, property tax be current, and that any discrepancies in address or RR No./Account ID between documents and BESCOM records be clarified before accepting the application.docupro+2

4.2 Scenario (a): After property purchase (sale deed)

4.2.1 Offline (sub‑division) process

1. **Document collation**

   * Collect a notarised copy of the **registered sale deed**, latest property tax receipt, khata certificate/khata extract, latest paid BESCOM bill, and photo ID (Aadhaar, PAN, Passport or Voter ID).zencitizen+3

   * Obtain a **No Objection Certificate (NOC)** or consent letter from the seller (registered consumer or builder) to transfer the installation and security deposit, unless the sale deed itself expressly transfers the BESCOM installation and deposit.ezylegal+2

2. **Prepare statutory forms and bonds**

   * Download or obtain **Form 13 – Transfer of Installation** from the BESCOM forms page (Downloadable Forms) and fill in installation address, RR No./Account ID, and contact details of the proposed consumer.bescom.karnataka+1

   * Tick the reason “Premises now owned by me (Attach copy of Khata/Registered sale deed)” under point 1 of Form 13.\[[bescom.karnataka.gov](https://bescom.karnataka.gov.in/storage/pdf-files/Download%20Forms-English/FORM%2013%20TRANSFER%20OF%20INSTALLATION.pdf)\]​

   * Prepare a **Power Supply Agreement (Annexure‑7)** in duplicate on appropriate stamp paper for LT supply and an **Indemnity Bond (Annexure‑9)** indemnifying BESCOM against disputes regarding ownership or prior dues; these formats are reproduced in the BESCOM Form 13 packet.bescom.karnataka+1

3. **Submission at BESCOM Sub‑Division**

   * Submit the filled Form 13, power supply agreement, indemnity bond, and supporting documents at the office of the **Assistant Executive Engineer (Ele), Sub‑Division**, along with the prescribed transfer fee and any fresh security deposit if the seller has not consented to transfer the deposit.docupro+2

   * The Sub‑Division issues an acknowledgement / GSC number under the Karnataka Sakala service framework, which can be used to track the application.ezylegal+1

4. **Meter and site verification**

   * A BESCOM Assistant/Junior Engineer inspects the installation, records meter make, serial number, capacity and reading, and confirms whether the meter is inside or outside the premises, as per the office‑use section of Form 13.zencitizen+1

   * If any arrears, irregularities or discrepancies are noted (e.g., unauthorised load), BESCOM may require rectification or payment before proceeding with transfer.ezylegal+1

5. **Approval and issuance of Official Memorandum**

   * Once documents are in order and payments are confirmed, BESCOM issues an **Official Memorandum** recording transfer of the installation and deposit from the previous consumer to the new consumer, explicitly stating that BESCOM will not be responsible for inter‑se disputes between the parties.scribd+1

   * The consumer’s name normally reflects in the BESCOM bill in the next billing cycle, typically within one to two cycles after approval.docupro+2

4.2.2 Online process – BESCOM Fast Track Name Change

1. **Portal registration/login**

   * Register as a customer or log in on the BESCOM Online Services site at \*\*[https://onlineservices.bescom.org\*\*.](https://onlineservices.bescom.org%2A%2A./)onlineservices.bescom+1

2. **Initiate Fast Track Name Change**

   * Navigate to the **“Fast Track Name Change”** page (FastTrackNameChangeDemo.aspx) which is described as allowing consumers to lodge name‑change requests for non‑RAPDRP subdivisions.\[[onlineservices.bescom](https://onlineservices.bescom.org/\(S\(3mhaue4is22tpfecqjckttqj\)\)/FastTrackNameChangeDemo.aspx)\]​

   * Enter the Account ID; the portal auto‑populates existing consumer name and premises address, and prompts for the applicant’s details and reason for transfer.\[[onlineservices.bescom](https://onlineservices.bescom.org/\(S\(3mhaue4is22tpfecqjckttqj\)\)/FastTrackNameChangeDemo.aspx)\]​

3. **Specify deposit handling and upload documents**

   * Select whether the **security deposit is to be transferred** to the new consumer; if “No”, be prepared to pay fresh deposit offline or as directed.anyflip+1

   * Upload scanned copies (JPG/PDF etc., total upload size limit stated as 4 MB) of identity proof, registered sale deed (ownership proof), latest tax paid receipt/khata certificate, consent letter/NOC, indemnity bond, and any statutory licence/clearance required.\[[onlineservices.bescom](https://onlineservices.bescom.org/\(S\(3mhaue4is22tpfecqjckttqj\)\)/FastTrackNameChangeDemo.aspx)\]​

4. **Undertaking and submission**

   * Tick the on‑screen **undertaking** accepting liability to pay any outstanding dues or short claims detected later for periods prior to transfer, mirroring Clause 36.01 requirements.anyflip+1

   * Submit the request and note the service request number, which can be tracked on the **Track Fast Track Service Requests** page.onlineservices.bescom+1

5. **Office verification and finalisation**

   * The applicant may still be asked to visit the sub‑division with originals for verification and to sign physical agreements and pay fees/security deposit.ezylegal+1

   * After internal approval, the name is updated in BESCOM’s billing system similar to the offline route.ezylegal+1

4.2.3 Online process – Seva Sindhu (Name Change – Non RAPDRP)

1. Go to [**https://sevasindhu.karnataka.gov.in**](https://sevasindhu.karnataka.gov.in/) and select “Departments & Services” → “Bangalore Electricity Supply Company Limited” → “Name Change – Non RAPDRP”.sevasindhu.karnataka+1

2. Click “Apply Online”, log in using Seva Sindhu credentials (username/password or OTP), then enter the **Account ID/Connection ID** and click “Get Details” to auto‑fill the form.\[[sevasindhu.karnataka.gov](https://sevasindhu.karnataka.gov.in/Updated/Bangalore%20Electricity%20Supply%20Company-BESCOM/Name%20change%20BESCOM.pdf)\]​

3. Verify details, correct if necessary, then proceed to **attach annexures** (ownership proof, ID proof, NOC, indemnity bond etc.), save annexures, e‑sign using Aadhaar OTP, and make payment online.\[[sevasindhu.karnataka.gov](https://sevasindhu.karnataka.gov.in/Updated/Bangalore%20Electricity%20Supply%20Company-BESCOM/Name%20change%20BESCOM.pdf)\]​

4. An **acknowledgement** with applicant, application and payment details is generated; this can be used to follow up at the sub‑division.\[[sevasindhu.karnataka.gov](https://sevasindhu.karnataka.gov.in/Updated/Bangalore%20Electricity%20Supply%20Company-BESCOM/Name%20change%20BESCOM.pdf)\]​

4.3 Scenario (b): After death of owner / registered consumer

4.3.1 Typical fact patterns

* Consumer‑help resources describe cases where a father or earlier owner dies and the son or another heir in possession seeks to change the BESCOM bill name.nobroker+2

* In these situations, the legal heirs must both prove succession rights and demonstrate that they are the current owners/occupiers of the premises for the purposes of Clause 36.01.bescom.karnataka.gov+1

4.3.2 Offline process steps

1. **Succession documentation**

   * Obtain the **death certificate** of the deceased registered consumer from the local registrar of births and deaths.\[[nobroker](https://www.nobroker.in/forum/if-father-has-died-then-how-can-son-change-name-in-the-bill/)\]​

   * Secure a **legal heirship certificate** or **succession certificate** (as applicable), or a **probated will / deed of last will**, which documents recognised heirs to the property; these are expressly accepted by BESCOM/ESCs as ownership proof for transfer.ezylegal+2

   * If multiple heirs exist, obtain **NOCs from other heirs** in favour of the heir who will be registered as BESCOM consumer, to avoid inter‑se disputes later.nobroker+1

2. **Ownership and occupancy proof**

   * Produce the property ownership documents showing transmission to the heir (mutation entries/khata in heir’s name, partition deed with heir’s share, or probate order linking the will to the property) along with the latest tax paid receipt.docupro+2

   * Attach the last paid electricity bill and any receipts demonstrating that the applicant has been paying bills after the consumer’s death.nobroker+1

3. **Form 13 and indemnity**

   * Fill Form 13, selecting **“Succession (Attach succession or heirship certificate)”** as the reason for transfer and mentioning the deceased consumer’s name and date of death in the body of the application or covering letter.\[[bescom.karnataka.gov](https://bescom.karnataka.gov.in/storage/pdf-files/Download%20Forms-English/FORM%2013%20TRANSFER%20OF%20INSTALLATION.pdf)\]​

   * Execute an indemnity bond (Annex‑9) explicitly indemnifying BESCOM against disputes among heirs or future challenges to the heir’s title, as per Form 13 instructions.bescom.karnataka+1

4. **Submission and verification**

   * Submit the complete file to the Sub‑Division, pay the transfer fee and any required security deposit (if existing deposit is not being transferred), and obtain acknowledgement.anyflip+1

   * BESCOM’s field staff verify occupancy and meter particulars; if the property title is still in the deceased’s name but succession documents are clear, BESCOM may proceed based on those documents and heir NOCs.nobroker+1

5. **Approval and billing update**

   * After scrutiny, BESCOM issues an Official Memorandum transferring the installation from the deceased consumer to the heir, subject to the heir’s undertaking to pay prior short claims and liabilities.anyflip+1

   * Name updation timelines are broadly similar to sale cases, typically within 1–3 weeks from complete submission depending on volume.ezylegal+2

4.3.3 Online process adaptations

* The BESCOM Fast Track Name Change portal includes a dropdown “Reason for seeking transfer” under which inheritance/succession options may be selected; heirs then upload death certificate, heirship/succession certificate or will, and heir NOCs, along with usual identity and ownership proofs.onlineservices.bescom+1

* Seva Sindhu’s Name Change service similarly allows attachment of death and succession documents as annexures and uses the same e‑sign and payment flow as for sale‑based cases.\[[sevasindhu.karnataka.gov](https://sevasindhu.karnataka.gov.in/Updated/Bangalore%20Electricity%20Supply%20Company-BESCOM/Name%20change%20BESCOM.pdf)\]​

4.4 Scenario (c): Inheritance or gift without immediate mutation / family transfers

4.4.1 Inheritance without immediate khata transfer

* Clause 36.01 recognises succession and “deed of last will” as acceptable proof of ownership; in practice, BESCOM may proceed with transfer based on these along with a legal heir/succession certificate and tax receipts in the heir’s name, even while khata mutation is in progress.ezylegal+2

* Legal‑service guides advise heirs to regularise BESCOM records soon after succession to prevent arrears or future supplementary bills being raised in the deceased’s name, which complicates estate administration.bescom.trm.ieasybill+2

4.4.2 Registered gift deed or family partition

1. **Gift deed scenario**

   * Use the **registered gift deed** as primary ownership proof, supported by khata/tax receipts and NOC from the donor if they are the current registered consumer.bescom.trm.ieasybill+1

   * Form 13 reason is normally “Premises now owned by me”, with the gift deed attached instead of a sale deed; otherwise the steps mirror the sale‑based process.bescom.karnataka.gov+1

2. **Family partition scenario**

   * Where a family partition has been effected, attach the **partition deed** indicating which schedule property has fallen to the applicant, as envisaged in Form 13 reason “Partition (Attach partition deed)”.anyflip+1

   * If a joint BESCOM connection previously served the undivided property, BESCOM may require separate new connections or load adjustments in addition to transfer, depending on how the premises are physically divided.bescom.karnataka+1

4.4.3 Tenant/occupant name change (when allowed)

* For non‑commercial lighting/combined lighting and heating, Clause 36.01 permits transfer based on proof of occupancy such as lease deed or rent receipts, in which case the tenant executes the power supply agreement and indemnity bond, often with a landlord NOC.bescom.karnataka.gov+1

* Guides caution that some sub‑divisions prefer that ownership transfers (sale/gift/inheritance) be regularised first, with tenant‑based transfers treated more cautiously to avoid disputes between landlord and tenant.alltechinformation+2

5. Document checklist by scenario

5.1 Core documents (common across most scenarios)

* **Form 13 – Transfer of Installation**, duly filled and signed by the proposed consumer.bescom.karnataka+1

* **Photo ID proof**: Aadhaar, PAN, Passport, Voter ID, or Driving Licence.alltechinformation+3

* **Latest paid BESCOM bill** and, where available, receipt showing no outstanding dues.nobroker+3

* **Property tax receipt** (latest year) and **khata certificate/khata extract/e‑khata**.ezylegal+4

* **Electricity meter photograph** clearly showing meter number, RR No. and latest reading, often requested at filing or by JE during verification.ezylegal+2

* **Power Supply Agreement (Annexure‑7)** on stamp paper in duplicate and **Indemnity Bond (Annexure‑9)** on stamp paper in the format approved by KERC.bescom.karnataka+2

5.2 Sale/purchase (registered sale deed) – additional documents

* Notarised copy of **registered sale deed** for the property (primary ownership proof).zencitizen+3

* **NOC / consent letter** from the seller/previous consumer authorising transfer of installation and security deposit, unless this is clearly provided for in the sale deed; Form 13 includes a standard consent letter template for this purpose.ezylegal+2

* Encumbrance Certificate (EC) for the property is sometimes requested as supporting evidence, especially when there have been multiple transfers.bescom.trm.ieasybill+1

5.3 Death/succession scenarios – additional documents

* **Death certificate** of the deceased registered consumer.\[[nobroker](https://www.nobroker.in/forum/if-father-has-died-then-how-can-son-change-name-in-the-bill/)\]​

* **Legal heirship certificate** or **succession certificate** from competent authority, and/or probated **deed of last will**, all of which are recognised by Clause 36.01 or Form 13 instructions as acceptable ownership proofs.ezylegal+2

* **NOCs from co‑heirs** where the installation is to be transferred to only one heir, to reduce the risk of later disputes and indemnity claims.nobroker+1

5.4 Inheritance/gift/partition – additional documents

* **Registered gift deed** for gifted properties; documentation sources confirm that BESCOM accepts gift deeds as proof of ownership in name‑change applications.bescom.trm.ieasybill+1

* **Partition deed** specifying the schedule property allotted to the applicant, as explicitly referenced in Form 13.anyflip+1

* Proof of khata/assessment being transferred to or applied for in the transferee’s name can strengthen the case, though not always mandatory at the initial stage.docupro+1

5.5 Tenant/occupant‑based transfers – additional documents

* **Lease deed**, rent agreement or power of attorney, together with latest rent receipts, which Form 13 treats as proof of occupancy for non‑commercial installations.bescom.karnataka.gov+1

* **Owner/builder NOC** clearly authorising the tenant or occupier to hold the BESCOM account in their name.alltechinformation+2

5.6 Online‑specific technical requirements

* Scanned copies of all relevant documents (usually in PDF/JPEG formats) within the size limits (e.g., 4 MB total on the Fast Track Name Change page) for upload.sevasindhu.karnataka+1

* Aadhaar‑linked mobile number for **e‑sign** through Seva Sindhu’s Aadhaar OTP workflow.\[[sevasindhu.karnataka.gov](https://sevasindhu.karnataka.gov.in/Updated/Bangalore%20Electricity%20Supply%20Company-BESCOM/Name%20change%20BESCOM.pdf)\]​

6. Fee structure, deposits, and dues

6.1 Statutory name‑change / transfer fees

* Under Clause 30.10 of the Conditions of Supply, **transfer of installation charges** are fixed at **₹100 per LT installation** and **₹500 per HT installation**.bescom.karnataka+2

* Field experience‑based guides often mention transfer fees in the range of ₹100–₹250, which typically accounts for the statutory ₹100 plus incidental or service charges or local variations in processing; the official benchmark remains the KERC‑approved schedule.nobroker+3

6.2 Security deposit – transfer vs fresh deposit

* Clause 36.01 requires the transferee to indicate whether the existing **security deposit / “2 Monthly Minimum Deposit” (2MMD)** is to be transferred from the present consumer or a **fresh deposit** is to be paid, and Form 13 captures these options.anyflip+1

* Where the previous consumer gives written consent to transfer the deposit (via Form 13 consent letter or sale‑deed clause), BESCOM normally transfers the deposit and **does not demand additional deposit at the time of transfer**, subject to later Additional Security Deposit (ASD) reviews.docupro+2

* In the absence of such consent, the transferee must pay fresh **Initial Security Deposit (ISD)** calculated under Clause 30.02 and KERC (Interest on Security Deposit) Regulations, and the earlier consumer retains the right to refund of the old deposit upon surrender/disconnection.bescom.trm.ieasybill+2

6.3 Other associated costs

* Execution of the Power Supply Agreement and Indemnity Bond typically requires **stamp paper** (commonly quoted at around ₹500 each in practice‑oriented guides), plus notarisation charges where applicable.alltechinformation+2

* Some applicants incur professional fees to documentation agents or legal service providers for drafting NOCs, affidavits and liaising with BESCOM, which are private arrangements not regulated by KERC.ezylegal+2

6.4 Dues transfer and liability for arrears

* Clause 36.01(c) and the undertaking on the Fast Track Name Change page require the transferee to accept liability to pay any **short claims or outstanding dues detected later**, even if they relate to periods before the transfer was granted.onlineservices.bescom+1

* The Conditions of Supply also stipulate that there must be **no arrears outstanding against the installation** at the time of transfer, failing which BESCOM may refuse to process the application until dues are cleared.bescom.karnataka.gov+1

* The Supreme Court has upheld the power of distribution licensees, under supply codes framed pursuant to Sections 50 and 181 of the Electricity Act 2003, to impose conditions requiring subsequent owners or occupiers to clear electricity arrears of previous consumers as a pre‑condition for supply or reconnection in order to protect the utility’s financial interests.\[[digiscr.sci.gov](https://digiscr.sci.gov.in/admin/judgement_file/judgement_pdf/2023/volume%209/Part%20I/2023_9_637-782_1702990389.pdf)\]​

7. Processing timeline – official SLA and actual practice

7.1 Official service‑level commitments

* Form 13’s general instructions specify an **“Indicative Maximum time limit – 7 days from the date of receipt of application for transfer”**, effectively operating as a BESCOM service‑level target for transfer of installation once a complete application is received.scribd+1

* This period is consistent with broader KERC service‑quality expectations under its regulations for licensee duty to supply and complaint handling, though the Conditions of Supply do not spell out a separate SLA beyond what appears on the form.kerc.karnataka+2

7.2 Observed timelines in practice

* Multiple user guides and advisory articles for Bengaluru report that after document submission and JE verification, BESCOM typically completes name transfer within **7–15 working days**, with the updated name reflecting in the **next 1–2 billing cycles**.nobroker+4

* High transaction volumes in particular subdivisions, incomplete documentation, or the need for additional clarifications frequently cause delays beyond the nominal seven‑day period, prompting recommendations to track status via Sakala, the Fast Track tracking page, or the 1912 helpline.bescom.karnataka.gov+4

8. Relevant BESCOM regulations and statutory framework

8.1 KERC Conditions of Supply of Electricity (CoS)

* The **Conditions of Supply of Electricity of Distribution Licensees in the State of Karnataka**, first notified in 2006 with subsequent amendments up to 2017, apply to all ESCOMs including BESCOM and constitute the primary regulatory instrument for connection policies and transfer procedures.kerc.karnataka+1

* Clause **36.00 – Transfer of Installation (Change in the name of Consumer)** is the central provision governing name change; its sub‑clauses cover consent from the existing consumer, acceptable ownership/occupancy proofs, indemnity bond, no‑arrears condition, transfer fee, execution of fresh agreement, treatment of deposits, and termination of the old agreement.bescom.karnataka+1

* Clause **30.10** prescribes the charges for transfer of installation (₹100 for LT, ₹500 for HT), while Clauses 30.02–30.05 govern initial and additional security deposits and their refund and interest.bescom.karnataka+1

8.2 BESCOM forms and internal instruments

* **Form 13 – Transfer of Installation**, hosted on the BESCOM website’s Downloadable Forms section, operationalises Clause 36 for consumers and contains the official instructions, consent format, power supply agreement and indemnity bond templates.bescom.karnataka+1

* The **Official Memorandum** appended to Form 13 documents the administrative act of transfer and states that BESCOM will not be responsible for any disputes that may arise between transferor and transferee after transfer is recorded.scribd+1

8.3 KERC (Electricity Supply) Code, 2004 and related regulations

* The **KERC (Electricity Supply) Code, 2004** (Supply Code) lays down procedures for billing, disconnection, reconnection and recovery of charges, which frame how arrears and supplementary claims interact with transfers of installation.kerc.karnataka+1

* Other KERC regulations – notably the **KERC (Interest on Security Deposit) Regulations, 2005** and the **KERC (Duty of Licensee to Supply on Request) Regulations, 2004** (annexed to the Conditions of Supply) – govern interest on deposits, licensee response timelines and service standards relevant to BESCOM’s handling of transfer applications.kerc.karnataka+1

8.4 Parent statutes

* The Conditions of Supply and Supply Code are issued under the **Electricity Act 2003** and the **Karnataka Electricity Reform Act 1999**, which empower KERC to specify conditions applicable to distribution licensees such as BESCOM.kerc.karnataka+1

* The Supreme Court’s 2023 judgment on recovery of electricity arrears recognises that, under these statutory powers, State Commissions may frame conditions allowing utilities to require payment of previous owners’ dues as a condition of supply to new owners or occupiers, which underpins BESCOM’s stance on dues clearance at the time of transfer or reconnection.\[[digiscr.sci.gov](https://digiscr.sci.gov.in/admin/judgement_file/judgement_pdf/2023/volume%209/Part%20I/2023_9_637-782_1702990389.pdf)\]​

9. Common rejection and delay reasons

9.1 Documentation‑related issues

* Applications are frequently delayed or rejected for **incomplete or mismatched documentation**, such as sale deeds or khata not matching the BESCOM address, missing tax receipts, or uncertified copies where notarisation is required.zencitizen+2

* Failure to provide a proper **NOC/consent letter** from the existing registered consumer or builder, or from all relevant legal heirs in inheritance cases, is a common reason for objections, especially when the BESCOM records still show an earlier owner or the developer.nobroker+3

9.2 Arrears and billing irregularities

* BESCOM may refuse to process a transfer where there are **outstanding arrears** on the installation until the dues are cleared or responsibility is formally allocated via indemnity and payment, because Clause 36.01 requires that no arrears be outstanding at transfer.digiscr.sci.gov+2

* Situations involving previous unrecorded load, suspected theft, or pending supplementary bills (for example, after meter testing or inspection under the Supply Code) often lead to protracted scrutiny before transfer is approved.docupro+2

9.3 Procedural and portal issues

* Online applicants often report problems such as account ID not recognised, upload size limits, or timeouts on the Fast Track and Seva Sindhu portals, requiring physical follow‑up at the sub‑division.nobroker+2

* Failure to attend at the office with **original documents for verification**, or to sign the physical power supply agreement and indemnity bond where insisted on, can stall an application even if the online stage was completed.zencitizen+2

9.4 Title and heirship disputes

* Where there is a **dispute over ownership or succession** (e.g., rival sale deeds, contested wills, or pending civil litigation), BESCOM typically takes a cautious approach and may keep the connection in the existing name or require an indemnity bond and comprehensive heir NOCs before considering transfer.ezylegal+2

* In complex apartment situations where the builder never transferred connections to first buyers and is defunct, guidance suggests that BESCOM may ask for additional indemnity and documentary evidence (including chain of title and NOCs from earlier buyers) before approving transfers to current owners.nobroker+1

10. Legal and practical consequences of not changing the BESCOM name

10.1 Liability for past and future dues

* If the BESCOM account remains in the **previous owner’s or deceased’s name**, BESCOM’s contractual counter‑party remains that person or estate, but in practice BESCOM can insist that any new owner or occupier clear arrears as a condition for granting continued or new supply, consistent with the Supreme Court’s recognition of such conditions of supply.digiscr.sci.gov+1

* Because Clause 36.01 requires transferees to undertake liability for short claims even for pre‑transfer periods, failing to regularise the name can result in historic under‑billing or penalty demands emerging later and being enforced against whichever party BESCOM currently recognises as consumer, often leading to disputes between buyer and seller or among heirs.docupro+2

10.2 Risk of disconnection and service disruption

* Consumer guides warn that if ownership changes but the BESCOM name is not updated and future bills are not paid or are misrouted, BESCOM may **disconnect the supply** under its Supply Code powers for non‑payment, and reconnection may be made conditional on both name transfer and arrears clearance.nobroker+3

* In death cases where heirs do not approach BESCOM, continuing to run the connection in the deceased’s name can also complicate fault reporting, load‑change requests and other service interactions that require identity verification of the registered consumer.nobroker+2

10.3 Impact on property transactions and evidence

* Advisory articles emphasise that prospective purchasers, lenders and legal‑documentation services increasingly expect **electricity bills to align with current ownership**, and a mismatch can trigger additional due‑diligence questions, delays in loan sanction, or insistence that the seller first regularise the BESCOM name.ezylegal+2

* For heirs, electricity bills in the deceased’s name are weaker evidence of current possession and can hinder routine interactions with civic authorities and private entities that accept utility bills as address proof; regularising the BESCOM name in favour of a particular heir strengthens that heir’s documentary trail.bescom.trm.ieasybill+1

10.4 Security deposit and refund complications

* If the installation and deposit remain in the earlier consumer’s name, that person (or their estate) is generally the party entitled to **refund of the security deposit** upon surrender/disconnection, so a later buyer who has been paying bills without formal transfer may face significant hurdles in claiming that deposit.bescom.trm.ieasybill+2

* Property‑documentation services in Bengaluru therefore recommend that deposit transfer or refund be explicitly addressed during sale, inheritance or gift, and that BESCOM name change be completed promptly to align deposit records with the person actually bearing consumption risk.docupro+2

10.5 Inter‑se disputes and litigation risk

* BESCOM’s Official Memorandum for transfer of installation states that BESCOM will not be responsible for any future disputes between the previous and new consumer, effectively shifting responsibility for allocating liabilities and rights back to the parties and the civil courts.scribd+1

* When parties neglect BESCOM transfer and simply agree privately that the buyer will “take care of the bills”, later arrears, disconnections or supplementary demands can become contentious, creating scope for suits for indemnity or specific performance of sale covenants that could have been mitigated by timely compliance with the formal transfer procedure.digiscr.sci.gov+3
