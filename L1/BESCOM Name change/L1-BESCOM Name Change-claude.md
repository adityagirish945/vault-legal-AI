## **1\. LEGAL DEFINITION — WHAT CHANGES AND WHY IT MATTERS**

**Definition:** A BESCOM Name Change (also called "Transfer of Installation" or "Change of Name in Electricity Connection") is the administrative and legal process by which the registered consumer name, address, and associated liabilities on a BESCOM electricity connection — identified by its unique RR Number (Revenue Receipt Number) or Account ID — are formally updated in BESCOM's billing and consumer database to reflect the current legal owner or occupant of the premises.

**What specifically changes in the record:**

* The consumer's name on the RR (Revenue Receipt) register  
* The billing address and correspondence details  
* The mobile number and email associated with the account  
* Ownership of the Additional Security Deposit (ASD) / Security Deposit standing to the account  
* The Power Supply Agreement (contractual relationship with BESCOM)

**Legal and practical importance:**

(a) **Contractual liability:** The Power Supply Agreement (a contract between consumer and BESCOM governed by the KERC Conditions of Supply of Electricity, Karnataka, 2006 — notified under the Electricity Act, 2003\) runs in the registered consumer's name. The registered consumer remains legally liable for all electricity dues, defaults, and consumption charges. An unregistered occupant or new purchaser has no standing to contest dues or disconnection notices in their own right.

(b) **Address proof validity:** An electricity bill in one's name constitutes valid address proof under the KYC norms of the Government of India, accepted by banks, courts, passport offices, income tax departments, and educational institutions. A bill in the previous owner's name cannot serve as the new owner's address proof.

(c) **Security deposit ownership:** The Additional Security Deposit (ASD) held by BESCOM belongs to the registered consumer. Without a name change, the new owner cannot claim this deposit upon surrendering the connection, nor can they avoid being billed for tariff revisions that affect the ASD.

(d) **Property transaction records:** Absence of a BESCOM name change creates inconsistency in the paper trail of property ownership, potentially creating complications in future property sales, encumbrance certificates, and due diligence processes.

(e) **Tariff category compliance:** The tariff category (LT-1 Domestic, LT-2 Non-Domestic, etc.) must match the actual occupant's usage category. Name change facilitates review of correct tariff assignment.

## **2\. SCENARIOS WHERE IT IS REQUIRED**

**2.1 Property Purchase (New and Resale)** When a property with an existing electricity connection is purchased — whether from a builder/developer (primary market) or from an individual (resale/secondary market) — the connection remains registered in the seller's or builder's name until formally transferred. Both parties' interests are at stake: the new purchaser risks accumulating dues, and the previous owner continues to bear contractual liability.

**2.2 Death of the Registered Consumer** On the death of the account holder, the electricity account remains active and billed in the deceased's name. Bills cannot legally be contested or acted upon by heirs without formal transfer. Service requests (load enhancement, meter complaints, disconnection) cannot be accepted from non-registered persons. This scenario requires substitution of the legal heir as registered consumer using inheritance documents.

**2.3 Inheritance (Without a Will — Intestate)** When a property passes to legal heirs through intestate succession under the Hindu Succession Act, 1956 (for Hindus) or applicable personal law, legal heirs must first obtain a Legal Heir Certificate from the Tahsildar's office or a Succession Certificate from a civil court, then apply for BESCOM name transfer in the name of the heir who will be the primary account holder.

**2.4 Inheritance (With a Will — Testamentary)** Where a registered Will exists, the beneficiary named in the probated Will (probate from competent civil court) may apply for transfer. A probate order or letters of administration serve as the ownership document.

**2.5 Gift Deed** When a property is transferred by way of a registered gift deed, the donee (recipient) must update the BESCOM records after completing Khata transfer in their name at BBMP/relevant municipal body.

**2.6 Partition Deed** When a property is partitioned among co-owners and individual connections are assigned, each partition deed beneficiary must update the relevant electricity connection records.

**2.7 Court Decree / Partition** Decrees from family courts, civil courts, or revenue courts directing ownership of property also trigger the need for BESCOM name change.

**2.8 Tenancy (Limited Use)** Tenants generally do not require a full name change but may use an Indemnity Bond (Annexure-8 of KERC Conditions of Supply) to regularize their billing relationship. A full name change for tenants is discretionary and requires the property owner's NOC.

**2.9 Change from Builder to Flat Owner** In apartment complexes, the builder initially holds the connection in their company/firm name during construction. Upon possession, each flat owner must transfer the individual meter to their name.

## **4\. STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS FOR EACH SCENARIO**

### **4(a). After Property Purchase (from Builder or Resale)**

**Pre-requisites:**

1. Registered Sale Deed in the purchaser's name  
2. Khata Certificate transferred to purchaser's name at BBMP (or relevant local body — BDA, CMC, Gram Panchayat)  
3. Latest property tax paid receipt in the purchaser's name  
4. All BESCOM dues cleared (arrears on the account must be nil)

**Step 1 — Obtain Forms** Download Form 13 (Application for Transfer of Installation / Name Change) from the BESCOM website (https://www.bescom.co.in/bescom/main/downloadForms). The PDF contains: (i) Application (Pages 1–2), (ii) General Instructions (Page 3), (iii) NOC format from previous owner (Page 8/applicable page), (iv) Power Supply Agreement template, and (v) Indemnity Bond format.

Alternatively, collect forms from the Soujanya counter (public assistance counter) at your jurisdictional BESCOM Sub-Divisional Office.

**Step 2 — Purchase Stamp Papers** Obtain two stamp papers of Rs. 500 each (current requirement — note: older accounts reference Rs. 200, which has been revised upward). These are for the Indemnity Bond and the Power Supply Agreement. These are typically available at Karnataka State Licensed Electrical Contractors Association (KSLECA) offices located near most BESCOM offices, or from licensed stamp vendors.

**Step 3 — Prepare and Compile File**

* Fill Pages 1–2 of Form 13 (Applicant details, RR Number, reason for transfer, new consumer details)  
* Obtain NOC from previous owner / builder (on letterhead for builders; handwritten on the BESCOM template for individuals). If NOC is unavailable (previous owner untraceable), a self-declaration affidavit with ownership proof may be substituted in some offices.  
* Fill Indemnity Bond (in case of transfer of installation) — Annexure-9 of KERC Conditions of Supply — on Rs. 500 stamp paper  
* Fill Power Supply Agreement on Rs. 500 stamp paper  
* Assemble all documents in a blank cardboard file (usually available at the BESCOM office stationery counter or nearby stationery shops)  
* Affix photocopies of all ownership documents (notarized copies of Sale Deed, Khata Certificate, Tax Paid Receipt as instructed by specific SDO — practice varies by office)

**Step 4 — Visit O\&M (Operations & Maintenance) Unit / Junior Engineer** Locate the O\&M unit servicing your area (the JE's office, often separate from the SDO). Present the file. The JE will:

* Verify the meter serial number, capacity, RR number, and present reading (cross-checking against the meter photo/printout you bring)  
* Fill the "For Office Use" section (Page 2 of Form 13\)  
* Sign and stamp the field report section  
* May dispatch a lineman to physically inspect the meter at the premises

Take a clear photograph of the electricity meter showing: (i) RR Number on the meter box cover, (ii) serial number on the meter, and (iii) present meter reading. Submit a printout of this photo (taken within 2–3 days of application submission).

**Step 5 — Submit File at SDO** Return to the Sub-Divisional Office with the complete file. At the Soujanya counter, have documents verified for completeness. The assigned BESCOM official (Junior Engineer or AEE — Assistant Executive Engineer) will:

* Review all documents  
* Generate a Service Request (SR) number in the BESCOM system  
* Issue a payment challan

**Step 6 — Pay Transfer Fee** Proceed to the cash counter (typically open 10:00 AM – 1:30 PM and 2:30 PM – 3:30 PM on working days). Pay the applicable transfer fee (see Section 6 for fee structure). Collect the payment receipt. Attach the receipt to the file and return it to the official.

**Step 7 — Collect Acknowledgement** The Sakala acknowledgement number (GSC number) will be provided. Retain this for tracking.

**Step 8 — Track and Follow Up** Track status via:

* BESCOM portal using SR number  
* Sakala portal using GSC number: https://sakala.karnataka.gov.in  
* BESCOM helpline: 1912

The updated name should reflect in the next bill cycle, typically within 15–30 days from successful application.

**Online Alternative:**

* Log in to the BESCOM Online Services Portal: https://www.bescom.co.in/bescom/main/onlineServices  
* Select "Name Change" under service requests  
* Enter Account ID / RR Number and verify consumer details  
* Upload documents (PDF/JPG, max 2MB per file)  
* Pay fees online (UPI/net banking)  
* Note SR number; visit sub-division office within 7 days for physical document verification  
* Track status via portal

Note: As of 2025–2026, physical verification at the SDO remains mandatory even for online applications. The online route initiates the application but does not eliminate office visits entirely.

### **4(b). After Death of Account Holder**

**Additional Pre-requisites:**

* Death Certificate of the deceased (from municipal body / hospital)  
* Legal Heir Certificate (from Tahsildar / Revenue Department) OR Succession Certificate (from civil court for larger estates)  
* Surviving heirs' consensus or relinquishment deed if multiple heirs exist

**Step 1 — Obtain Death Certificate** Secure a certified copy of the death certificate from BBMP (for Bangalore city) or the Sub-Registrar's office.

**Step 2 — Obtain Legal Heir Certificate** Apply to the Tahsildar (Taluk office) or use Seva Sindhu (https://sevasindhu.karnataka.gov.in) to obtain a Legal Heir Certificate. Required documents: death certificate, identity proof of applicant, address proof of deceased, relationship proof (birth certificate/marriage certificate), and an affidavit listing all legal heirs. This process can take 15–30 days.

For disputes or larger estates, a Succession Certificate from the Civil Court (under the Indian Succession Act, 1925\) may be required.

**Step 3 — Obtain Khata Transfer in Legal Heir's Name** Submit mutation application to BBMP with death certificate, legal heir certificate, and property documents to update Khata to the legal heir's name.

**Step 4 — Prepare BESCOM Application** Follow the same form preparation as in Step 1–3 of Scenario 4(a), substituting:

* The "previous owner NOC" with a death certificate (the deceased cannot provide an NOC)  
* Sale deed with legal heir certificate \+ succession/partition documents showing the heir's entitlement  
* Indemnity Bond executed by the applying heir (indemnifying BESCOM against claims by other heirs)

Note: In the death scenario, NOC from the deceased is replaced by the legal heir certificate/succession certificate, and the indemnity bond becomes especially important to protect BESCOM from competing heir claims.

**Step 5 – If Multiple Heirs** All legal heirs must either: (a) execute a Relinquishment Deed in favour of the applying heir at the Sub-Registrar's office (registered on stamp paper), or (b) jointly apply, listing all heirs as co-consumers. A No-Objection from all other heirs (notarized affidavit) is typically accepted at most BESCOM offices.

**Steps 5 onwards:** Follow the same process as Steps 4–8 of Scenario 4(a).

### **6.3 Security Deposit / ASD (Additional Security Deposit)**

**Principle:** The security deposit (ASD) held by BESCOM belongs to the registered consumer and is technically non-transferable as a credit — it is linked to the RR Number, not the person.

**In practice:** When a name change occurs:

* The existing ASD on the account remains attached to the connection/RR number. It does not need to be refunded to the old owner and re-deposited by the new owner in most routine domestic transfers.  
* The NOC from the previous owner explicitly includes consent to transfer the ASD standing to the account to the new consumer.  
* If the NOC specifically requests refund of the ASD to the old owner, BESCOM may process a refund and require the new owner to pay a fresh security deposit.  
* For connections where the ASD has shortfalls (indicated on the bill as "ASD dues"), the new consumer must clear these before or during the name change process.

**Outstanding Dues Rule:** All arrears and outstanding dues must be cleared before a name change is processed. BESCOM will not transfer a connection with pending dues. The applicant undertakes (by ticking the declaration on the online form) to pay any outstanding dues that surface post-transfer for periods prior to the date of transfer.

## **9\. COMMON REJECTION AND DELAY REASONS**

### **9.1 Document-Related Rejections**

1. **Khata not transferred to applicant's name:** This is the single most common rejection reason. BESCOM requires the Khata certificate to be in the applicant's name prior to processing a name change. Attempting the process with the previous owner's Khata leads to rejection.  
2. **Outstanding dues not cleared:** Any arrears, short-assessed dues, or ASD shortfalls on the account will block processing.  
3. **NOC missing or invalid:** NOC not on BESCOM-prescribed format, not signed by authorized signatory (in case of builder — must be on company letterhead with authorized signatory), or NOC issued by a person other than the registered consumer.  
4. **Meter photo not recent or unclear:** Meter photo taken more than a few days before submission, or not clearly showing RR number, serial number, and current reading.  
5. **Stamp papers not of required denomination:** Old Rs. 200 stamp papers (pre-revision) being submitted instead of Rs. 500\.  
6. **Sale deed copies incomplete:** Missing pages, or the copy not including the schedule/annexure showing the property description; bank-certified copy not obtained where home loan exists.  
7. **Notarization disputes:** Some SDOs require notarized copies; others do not. Presenting un-notarized copies where required leads to rejection.  
8. **Address mismatch:** The address on the sale deed not matching the address on the electricity bill. Minor discrepancies (flat number format, floor designation) can cause delays requiring affidavit of identity.  
9. **Form 13 incompletely filled:** Missing meter details (make, serial number, capacity) on Page 2; the JE's section left blank.  
10. **PAN not included:** Some offices request PAN card copy for compliance reasons.

### **9.2 Process-Related Delays**

1. **Server outages on BESCOM portal:** Online applications frequently face technical issues; offline submission recommended for time-sensitive cases.  
2. **JE not available:** The JE's in-person visit or field report section cannot be filled if the JE is absent; applicants may need multiple visits.  
3. **Queue and workload:** Popular BESCOM offices in dense residential zones process hundreds of applications; cash counter queue alone can consume an hour.  
4. **Broker/intermediary approach:** Applicants who approach brokers near offices are often told additional documents are needed or that the process requires extra fees; these claims are frequently fabricated.  
5. **Multiple-owner dispute:** Where the account has multiple registered co-owners (e.g., property in joint names), all co-owners' signatures may be required on the NOC/transfer application.  
6. **Chain transfer complications:** If the connection was never transferred in the name of an intermediate owner (e.g., third owner trying to transfer from first owner's name, skipping second owner), the process becomes more complex and requires a chain of ownership documents.  
7. **ASD discrepancy:** If the ASD shown on the bill differs from what the previous owner claims to have paid, it must be reconciled before transfer.

## **10\. LEGAL AND PRACTICAL CONSEQUENCES OF NON-TRANSFER**

### **10.1 Consequences for New Owner (Property Purchaser / Heir / Donee)**

(a) **No valid address proof:** The electricity bill continues to be issued in the previous owner's/deceased's name and cannot serve as valid address proof for the new occupant for KYC, passport, bank account, or legal proceedings.

(b) **No standing to make service requests:** BESCOM service requests (load enhancement, meter complaints, tariff change, new connection tapping) can only be processed upon request by the registered consumer. The new occupant has no locus standi as an unregistered person.

(c) **Cannot claim security deposit:** The ASD held by BESCOM in the previous owner's name cannot be refunded to or credited in favour of the new occupant.

(d) **Liability for unresolved dues:** BESCOM may disconnect the supply or initiate recovery of dues billed in the previous consumer's name, directly affecting the new occupant's electricity supply even if they are not responsible for the arrears. The new occupant has little legal recourse without registered status.

(e) **Complications in future property sale:** A future purchaser conducting due diligence will note the discrepancy between property ownership (new buyer's name) and BESCOM records (previous owner's name), creating a title risk flag and potentially delaying or complicating the transaction.

(f) **Risk of wrongful disconnection:** BESCOM may issue disconnection notices to the property for dues or violations tracked under the previous consumer's profile without notifying the actual occupant.

### **10.2 Consequences for Previous Owner / Estate of Deceased**

(a) **Continued legal liability:** The previous registered consumer (or their estate, in case of death) remains the contracting party under the Power Supply Agreement and is technically liable for consumption charges and dues accruing at the property until formal transfer.

(b) **Security deposit cannot be claimed:** The previous owner cannot claim the ASD refund without surrendering the connection, and cannot surrender the connection while someone else is using the premises.

(c) **Credit score / debt recovery risk:** If BESCOM initiates legal proceedings for dues under the Electricity Act, 2003, it will proceed against the registered consumer's name, potentially affecting the previous owner or their legal heirs.

(d) **Estate administration complications:** For the estate of a deceased person, an unresolved BESCOM connection in the deceased's name is a pending liability that must be declared and resolved in estate administration.

### **10.3 Regulatory and Compliance Risk**

Prolonged use of an electricity connection in a name other than the actual occupant can, in extreme cases, attract scrutiny under Section 135 (theft of electricity) of the Electricity Act, 2003, though this is rarely invoked in standard residential transfer situations. However, in commercial or industrial connections, the risk of regulatory action is higher if the tariff category (which is consumer-specific) does not match the actual use.
