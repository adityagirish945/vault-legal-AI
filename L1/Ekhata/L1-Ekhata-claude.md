## **1\. LEGAL DEFINITION — What E-Khata Is, Its Legal Standing, and Differences from Physical Khata, A-Khata, and B-Khata**

### **1.1 Definition**

E-Khata (electronic Khata) is the digitally issued, electronically stored version of the traditional Khata certificate maintained by the Bruhat Bengaluru Mahanagara Palike (BBMP) — and, as of 15 May 2025, by the successor municipal corporations under the Greater Bengaluru Governance Act, 2024 (GBGA). The term "Khata" derives from the Urdu/Kannada word for "account." A Khata is an entry in the municipal property tax register recording the property owner's name, the property's dimensions, location, assessment number (PID — Property Identification Document), and tax liability. It is **not a title deed** and does not confer ownership; it is an assessment and tax-account document.

E-Khata is the same legal instrument delivered through the BBMP e-Aasthi (ಇ-ಆಸ್ತಿ) digital platform. It carries a digitally verifiable signature and is stored in the BBMP's centralized property database, making it tamper-resistant and remotely accessible.

### **1.2 Legal Standing**

The Karnataka High Court, in **Jayamma v. Assistant Revenue Officer**, held that while a Khata does not confer title, it is essential for utility connections, mortgages by deposit of title deeds, conveyances, and construction permissions. In **Renuka Manghnani v. State of Karnataka and Ors**, the High Court further confirmed that a Khata is essential for the "effective enjoyment of property."

The Khata received explicit **statutory recognition under the Greater Bengaluru Governance Act, 2024 (GBGA)**. Section 106(1A) of the Karnataka Municipal Corporations Act, 1976 (as amended by the Karnataka Municipalities and Certain Other Law (Amendment) Act, 2024, gazetted September 10, 2024\) now mandates that **prior approval from the relevant planning authority is required before issuing new PID or Khata** for building sites, effectively elevating the Khata into a planning-compliance instrument.

The Karnataka Real Estate Regulatory Authority (KRERA), in **Complaint No. 632/2025**, acknowledged the E-Khata as a critical document in real estate transactions, including its role in mortgage creation, enforcement, and recovery under SARFAESI Act proceedings.

### **1.3 Difference Table: Physical Khata vs. A-Khata vs. B-Khata vs. E-Khata**

| Parameter | Physical/Manual Khata | A-Khata | B-Khata | E-Khata |
| ----- | ----- | ----- | ----- | ----- |
| **Form** | Paper-based, manually maintained in BBMP registers | Physical document; same as manual Khata but for legally compliant properties | Physical document for non-compliant/unapproved properties | Digital document; electronically issued via e-Aasthi portal |
| **Legal compliance** | Depends on property status | Issued only for fully approved properties conforming to BBMP bylaws, sanctioned layouts, and KTCP Act | Issued for properties with pending approvals, unauthorized constructions, gram panchayat areas merged into BBMP, or revenue layouts lacking full municipal approval | Digital version that can represent either A-Khata or B-Khata status, but integrates records for online access |
| **Eligibility for loans** | Yes (if A-Khata) | Yes — banks and HFCs readily accept | Limited — most nationalized banks decline; some NBFCs/HFCs may lend at higher rates | Yes, if the underlying classification is A-Khata; B-Khata E-Khata has the same lending restrictions as physical B-Khata |
| **Construction permissions** | Yes (if A-Khata) | Yes | No — building plan applications require A-Khata (from July 1, 2025, E-Khata mandatory for OBPS submissions) | Yes, if A-Khata status; mandatory from July 1, 2025 for all Online Building Plan approval applications |
| **Property registration** | Required at sub-registrar | Required | Can be registered, but limited utility | Mandatory for all property registrations from October 1, 2024 |
| **Fraud risk** | High — prone to tampering, duplication, and forgery | High | High | Low — digitally signed, centrally stored, Aadhaar eKYC-linked |
| **Future eligibility** | Will be phased out | Remains valid; should be converted to E-Khata | Properties constructed after September 30, 2024 are ineligible for B-Khata (GBGA mandate) | Mandatory and the only format being issued for new Khatas |
| **Accessibility** | Physical visit to BBMP ward office | Physical visit | Physical visit | Online via bbmpeaasthi.karnataka.gov.in, 24/7 |

**Critical distinction**: A-Khata and B-Khata describe the **legal compliance status** of a property. E-Khata describes the **format/delivery mechanism**. A property can have an E-Khata that reflects either A-Khata or B-Khata status. The E-Khata system absorbs and digitizes both categories of records.

## **3\. OFFICIAL STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS**

### **3.1 Scenario A: Obtaining Final E-Khata (Draft E-Khata Already Issued)**

BBMP has pre-issued draft E-Khatas for approximately **22 lakh (2.2 million) properties** based on the existing BBMP Property Tax Register. Citizens in this category must upload supplementary information to convert draft E-Khata into final E-Khata.

**Step 1:** Visit **https://bbmpeaasthi.karnataka.gov.in/CitzLogin.aspx**. Log in using your mobile number (OTP-verified) or Aadhaar-linked credentials.

**Step 2:** Search for your property using PID number, Khata number, or owner name. Verify the pre-populated draft E-Khata details (owner name, property dimensions, ward, tax status).

**Step 3:** If details are correct, upload the required documents (see Section 4 below) in the specified format and size.

**Step 4:** Complete **Aadhaar eKYC** (electronic Know Your Customer) — mandatory for Indian residents; optional for NRIs as of October 2024\. OTP sent to Aadhaar-linked mobile number.

**Step 5:** Verify the property location using the interactive GIS-based map on the portal. Confirm GPS coordinates match property boundaries.

**Step 6:** Review all entries. Submit the application. Note the **acknowledgement/reference number** for tracking.

**Step 7:** BBMP's Revenue Department team (ARO) reviews the submitted documents. For discrepancies, disputed properties, or B-Khata conversions, a **field inspection** may be conducted.

**Step 8:** Upon approval, receive SMS/email notification. Download the final E-Khata Certificate (PDF, digitally signed) and E-Khata Extract from the portal.

**Step 9:** If corrections are needed, the application is returned with remarks. Resubmit corrected documents via the same portal.

### **3.2 Scenario B: New E-Khata Application (No Prior Khata)**

**Step 1:** Visit **https://bbmpeaasthi.karnataka.gov.in/** or **https://sevasindhu.karnataka.gov.in**.

**Step 2:** Register as a new user with email ID, mobile number, and password. Verify via OTP.

**Step 3:** Navigate to: Departments & Services → Revenue Department / BBMP → "Application for New E-Khata."

**Step 4:** Enter property details: property location, dimensions (in sq. ft. / sq. m.), survey number, ward number, owner name, and PID (if available).

**Step 5:** Upload all required documents (see Section 4).

**Step 6:** Complete Aadhaar eKYC.

**Step 7:** Verify property location on the interactive GIS map.

**Step 8:** Pay the applicable fee online (UPI, debit/credit card, net banking).

**Step 9:** Submit and record the acknowledgement number.

**Step 10:** BBMP ARO verifies documents and tax status; RO provides final approval. Site inspection possible.

**Step 11:** Download approved E-Khata Certificate and Extract from portal.

### **3.3 Scenario C: Manual Khata to E-Khata Conversion**

**WARNING:** BBMP has issued an official warning — property owners who already hold a manual Khata must **NOT apply for a new Khata**. Applying for a new Khata when one already exists may be treated as an attempt to obtain duplicate records and can result in **criminal action**. Instead, the conversion process is:

**Step 1:** Log into e-Aasthi portal. Search your existing Khata number.

**Step 2:** Select "Conversion of Manual Khata to E-Khata."

**Step 3:** Upload existing manual Khata certificate (physical scan), plus supporting documents.

**Step 4:** Pay ₹40 service charge.

**Step 5:** Submit and track. Existing manual Khata records are digitized and migrated.

### **3.4 Scenario D: B-Khata to A-Khata Conversion (Regularization)**

Per BBMP mandate effective September 30, 2024, and the GBGA 2024:

**Step 1:** Confirm property registered prior to September 30, 2024 (post this date, no new B-Khata eligible).

**Step 2:** Obtain plan approval/conversion certificate under Karnataka Land Revenue Act or KTCP Act.

**Step 3:** Pay applicable regularization charges/penalties (quantum per BBMP circular; detailed notifications awaited from BBMP/Urban Development Department as of early 2026).

**Step 4:** Apply through e-Aasthi portal under "B-Khata to A-Khata Regularization" module.

**Step 5:** Field verification by ARO.

**Step 6:** On approval, A-Khata E-Khata issued.

### **3.5 Downloading E-Khata (Post-Approval)**

* Via e-Aasthi portal: Login → View Status of Application → Issue Document → Download PDF.  
* Via Sevasindhu: Track Application Status → Enter details → Download E-Khata Certificate.  
* Via Sakala portal: Enter PID/application number → E-Khata Download section.

## **8\. COMMON REJECTION REASONS**

The following grounds for rejection have been officially acknowledged by BBMP and documented through citizen complaints and BBMP circulars:

### **8.1 Document-Related Rejections**

1. **Incomplete documents:** Missing sale deed, EC, or tax receipts.  
2. **Outdated Encumbrance Certificate:** EC older than 12 months or not covering the full chain of ownership.  
3. **Document mismatch:** Name, survey number, extent, or boundaries differ between sale deed and tax records.  
4. **Poor-quality scans:** Blurry, illegible, or incorrectly cropped document uploads.  
5. **Wrong file format:** Non-PDF/JPEG files or files exceeding portal size limits.  
6. **Missing co-owner consent:** Joint ownership applications without all co-owner affidavits or ID proofs.

### **8.2 Property Status–Related Rejections**

7. **Unpaid property tax dues:** Any outstanding dues automatically blocks E-Khata issuance.  
8. **Property outside BBMP jurisdiction:** E-Khata is only for BBMP/GBGA-governed properties; properties under BDA, BMRDA, gram panchayat (not merged), or other local bodies require separate processes.  
9. **Unconverted revenue land:** Properties on revenue land without a DC Conversion Order are ineligible for A-Khata E-Khata.  
10. **No plan approval:** Constructed buildings without sanctioned building plans are ineligible.  
11. **Ongoing litigation or encumbrances:** Active court cases, mortgages, or attachment orders recorded in the EC prevent E-Khata issuance.  
12. **Property marked as government land:** Applications flagged for "Government Land Verification" are held pending physical inspection.  
13. **Properties constructed post–September 30, 2024 attempting B-Khata:** Legally barred under GBGA 2024\.

### **8.3 Data Entry and System Errors**

14. **Incorrect property details:** Wrong PID, survey number, site size, or ward number.  
15. **Outdated PID/layout map:** Mismatch between applicant-submitted PID and BBMP database records.  
16. **Duplicate Khata attempt:** Applying for new Khata while manual Khata exists — triggers criminal liability warning.  
17. **Kaveri portal integration failure:** Applications dependent on sub-registrar's Kaveri 2.0 data face system-level rejections if property details are not synced.  
18. **Records not found in database:** Properties in areas where physical records were reportedly lost or not digitized (documented community complaints, early 2025).

### **8.4 Remedies**

* Log into portal → check rejection reason in application status.  
* Correct documents/details and resubmit.  
* Contact ARO/RO at BBMP ward office for manual intervention.  
* File grievance on **BBMP Sahaaya Portal** (Sahaaya 2.0) or call **9480683695**.  
* Contact: **e-khata@bbmp.gov.in**.

## **10\. WHEN E-KHATA IS MANDATORY — Non-Negotiable Scenarios**

### **10.1 Property Registration (Sale/Purchase)**

**Mandatory from October 1, 2024\.** The Sub-Registrar's office (Kaveri 2.0 system) requires E-Khata for all property registrations within BBMP/GBGA limits. No property sale deed can be registered without the seller presenting a valid E-Khata. This applies to:

* Absolute sale  
* Gift deed  
* Settlement deed  
* Partition deed (if BBMP property)  
* Release deed

### **10.2 Home Loans and Mortgages**

E-Khata is a prerequisite for:

* **Mortgage creation** — banks and Housing Finance Companies (HFCs) require E-Khata to verify property identity, ownership, and municipal compliance during loan sanction.  
* **Mortgages by deposit of title deeds (equitable mortgage)** — Karnataka High Court and KRERA have confirmed Khata is essential for this form of security.  
* **SARFAESI enforcement** — Absence of E-Khata during auction/recovery proceedings reduces collateral value, complicates title, and invites third-party claims.  
* **All nationalized banks, private banks, and major HFCs** require E-Khata as part of the legal/technical due diligence checklist for property loans.

### **10.3 Building Plan Approvals and Construction**

**Mandatory from July 1, 2025:**

* All **Online Building Plan Approval System (OBPS)** submissions require E-Khata.  
* All applications under the **Nambike Nakshe scheme** (self-certification for smaller constructions) require E-Khata.  
* Construction Commencement Certificate, Completion Certificate, and Occupancy Certificate applications will require the property to have a valid E-Khata.

### **10.4 Property Tax Assessment and Payment**

* Property owners are expected to use E-Khata as the basis for property tax self-assessment and payment via the BBMP tax portal.  
* E-Khata enables online tax payment without requiring physical BBMP visits.

### **10.5 Utility Connections**

Per Karnataka High Court (Jayamma v. ARO), Khata is essential for:

* BESCOM electricity connection  
* BWSSB water/sewage connection  
* BBMP trade/building licenses  
* Any municipal service requiring proof of property assessment

### **10.6 Khata Transfer (Post-Sale)**

After any property registration, the buyer must obtain E-Khata in their name (Khata transfer/mutation). Without this, the buyer cannot access loans, utilities, or approvals in their own name.

### **10.7 B-Khata to A-Khata Conversion**

Required for:

* Accessing bank loans on B-Khata properties  
* Obtaining building plan sanctions  
* Re-selling at full market value

### **10.8 Consequences of Non-Compliance**

* Property cannot be legally sold or registered.  
* Bank loans will be denied or delayed.  
* Building plan applications (OBPS, Nambike Nakshe) will be rejected.  
* Utility connections (BESCOM, BWSSB) may be withheld.  
* Property is not independently recognised in civic records.  
* Auction/recovery values under SARFAESI are discounted.  
* Municipal services, trade licenses, and occupancy certificates are inaccessible.
