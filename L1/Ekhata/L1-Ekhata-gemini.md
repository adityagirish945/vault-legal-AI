# **The Jurisprudential and Administrative Framework of E-Khata in Bengaluru: A Definitive Reference for Legal Knowledge Bases**

The rapid urbanization of Bengaluru has necessitated a sophisticated digital evolution of municipal land record management. Central to this evolution is the E-Khata system, an initiative spearheaded by the Bruhat Bengaluru Mahanagara Palike (BBMP) and integrated under the broader Greater Bengaluru Authority (GBA) framework.1 Historically, the "Khata" has served as a critical administrative account, identifying the person primarily responsible for property tax payments and recording essential property characteristics such as size, location, and assessment value.5 The transition to E-Khata addresses long-standing vulnerabilities in manual record-keeping, including tampering, forgery, and the persistence of non-compliant properties recorded in the secondary "B Register".1

This document provides a comprehensive analysis of the E-Khata framework, detailing its legal standing, procedural nuances, and mandatory applications as of mid-2025.

## **1\. Legal Definition and Hierarchical Standing**

In the context of Karnataka municipal law, a "Khata" is essentially an "account".5 It represents a property's registration within the municipal assessment rolls for taxation and administrative purposes.1 Legal practitioners must distinguish between the Khata and the Title Deed; while a title deed constitutes proof of ownership, the Khata is an evidentiary record of the tax assessment and the identity of the current assessee.5

### **1.1 The Nature of E-Khata**

E-Khata (Electronic Khata) is the digitized version of the traditional Khata certificate and extract, issued through the centralized Urban Property Management System (UPMS), commonly known as e-Aasthi.1 It is critical to understand that E-Khata is not a new legal category but a modern *format* for existing property records.11 An E-Khata record can be generated for both compliant and non-compliant properties, maintaining their underlying legal status as A-Khata or B-Khata.1

### **1.2 Distinctions from Physical and Traditional Khatas**

The administrative landscape classifies property records into three distinct formats and two primary legal statuses:

| Feature | Physical Khata | E-Khata (e-Aasthi) |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Storage Medium** | Handwritten registers in zonal offices 9 | Centralized electronic database/Blockchain 9 |
| **Security Protocol** | Prone to tampering and misplacement 1 | Secure, Aadhaar-verified, and tamper-proof 2 |
| **Accessibility** | Requires physical presence and manual retrieval 20 | 24/7 online access for verification and download 11 |
| **Integration** | Isolated from other departments 21 | Integrated with Registration (Kaveri) and Utilities (BESCOM) 21 |

### **1.3 The A-Khata and B-Khata Dichotomy**

The distinction between A and B Khatas remains the most significant legal hurdle for property owners in Bengaluru.

* **A-Khata:** This status is reserved for properties that fully comply with building bylaws, zoning regulations, and town planning norms.11 An A-Khata is issued to properties in approved layouts that have cleared all betterment charges and obtained Occupancy Certificates (OC).5 It provides full legal validity for all municipal approvals and bank financing.5  
* **B-Khata:** Following the Karnataka High Court's intervention in 2014, the BBMP maintains a separate register for properties with deviations or those built on unapproved revenue land.7 While B-Khata allows the BBMP to collect tax, it is technically an irregular record.17 B-Khata properties are generally ineligible for building plan approvals, official transfers at sub-registrar offices, or standard mortgages from nationalized banks.13

## **2\. Governing Body and Digital Infrastructure**

The governance of Bengaluru's property records is centralized under the **Bruhat Bengaluru Mahanagara Palike (BBMP)**, with direct oversight from the **Urban Development Department (UDD)** of the Government of Karnataka.1 The transition is part of the state's e-governance mandate to unify urban and rural land records through digital platforms.9

### **2.1 Departmental Responsibility**

Within the BBMP, the **Revenue Department**, led by the Special Commissioner (Revenue), is responsible for the verification, issuance, and mutation of E-Khatas.3 Following the 2024 administrative reforms, the **Greater Bengaluru Authority (GBA)** serves as the apex planning and administrative body for the five city corporations comprising the metropolitan area.4

### **2.2 Official Portal Navigation and URL Structure**

* **Principal Portal (e-Aasthi):** [https://bbmpeaasthi.karnataka.gov.in](https://bbmpeaasthi.karnataka.gov.in).1 This is the primary interface for searching property details, viewing draft Khatas, and completing the Aadhaar-based finalization process.  
* **Property Tax Interface:** [https://bbmptax.karnataka.gov.in/](https://bbmptax.karnataka.gov.in/).3 Essential for current tax compliance and for obtaining the 10-digit SAS Property Tax Application Number, which is a mandatory data point for E-Khata linkage.  
* **State-Wide Service Portal:** [https://sevasindhu.karnataka.gov.in/](https://sevasindhu.karnataka.gov.in/).1 Often used for "New Khata Registration" or "B-Khata to A-Khata Conversion" requests that require deeper administrative routing.  
* **Rural Counterpart:** [https://eswathu.karnataka.gov.in](https://eswathu.karnataka.gov.in).10 Manages property records (Form 9 and 11\) for properties in Bengaluru Rural districts or areas within the city limits that remain under Gram Panchayat jurisdiction.

## **3\. Official Step-by-Step Process for E-Khata Issuance**

The E-Khata system is designed to be faceless, contactless, and paperless.41 The process involves two distinct phases: the retrieval of a "Draft E-Khata" and the submission of supplementary data to achieve "Final E-Khata" status.9

### **3.1 Initial Identification and Draft Access**

1. **System Entry:** The property owner logs into the e-Aasthi portal using a mobile number.23 Authentication is strictly via One-Time Password (OTP) sent to the registered mobile device.23  
2. **Property Search Parameters:** Navigate to the "Get E-Khata" section.21 The system requires the user to select the appropriate Zone and Ward Number.35 This information is found on the top corner of the property tax receipt.35  
3. **Search Logic:** Properties can be located by:  
   * **Property Identification Number (PID):** The standard 10-digit tax identifier.14  
   * **Owner Name:** Requires exact spelling match with manual register data. (Transliteration variations often require searching multiple spellings).35  
   * **Assessment Number/Book Number:** References the physical ledger where the property was originally recorded.35  
4. **Draft Download:** Once the property is found, a "Draft E-Khata" is displayed.9 This draft is based on current BBMP tax records and is unverified.9

### **3.2 Supplementary Data Submission (E-Khata Finalization)**

To move from a draft to a final, E-signed certificate, the owner must provide additional authenticated data:

1. **Aadhaar eKYC:** The owner must perform a biometric or OTP-based Aadhaar validation to link their digital identity to the property record.9  
2. **Registered Deed Entry:** For properties registered after April 1, 2004, the system can auto-fetch data via the registration number from the Kaveri portal.19 For older deeds, a full high-resolution scan must be uploaded.35  
3. **Property Geotagging:** Owners must use the portal's integrated map tool to verify the property's exact location.9 This records GPS coordinates, which are now mandatory for the E-Khata.9  
4. **Property Photography:** A recent photograph of the property must be uploaded to confirm the current state of construction and occupancy.9  
5. **Utility and Tax Linkage:** Provide the 10-digit BESCOM account number and the latest SAS Property Tax Application Number to ensure cross-database consistency.9

### **3.3 Administrative Review and Objections**

1. **The Objection Window:** Upon the publication of the final draft information, a 7-day period is provided for any stakeholder to file an online objection against the issuance of the Final E-Khata.22  
2. **Verification Engine:** If all data points match perfectly across the Kaveri (Registration), BESCOM (Electricity), and SAS (Tax) databases, the Final E-Khata may be issued instantly without manual intervention.9  
3. **Assistant Revenue Officer (ARO) Routing:** Any discrepancy—even minor spelling variations or initial mismatches—routes the application to the jurisdictional ARO for manual scrutiny and signature.37

## **4\. Exhaustive Document Checklist and Specifications**

Property documentation in Bengaluru is categorized based on the source of allotment and the current nature of the asset (e.g., land vs. constructed flat).

### **4.1 Categorical Document Requirements**

| Category | Required Documents |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Common (All Types)** | Registered Title Deed, Identity Proof (Aadhaar/PAN), Current Year Tax Receipt, Property Photo.24 |
| **BDA Allotted Sites** | Allotment Letter, Possession Certificate, Lease-cum-Sale Deed.1 |
| **Apartments/Constructed Units** | Sanctioned Building Plan, Occupancy Certificate (OC), Sale Deed with UDS details.14 |
| **Private Layouts** | Layout Plan Approval Order, Release Order from BDA/BMRDA, Sale Deed.1 |
| **Inherited Property** | Death Certificate, family tree, notarized NOC from all heirs, Will (if any).1 |

### **4.2 Technical and Submission Specifications**

Legal compliance requires strict adherence to digital upload standards to avoid technical rejections:

* **Resolution and Clarity:** All documents must be scanned at a minimum of 300 DPI resolution.22 Unclear or blurred scans that hinder the reading of survey numbers or schedules are common grounds for hold-status.22  
* **File Constraints:** The system accepts PDF or JPEG/JPG formats.21 Individual file sizes are capped at 5 MB.12 Total application payloads exceeding this may require the use of PDF compression tools.2  
* **Encumbrance Certificate (EC):** For a simple E-Khata verification, the EC has been made optional in 2025 to assist senior citizens.9 However, for a sale, transfer, or mortgage, a current EC (Form 15\) is non-negotiable.9 The EC must cover at least 13 to 15 years, or from the date of the previous registered deed to the current date.1

## **5\. Fee Structure and Digital Payment Modules**

The E-Khata system aims to eliminate the opaque pricing historically associated with manual ward offices. Fees are now calculated through standardized schedules.21

### **5.1 Direct Administrative Fees**

* **Application Processing:** Initial online submission via the e-Aasthi portal is essentially free.1  
* **Service Charge:** A mandatory charge of ₹40 \- ₹45 is levied for applications routed through Seva Sindhu or Sakala.1  
* **E-Khata Issuance/Download:** A standard online payment of **₹125** is required to generate the final verified certificate.9  
* **Janasevaka/Doorstep Assistance:** ₹115 for the home-delivery module, plus ₹5 per scanned page if utilizing center support.9

### **5.2 Ownership Transfer and Mutation Charges**

When a property changes hands due to sale or gift, the following fees apply during the mutation process:

* **Transfer Fee:** Calculated as **2% of the stamp duty** paid during property registration.44 Note: Legal professionals should verify the current zonal circular as some reports indicate a shift toward 2% of the total sale deed value (A-Khata) or registration value.2  
* **Improvement Charges (Betterment Fee):** For the first Khata issuance or regularization:  
  * **Core BBMP Wards:** ₹200 per square meter.44  
  * **Newly Integrated Zones:** ₹250 per square meter.44  
* **B-Khata to A-Khata Conversion Fee:** For properties eligible under the 2025 regularization scheme, the fee is set at **5% of the prevailing guidance value**.61

### **5.3 Payment Gateways**

All payments must be completed online through the e-Aasthi portal's secure dashboard.58

* **Digital Channels:** RuPay/Visa/Master Debit and Credit Cards, Net Banking (all major banks), and UPI.39  
* **Challan Module:** For large payments, the system allows the generation of a bank challan for over-the-counter deposit at HDFC or ICICI branches.21

## **6\. Processing Timelines: Official SLAs vs. Empirical Realities**

The primary objective of the E-Khata transition is the reduction of processing latency from months to days.21

### **6.1 Official Sakala Timelines (SLAs)**

Under the Karnataka Guarantee of Services to Citizens Act (Sakala), the following timelines are statutory 12:

* **Verified E-Khata Finalization:** Directed for disposal within **7 working days**.79  
* **Simple Khata Transfer/Mutation:** Statutory limit of **30 working days**.12  
* **New Property Registration:** Up to **60 working days** for initial entry into the property rolls.12

### **6.2 The Real-World Processing Landscape in 2025**

| Scenario | Average Processing Time |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Perfect Data Match** | Many simple requests are resolved within **48 hours** to **3 days**.15 |
| **Straightforward Application** | issued within **15 days** if all documents are clear and accurate on the first attempt.1 |
| **Name/Spelling Discrepancy** | Typically extends to **15-30 working days** due to mandatory manual ARO review.33 |
| **High Backlog Zones** | Wards in Mahadevapura or South Zones may see **30-45 day** delays (citywide pendency reported at 1.4 lakh files in mid-2025).65 |

## **7\. Legal Provisions and Regulatory Framework**

The digital Khata transition is anchored in the Karnataka Municipal Corporations Act and bolstered by recent high-profile High Court rulings.72

### **7.1 Statutory References in the KMC Act, 1976**

* **Section 114 (Notice of Transfer):** Obligates the seller and buyer to notify the Commissioner of ownership changes within 90 days of execution of the registered deed.26 Failure to comply hinders future E-Khata mutation.86  
* **Section 108 (Property Tax Description):** Defines the Unit Area Value (UAV) system, which formulates the tax basis displayed in the E-Khata.85  
* **Section 112B (Property Tax Register):** Mandates the municipal preparation of the property register. This register is the legal bedrock upon which the e-Aasthi digital database is constructed.85  
* **Section 112C (Penalty on Unlawful Building):** Empowers the BBMP to collect tax on irregular properties (B-Khata), distinguishing this from legal approval.85

### **7.2 Recent Legislative and Judicial Context (2024-2025)**

* **Greater Bengaluru Governance (GBG) Act, 2024:** Formally recognized the digital E-Khata as a statutory requirement and abolished the creation of new B-Khatas for any construction occurring after September 30, 2024\.13  
* **Karnataka Municipalities Taxation (Amendment) Rules, 2025:** Introduced stricter scrutiny of property tax returns. Under Rule 10B, appeals against demand notices issued through E-Khata now require a 50% deposit of the contested tax amount.90  
* **Renuka Maghnani v. State of Karnataka (WP 4821/2023):** The High Court ruled that E-Khata must be issued *immediately* upon application if a manual Khata has already been issued, emphasizing that the digital shift should not create administrative gridlock.84  
* **U Mamatha v. State of Karnataka (WP 21648/2024):** Held that the BBMP cannot be compelled to issue E-Khata for plots lacking layout plan approval, reinforcing the legal link between digital records and planning compliance.92

## **8\. Common Grounds for Application Rejection**

The transition to an automated matching system has created a high failure rate for applications that do not possess exact data hygiene.37

### **8.1 Data Integration Failures**

1. **Name Transliteration and Initial Mismatches:** The software uses "fuzzy logic" (70-80% match tolerance).45 However, the common South Indian practice of using initials on one document (e.g., V. Kumar) and full names on another (e.g., Vijay Kumar) frequently triggers a mismatch flag.45  
2. **Transliteration Inconsistency:** Names written in Kannada script may convert to different English spellings (e.g., Sridevi vs. Shreedevi), leading to automated rejection until manual ARO correction.45  
3. **Property Detail Variances:** Plots built on divided land (bifurcation) often face rejection if separate sale deeds exist but the BBMP has not approved the formal Khata bifurcation.64

### **8.2 Compliance and Technical Barriers**

1. **Tax Arrears:** Any unpaid property tax—including revised demand notices or show-cause penalties—will block the generation of the Final E-Khata.58  
2. **PID Duplication/Error:** Clerical errors in the digitization of manual registers have resulted in incorrect PIDs that reference non-existent properties. Applicants often find their property "un-locatable" despite paying tax for years.64  
3. **Geotagging Divergence:** If the owner pins a location that deviates more than 10 meters from the survey map linked to the e-Aasthi backend, the application is held for site inspection.47  
4. **Impersonation and Forgery:** In late 2024, the "blanket rejection" option for AROs was limited solely to cases of suspected impersonation to reduce opportunities for administrative harassment.37

## **9\. Recent Administrative Changes (2023–2025)**

The policy environment governing Bengaluru real estate has been extremely dynamic between 2023 and 2025, focused primarily on enforcing digital compliance.

### **9.1 The October 2024 Mandate**

Starting **October 1, 2024**, the Karnataka Government made E-Khata mandatory for all property registrations citywide.10 This effectively paralyzed registration for any property without a verified digital ID, leading to a reported 21-60% dip in registration volume during the stabilization phase.10

### **9.2 Building Plan Integration (EoDB-OBPS)**

Effective **July 1, 2025**, the BBMP implemented a mandatory integration between the Town Planning department's Online Building Permission System (OBPS) and the e-Aasthi database.34 Builders can no longer apply for sanctions manually; the system automatically validates the property's E-Khata/EPID to check tax compliance and ownership status before accepting building drawings.22

### **9.3 Regularization Schemes and Drives**

* **B-Khata Upgrade (November 2025):** The state cabinet approved a one-time 100-day drive to convert B-Khatas issued before September 30, 2024, into A-Khatas.13 This required properties to first onboarding to E-Khata as a primary step.61  
* **Doorstep Draft Delivery:** In July 2025, the BBMP launched a constituency-level drive to hand-deliver E-Khata drafts to residents to accelerate the finalization process.15

## **10\. Scenarios Where E-Khata is Non-Negotiable**

For any property stakeholder in Bengaluru, the E-Khata is now the primary gateway for essential financial and administrative activities.

### **10.1 Real Estate Transactions**

* **Sub-Registrar Registration:** No sale deed, mortgage, or gift deed can be registered without a verified E-Khata extract.9 This is enforced to eliminate duplicate registrations of the same site.10  
* **Title Verification:** Commercial and high-value residential buyers now treat the absence of a verified Final E-Khata as a major title defect, often demanding price reductions of 2-5% for non-digitized assets.33

### **10.2 Financial Services**

* **Home Loan Approval:** Most nationalized and leading private banks (SBI, HDFC, ICICI) now perform real-time verification of property records on the e-Aasthi portal.2 Loan disbursement is routinely stalled for 30-60 days if the property record is missing or incorrect in the digital system.80  
* **Collateral and De-mortgaging:** The unique EPID number generated by the E-Khata is now a mandatory field for de-mortgaging property records after loan closure.55

### **10.3 Construction and Essential Services**

* **Plan Sanction:** Under the "Nambike Nakshe" (Trust and Verify) fast-track approval scheme, an E-Khata is a foundational requirement.34  
* **Occupancy Certificate (OC):** For developers, the final OC issuance is now linked to the digital reconciliation of individual units within the E-Khata system.20  
* **Utility Infrastructure:** Permanent permanent water (BWSSB) and electricity (BESCOM) connections for new buildings are predicated on the submission of a valid E-Khata.6

The E-Khata system, while currently undergoing stabilization, represents a profound shift toward transparency and administrative accountability in Bengaluru's property market. By creating a "Single Source of Truth," it safeguards property rights and facilitates a more efficient real estate ecosystem for millions of stakeholders.33 Owners are advised to complete their digital onboarding promptly to avoid operational hurdles in future transactions.
