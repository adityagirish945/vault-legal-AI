## 1. LEGAL DEFINITION — What Property Due Diligence Means, What It Covers, and Its Legal Importance

### 1.1 Definition

Property due diligence (also termed "title investigation," "property verification," or "legal scrutiny") is the systematic, pre-transaction investigation of all legal, factual, regulatory, financial, and physical aspects of an immovable property to determine whether the seller has a clear, marketable, and unencumbered title capable of lawful transfer, and whether the property is free from legal or regulatory defects that could affect the purchaser's rights.

In the Karnataka context, due diligence is not merely a procedural formality; it is a legally mandated or professionally indispensable exercise demanded by: (a) financial institutions before sanctioning home loans; (b) advocates advising buyers; (c) RERA regulations governing builder-buyer relationships; and (d) courts that have consistently held that a buyer dealing negligently with title verification does so at their own peril (the doctrine of constructive notice under Section 3 of the Transfer of Property Act, 1882 applies).

### 1.2 Scope of Property Due Diligence

A thorough due diligence exercise in Karnataka covers:

**Title Verification**: Examination of the chain of ownership documents going back a minimum of 30 years (recommended 30–60 years for complex cases). This includes the root of title, all intermediate conveyances, devolutions by inheritance, partition deeds, gift deeds, and any court decrees affecting title.

**Encumbrance Search**: Verification through the Encumbrance Certificate (Form 15 and Form 16) issued by the Sub-Registrar's Office (SRO) to confirm whether the property is free of mortgages, charges, attachments, court injunctions, or prior sale agreements registered against it.

**Revenue Records Verification**: Examination of RTC (Record of Rights, Tenancy and Crops, also called Pahani), mutation register, and Khata to confirm lawful possession, proper mutation, land classification, and absence of government or dispute entries.

**Regulatory Compliance Verification**: Confirmation that approvals from BBMP/BDA/BMRDA/relevant authority were obtained for layouts and building plans, that the Occupancy Certificate (OC) or Completion Certificate (CC) was issued, and that RERA registration is valid (for new projects).

**Litigation Search**: Search of records at the City Civil Court, Karnataka High Court, District Court, and Revenue Tribunals to identify pending or disposed suits/appeals involving the property or the seller.

**Physical Verification**: Matching of survey plan, boundaries, measurements, and actual possession with registered documents; checking that the physical property corresponds to what is described in title deeds.

**Statutory Dues Verification**: Confirmation that property tax, municipal dues, utility bills (BWSSB, BESCOM), and other government dues are paid and up to date.

**FEMA/NRI Compliance**: Where the seller is a non-resident Indian or foreign citizen, verification that acquisition and proposed sale comply with FEMA 1999 and RBI regulations.

**Agricultural Land Conversion**: Where land was previously agricultural, verification that the DC Conversion Order has been obtained under Section 95 of the Karnataka Land Revenue Act, 1964, and that the land has been properly converted for residential/commercial use.

### 1.3 Legal Importance

**Constructive Notice (Section 3, Transfer of Property Act, 1882)**: A person is deemed to have constructive notice of all facts that would have been known had they made proper inquiries. A buyer who skips due diligence cannot claim ignorance of a prior encumbrance or title defect that was registerable or discoverable.

**Doctrine of Lis Pendens (Section 52, Transfer of Property Act, 1882)**: A transfer of property during a pending litigation is void against the other party, if the transferee had actual or constructive notice. Litigation searches are therefore critical.

**Bona Fide Purchaser for Value (Section 41, Transfer of Property Act, 1882)**: A purchaser from an ostensible owner can be protected only if they acted in good faith and took reasonable care to ascertain the seller's title — which requires due diligence.

**Karnataka Registration Act, 1908 (applicable as per central Act)**: Registration of documents is compulsory for conveyances of immovable property above ₹100 in value (Section 17). Only a properly registered title deed in the purchaser's name provides legally recognised ownership.

**Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016 (RERA)**: Sections 3, 4, 11, and 18 mandate builder registration, disclosure of title documents, land title details, approvals, and timelines. Due diligence must include verification of RERA registration for all ongoing new projects.

**FEMA, 1999 and RBI Master Circular**: Transfer of immovable property in India by or to a person resident outside India is restricted. Failure to verify compliance exposes both parties to regulatory penalties.

## 2. WHEN DUE DILIGENCE IS REQUIRED

### 2.1 Resale Property Purchase

Due diligence is essential before purchasing any secondary-market (resale) property. The investigator must:

- Examine the seller's registered title deed (Sale Deed) and ensure it is duly stamped, registered, and the seller's name appears in the SRO records.
- Trace the full chain of title for a minimum of 30 years (Encumbrance Certificate + certified copies of all prior deeds).
- Verify mutation in revenue records (Khata transfer in the case of BBMP jurisdiction, or mutation in RTC for rural/agricultural land).
- Confirm property tax paid to date, with receipts in the seller's name.
- Check for pending court cases involving the property.
- In the case of apartments/flats, examine the Undivided Share (UDS) in the land, the Building Plan approval, and the Occupancy Certificate.
- For layouts/plots, verify BDA/BBMP/BMRDA approval of the layout.

### 2.2 New Apartment from Builder / Under-Construction Project

Specific additional checks apply:

- Verify RERA registration on https://rera.karnataka.gov.in/ and review all disclosed documents on the RERA portal, including land title documents, approved plans, and construction schedule.
- Verify builder's title to the land (freehold ownership or valid Joint Development Agreement with landowner, including General Power of Attorney registered under Section 17 of the Registration Act).
- Check building plan approval from BBMP, BDA, or BMRDA as applicable.
- Confirm DC Conversion order if land was agricultural.
- Verify BESCOM, BWSSB NOC or sanctions for utilities.
- Review the draft Sale Agreement to check if it is RERA-compliant (Section 13, RERA Act — no more than 10% can be collected before Sale Agreement).
- Verify Completion Certificate / Occupancy Certificate upon completion before final payment and registration.
- Confirm 70% of amounts collected are deposited in the designated RERA bank account (Section 4(2)(l)(D), RERA Act).

### 2.3 Agricultural Land Conversion

Karnataka restricts non-agriculturists from purchasing agricultural land (Section 79A/79B, Karnataka Land Reforms Act, 1961 — though Section 79A was partially diluted in 2020). Due diligence must:

- Examine the RTC to confirm land classification (agricultural, kharab, government, etc.).
- Check whether the land is under the Karnataka Land Reforms Act (occupancy rights, tenant protections).
- Verify DC Conversion Order under Section 95, Karnataka Land Revenue Act, 1964, for conversion from agricultural to residential/non-agricultural use.
- Examine mutation entries post-conversion.
- Confirm NA (Non-Agricultural) betterment charges have been paid.
- Verify there is no restriction under Section 79A/79B if buyer is a non-agriculturist.

### 2.4 NRI-Owned Property

- Verify the NRI seller's citizenship/residency status.
- Check that the original acquisition of the property by the NRI complied with FEMA 1999 and the applicable RBI general permissions or specific approvals.
- Confirm that any Power of Attorney used by the NRI is properly registered, attested by the Indian Consulate/Embassy, and adjudicated in India.
- Verify FEMA compliance for repatriation of sale proceeds.
- Ensure TDS compliance under Section 195, Income Tax Act, 1961 (typically 20–22.88% of sale consideration for NRI sellers, unless lower withholding certificate is obtained).
- Confirm no FEMA violation or Enforcement Directorate proceedings against the property or the NRI.

### 2.5 Inherited Property

- Verify the chain of devolution: legal heir certificate, succession certificate (for movable property but often used as reference), probated will (if applicable), partition deed, or family settlement deed.
- Check that all legal heirs have executed the Sale Deed or provided consent/NOC, especially in the case of ancestral property.
- Confirm mutation of revenue records in the names of legal heirs following the original owner's death.
- Examine RTC and Encumbrance Certificate post-mutation.
- Verify there are no probate disputes, will challenges, or inheritance litigation pending.
- In case of Hindu Undivided Family (HUF) property: confirm the Karta's authority and that all adult coparceners have joined or consented to the sale.

### 2.6 Auctioned Property (Bank / Court / DRT)

- Obtain and scrutinize the auction sale notice, terms and conditions, and the authority under which the auction was conducted (SARFAESI Act, 2002; DRT Rules; Civil Court Decree; Revenue Recovery).
- Verify the underlying title of the mortgagor/borrower before auction.
- Confirm the Encumbrance Certificate to identify all charges, including those of the auctioning lender and any subsequent/prior lenders.
- Check for pending legal challenges to the auction, including any stay orders or writ petitions before the High Court or DRT.
- In SARFAESI auctions, verify the Possession Notice under Rule 8(1) and Auction Notice under Rule 9 of the Security Interest (Enforcement) Rules, 2002 were properly served and published.
- Upon purchase, ensure a valid Sale Certificate is issued and registered in the Sub-Registrar's Office and mutation is obtained; courts have held that a SARFAESI Sale Certificate must be registered under Section 17(1A) of the Registration Act.
- Be aware: prior encumbrances not extinguished by the auction proceed against the property and the new buyer.

## 3. GOVERNING BODIES AND DATA SOURCES

### 3.1 Inspector General of Registration, Karnataka (IGR Karnataka) / Kaveri Online Services

**Role**: The apex authority for stamp duty, registration of property documents, and maintenance of Sub-Registrar's Office (SRO) records across Karnataka.

**Website**: https://igr.karnataka.gov.in/ | Kaveri Online Services: https://kaverionline.karnataka.gov.in/

**What it reveals in due diligence**:
- All registered documents involving the property (sale deeds, gift deeds, mortgage deeds, release deeds, partition deeds, settlement deeds, power of attorney) going back to the digitization date (post April 2004 online; pre-2004 records available at the SRO in physical form).
- Encumbrance Certificate (Form 15: Encumbrances found; Form 16: Nil Encumbrance) showing all registered transactions over the requested period.
- Certified copies of any registered document by providing the document number and registration year.
- Property market/guideline values for stamp duty assessment purposes.
- Current status of any pending/registered document.

**Key service**: The Encumbrance Certificate is the single most important document in Karnataka property due diligence. It is available online at: https://igr.karnataka.gov.in/new-page/Encumbrance%20Certificate%20After%2001-04-2004/en (post April 2004) and manually from SRO for pre-2004 records.

**Head Office**: Inspector General of Registration and Commissioner of Stamps, 3rd Floor, KHB Complex, Cauvery Bhavan, KG Road, Bengaluru – 560009. Phone: 080-22220984 / 080-22220985. Email: igr-ka@gov.in.

### 3.2 Bruhat Bengaluru Mahanagara Palike (BBMP)

**Role**: Municipal corporation governing the Bengaluru metropolitan area. Primary authority for property tax, Khata registration and transfer, building plan approval, and OC/CC within BBMP limits.

**Portals**: 
- Property Tax: https://bbmptax.karnataka.gov.in/
- Citizen Services (Khata, etc.): https://site.bbmp.gov.in/citizenservice.html

**What it reveals in due diligence**:
- Khata Certificate and Khata Extract: The Khata is the municipal register of a property owner. An "A Khata" indicates a property that is legally approved and pays regular property tax. A "B Khata" indicates an unauthorised structure. As of 2024, BBMP has instituted an e-Khata system. Absence of Khata or "B Khata" status is a significant red flag for financing and resale.
- Property Tax payment history: Confirms who has been paying property taxes (indicative of possession and ownership acknowledgement), current dues, and any arrears/penalties.
- Building Plan Approval status.
- Whether the property is on a storm-water drain (SWD) or road alignment, which may make it subject to demolition or acquisition.
- Occupancy Certificate (OC) and Completion Certificate (CC) for buildings within BBMP jurisdiction.

**Key check**: For any apartment in Bangalore, verify OC has been issued. Without OC, the building is technically unauthorised under the Karnataka Municipal Corporations Act, 1976, the National Building Code, and BBMP Building By-laws. Banks typically refuse loans on apartments without OC.

### 3.3 Real Estate Regulatory Authority, Karnataka (K-RERA)

**Role**: Statutory regulator for the real estate sector under the Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016. Mandatory registration of all new real estate projects (plotted developments, apartment projects, commercial projects) exceeding the prescribed thresholds.

**Website**: https://rera.karnataka.gov.in/

**What it reveals in due diligence**:
- RERA Registration Number of the project (format: PRM/KA/RERA/XXX/XXX/PRYYYY).
- Developer/promoter details, contact information, and track record.
- Land title documents disclosed by the builder on the RERA portal.
- Approved building plans and layout plans.
- Statutory approvals obtained (BDA/BBMP sanction, fire NOC, environmental clearance).
- Construction timeline, percentage of completion, and quarterly updates filed by the builder.
- Designated RERA escrow bank account details.
- Complaints filed against the project or the promoter.
- Real estate agent registration status (for verifying whether the agent handling the sale is RERA-registered).

**Critical check**: All ongoing and new residential/commercial projects with a plot area exceeding 500 sq. m. or involving more than 8 apartments MUST be registered with RERA. Selling or advertising without RERA registration is a statutory violation under Section 3, RERA Act. Buyers should cross-verify the RERA number cited by the builder against the official RERA portal: https://rera.karnataka.gov.in/viewAllProjects.

**K-RERA came into force**: May 1, 2017. Rules notified: Karnataka Real Estate Regulation and Development Rules, 2017, notified on July 10, 2017. Regular K-RERA Authority established: March 7, 2019.

### 3.4 Bangalore Development Authority (BDA)

**Role**: Planning and development authority for the Bengaluru Metropolitan Area. Approves layouts, allots plots, and issues building sanctions within its jurisdiction (areas outside BBMP but within the BDA planning area).

**Website (Property Tax)**: https://propertytax.bdabangalore.org/

**What it reveals in due diligence**:
- BDA allotment letters and possession certificates for BDA layout plots.
- Building plan approvals for properties in BDA jurisdiction.
- Status of BDA-approved layouts.
- Whether a particular survey number falls within BDA's Planning Area.
- BDA property tax records for BDA-allotted properties.
- Unauthorised layouts (sites carved out of agricultural land without BDA/BMRDA approval) can be identified.

**Key check**: Verify whether the layout in which the property is situated has received BDA/BMRDA/BBMP sanction. Purchasing a plot in an unapproved layout is one of the highest-risk transactions in Bangalore and has led to mass demolitions and acquisition of land by the BDA.

### 3.5 Bangalore Water Supply and Sewerage Board (BWSSB)

**Role**: Authority responsible for water supply and underground drainage (sewerage) connections in Bangalore.

**Website**: https://bwssb.gov.in/

**What it reveals in due diligence**:
- Whether a water connection (khata) exists in the property's name and whether dues are paid.
- Pending dues or disconnection orders affecting the property.
- Whether the property has obtained a proper water and sewerage connection (absence of which may indicate an unauthorised structure).
- NOC status for new connections.

### 3.6 Revenue Department — Bhoomi Portal / Atalji Janasnehi Kendra

**Role**: The Revenue Department of Karnataka maintains the foundational land records — particularly for land outside BBMP limits, agricultural land, and converted land. The Bhoomi Portal (https://landrecords.karnataka.gov.in/) is the official gateway for digital land records.

**Portals**: 
- Bhoomi (RTC/Pahani, Mutation, Khata Extract): https://landrecords.karnataka.gov.in/
- Digitally Signed RTC: https://rtc.karnataka.gov.in/

**What it reveals in due diligence**:
- RTC / Pahani (Record of Rights, Tenancy and Crops): The most important revenue record, showing the survey number, extent, owner's name, possessory rights, nature of land (wet/dry/garden), classification (whether agricultural/non-agricultural), tenancy details, any encumbrances noted (mortgages, attachments), and government demand (tax payable).
- Mutation Register (MR): Records of ownership changes following transactions, inheritances, court orders, or government orders. Verifying the mutation history establishes the chain of title in revenue records.
- Khata Extract: Municipal-equivalent for revenue areas showing registered owner and area.
- Bagair Hukum (Unauthorized Occupations): Bhoomi shows entries under this category, flagging unauthorized encroachments on government or reserved land.
- Land Classification: Reveals whether land is classified as "B Kharab" (government waste), gomala (grazing land), forest, or revenue land — with critical implications for purchasability.
- DC Conversion status: Whether conversion from agricultural to non-agricultural use has been recorded.

**Bhoomi launched**: 2000. I-RTC (Instant digitally signed RTC) available for download at ₹25 per document.

## 4. COMPLETE DUE DILIGENCE CHECKLIST

### 4.1 Title Chain Documents (Minimum 30 Years)

The title chain is the sequential record of all ownership transfers in the property's history. For a 30-year search (courts and banks typically require at least 30 years; advocates often recommend 60 years for older properties with complex histories):

**Step 1 — Identify Root of Title**: The "root of title" is the earliest document that establishes ownership. For Bangalore properties developed on converted agricultural land, this is typically the DC Conversion Order + the original Sale Deed from the layout developer. For older city properties, it may be a grant, partition decree, or colonial-era document.

**Step 2 — Obtain All Intermediate Documents**:
- All Sale Deeds / Conveyance Deeds
- Gift Deeds
- Partition Deeds and Family Settlement Deeds
- Release Deeds
- Court Decrees (by way of inheritance or partition)
- Probated Wills
- Power of Attorney (registered) used in any transaction
- Joint Development Agreement (JDA) if land was given for development
- General Power of Attorney (GPA) executed by landowner in favour of developer (IMPORTANT: verify it remains valid and was not revoked; GPA-based sales are high-risk since the Supreme Court in Suraj Lamp & Industries Pvt. Ltd. v. State of Haryana & Anr. (2012) held that GPA-based transactions do not confer title)

**Step 3 — Check for Continuity**: Each document in the chain must derive from the immediately preceding owner. Gaps in the chain (a deed from a person who does not appear in the chain of title) are serious defects requiring legal remedy.

### 4.2 Encumbrance Certificate (EC)

**What it is**: The official record from the Sub-Registrar's Office of all transactions registered against the property during the EC period. Issued as Form 15 (Encumbrances Found) or Form 16 (Nil Encumbrances).

**Period to search**: Minimum 30 years; ideally 60 years. The EC should cover the entire period of the title chain being verified.

**How to read**: Each entry in the EC corresponds to a registered document (sale deed, mortgage, release, partition, etc.). Verify every entry against the documents in the title chain. Any mortgage/charge not discharged is a live encumbrance.

**Limitation of EC**: The EC covers only registered transactions. Oral agreements, unregistered mortgages, government acquisition proceedings not yet registered, and certain statutory charges (income tax liens, ESIC dues) may not appear in the EC. Therefore, EC must be supplemented by revenue records, litigation search, and statutory dues verification.

**EC for pre-2004 periods**: Must be obtained manually from the SRO.

### 4.3 Khata Certificate and Khata Extract

**BBMP Jurisdiction**: Khata is the primary civic record of a property in Bangalore. It is maintained by BBMP ward offices. A Khata includes the owner's name, PID (Property Identification Number), property address, built-up area, and tax assessment.

- **A Khata**: Issued for properties that are legally approved (building plan sanctioned, layout approved, OC received). Required for home loans, registering property, and obtaining utility connections.
- **B Khata**: Issued under Section 144 of the BBMP Act for revenue-paying but legally irregular/unauthorized properties (e.g., properties on unapproved layouts, buildings without OC). Cannot be used for new building plan sanctions.
- **e-Khata (2024 onward)**: BBMP has introduced a digital e-Khata system. Verify e-Khata status on the BBMP portal.

**Khata Transfer**: Upon purchase, the buyer must apply for Khata transfer from the seller's name to the buyer's name within a reasonable time. Failure leads to difficulties in future property tax payments and resale.

**Revenue Area (outside BBMP)**: Khata Extract is available from the Bhoomi portal.

### 4.4 Property Tax Receipts

- Obtain property tax receipts for the last 5–10 years (minimum).
- Verify receipts are in the seller's name (or correctly reflecting the chain of title).
- Check for any arrears on the BBMP Property Tax portal: https://bbmptax.karnataka.gov.in/.
- Ensure latest financial year's property tax is paid up to date.
- Property tax receipts also serve as supplementary evidence of possession and ownership.

### 4.5 Approved Building Plan / Layout Plan

- Obtain sanctioned building plan issued by BBMP / BDA / BMRDA / local planning authority.
- Verify plan approval number, date, and the authority that granted it.
- Check if any deviations exist between the sanctioned plan and actual construction. Deviations may attract demolition notices or regularization fees.
- For apartment projects: verify every floor has plan approval; verify that the area being sold (super built-up, carpet area) corresponds to the sanctioned plan and RERA disclosure.
- For layout plots: verify layout approval from BDA/BBMP/BMRDA; check that the plot number, dimensions, and road widths match the approved layout plan.

### 4.6 Occupancy Certificate (OC) / Completion Certificate (CC)

- **OC (Occupancy Certificate)**: Issued by BBMP/BDA upon completion of construction confirming that the building has been constructed as per the approved plan and is fit for occupation.
- **CC (Completion Certificate)**: Similar to OC in many contexts; some authorities issue CC after construction and OC after inspection for habitability.
- OC is mandatory before taking possession of any new apartment/building. Under the Karnataka Apartment Ownership Act, 1972, the promoter must provide OC to the purchaser.
- Banks refuse to sanction loans on properties without OC.
- BBMP issues demolition notices for buildings occupied without OC.
- For older buildings (pre-2000), OC records may not be digitised; in such cases, verify that the building has been regularized under any government regularization scheme (e.g., BBMP regularization scheme) or obtain documentary evidence of age-old occupation.

### 4.7 RERA Registration Certificate

- For all new and ongoing residential/commercial projects, verify RERA registration at: https://rera.karnataka.gov.in/viewAllProjects
- Confirm the RERA registration number format: PRM/KA/RERA/[District Code]/[Project Number]/[Year].
- Check registration status (registered, expired, or lapsed).
- Review all documents uploaded by the developer on the RERA portal, including: land title opinion, approved plans, commencement certificate, sanctioned layouts, and quarterly project updates.
- Verify there are no complaints filed against the project or promoter on the RERA portal.
- Confirm that the Sale Agreement offered by the builder is RERA-compliant.

### 4.8 Litigation Search

- Conduct searches at the following fora:
  - **City Civil Court, Bengaluru / District Civil Court**: For civil suits relating to title, possession, partition, specific performance, injunctions.
  - **Karnataka High Court**: For second appeals, writ petitions, and company matters involving the property.
  - **Revenue Tribunal (SLAO / KSSAT)**: For land acquisition matters under RFCTLARR Act, 2013.
  - **Debt Recovery Tribunal (DRT), Bengaluru**: For recovery proceedings involving lenders holding mortgage over the property.
  - **Revenue Courts / Assistant Commissioner / Deputy Commissioner**: For RTC disputes, partition disputes, tenancy matters.
  - **RERA tribunal**: For complaints against builder.
  - **National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission / SCDRC Karnataka**: For consumer complaints against builders.
- Litigation search must be conducted by a qualified advocate who has access to court records. Online Court services at https://services.ecourts.gov.in/ can assist for preliminary searches.

### 4.9 Revenue Records (RTC, Mutation Register)

- Obtain current year RTC from Bhoomi portal (https://landrecords.karnataka.gov.in/) for the survey number.
- Verify: owner's name, extent of land, survey number, nature of land (dry/wet/garden), classification (agricultural, non-agricultural, B kharab, gomala, etc.), any government demand entries, any encumbrance/lis pendens entries in the RTC.
- Obtain Mutation Register extract to trace ownership history in revenue records.
- Check Bagair Hukum section on Bhoomi for unauthorized occupation entries.
- For converted land: confirm DC Conversion Order is reflected in the RTC and that the land is no longer classified as agricultural.
- Check Village Revenue Records at the Atalji Janasnehi Kendra (Nadakacheri): https://nadakacheri.karnataka.gov.in/ for Caste/Income/Residence certificates is not directly relevant, but the Taluk Office / Tahsildar records must be checked for any government orders affecting the land.

### 4.10 DC Conversion Order (For Agricultural Land)

- Obtained from the Office of the Deputy Commissioner (DC) of the concerned district.
- Confirms the land has been converted from agricultural to non-agricultural (residential/commercial) use under Section 95, Karnataka Land Revenue Act, 1964.
- Verify: date of conversion order, survey numbers covered, purpose of conversion (residential / commercial / industrial), any conditions attached, validity period of the conversion order, and whether NA betterment charges have been paid.
- Absence of DC Conversion means the property is still classified as agricultural land, making it (generally) ineligible for residential construction and for purchase by non-agriculturists.
- Post-2020 amendments to Karnataka Land Reforms Act relaxed Section 79A/79B restrictions for certain categories, but agricultural land classification in RTC and DC Conversion remains critical.

### 4.11 Other Critical Documents

- **Sanctioned Layout Plan**: For plotted developments, the approved layout plan from BDA/BBMP/BMRDA showing roads, civic amenity sites, parks, and individual plots.
- **Joint Development Agreement (JDA) and General Power of Attorney (GPA)**: For builder apartments constructed on land owner's land. Verify GPA is still valid (not revoked, not expired, and the principal is alive and competent).
- **Release Deed**: Where a prior mortgage has been discharged, verify a Release Deed was registered.
- **Partition Deed / Family Settlement**: For properties derived through partition, verify all co-sharers executed the partition deed and it is registered.
- **Will**: Where property was inherited by way of will, verify: will is probated (if required), or at minimum that it is duly attested, not contested, and mutation has been effected based on the will with the consent of all interested parties.
- **NOC from Housing Society / Apartment Association**: For apartment resales in cooperative housing societies or registered apartment associations, obtain NOC from the association before registration.
- **BESCOM NOC**: For confirming electrical connection status and any dues.

## 5. HOW TO OBTAIN EACH DOCUMENT — EXACT PORTALS AND OFFICES

### 5.1 Encumbrance Certificate (EC)

**Online (Post April 2004)**:
- Portal: https://kaverionline.karnataka.gov.in/ (Kaveri Online Services)
- Process: Register as user → Login → Select "Online EC" → Enter property details (SRO, District, survey/property number) → Pay fee online → Download EC.
- Also accessible at: https://igr.karnataka.gov.in/new-page/Encumbrance%20Certificate%20After%2001-04-2004/en

**Offline (Pre-2004 and for certified copies)**:
- Visit the concerned Sub-Registrar's Office (SRO) based on the jurisdictional location of the property.
- Submit Form 22A application with property details.
- The SRO extracts the EC from index registers (manual for pre-digitisation period).
- SRO offices in Bangalore are located at: Basavanagudi, Indiranagar, Jayanagar, Koramangala, Rajajinagar, Yelahanka, and other zones.

**Contact**: IGR Karnataka, 080-22220984 / 080-22220985, igr-ka@gov.in

### 5.2 Certified Copies of Registered Documents

**Online**: 
- Portal: https://kaverionline.karnataka.gov.in/ → "Online CC" (Certified Copy).
- Enter document number and registration year.
- Pay fee and download.

**Offline**: 
- Apply at the SRO where the document was registered.
- Submit Form 22A with document details.
- Fee: ₹1 per page (approximately), plus search fee.

### 5.3 RTC / Pahani (Land Revenue Records)

**Online**:
- Bhoomi Portal: https://landrecords.karnataka.gov.in/
- Direct RTC access: https://rtc.karnataka.gov.in/ (digitally signed I-RTC)
- Process: Select District → Taluk → Hobli → Village → Survey Number → View RTC.
- Digitally signed I-RTC: ₹25 (downloadable with official digital signature).

**Offline**:
- Atalji Janasnehi Kendra (Nadakacheri) at Taluk offices or any Common Service Centre (CSC).
- Bhoomi Monitoring Cell, SSLR Building, K.R. Circle, Bengaluru - 560001. Phone: 080-22113255.

### 5.4 Property Tax Records (BBMP)

**Online**:
- BBMP Property Tax Portal: https://bbmptax.karnataka.gov.in/
- Enter SAS Application Number, PID Number, or Owner Name.

## 7. TIMELINE — HOW LONG A THOROUGH DUE DILIGENCE TAKES

A properly conducted property due diligence in Bangalore typically requires:

### 7.1 Standard Resale Apartment / Flat
- **Document collection**: 3–7 days (online EC, RTC, BBMP records)
- **Advocate review and opinion**: 5–10 working days
- **Total**: **10–21 days** (for a clean property with all documents readily available)

### 7.2 Independent House / Plot / Converted Land
- **Document collection**: 7–15 days (pre-2004 EC requires manual SRO visit; older revenue records may need physical retrieval)
- **Advocate review**: 10–20 working days
- **Litigation search**: 3–7 additional days
- **Total**: **20–45 days** (for properties with complex title or older documents)

### 7.3 Agricultural Land / Conversion Land
- **Document collection including DC conversion records, RTC, mutation history**: 10–21 days
- **Advocate review**: 15–30 working days
- **Total**: **30–60 days** (for properties with complex agricultural law considerations)

### 7.4 New Builder Apartment (RERA)
- **RERA verification**: 1–3 days (online)
- **Document review (builder's land title, JDA, GPA, OC upon completion)**: 10–20 working days
- **Total**: **14–30 days**

### 7.5 Factors Causing Delays
- SRO offices are busy; manual EC searches for pre-2004 periods can take 5–15 working days per visit.
- Revenue records at the Bhoomi portal may have data inconsistencies requiring physical verification at the Nadakacheri / Taluk office.
- Complex title (multiple inheritance events, old partition deeds, handwritten documents in Kannada) requires more time.
- Court record searches (especially at DRT and Revenue Courts) can take additional time.

**Practical guidance**: A buyer should initiate due diligence immediately upon identifying a property and before signing any token advance / MOU. Signing agreements before due diligence completion exposes the buyer to legal risk.

## 8. LEGAL PROVISIONS RELEVANT TO CLEAR TITLE AND DUE DILIGENCE

### 8.1 Transfer of Property Act, 1882 (applicable in Karnataka)

- **Section 3 (Constructive Notice)**: A person is deemed to have notice of any fact if they would have discovered it by making reasonable inquiries. Buyers who fail to conduct due diligence have constructive notice of all discoverable encumbrances and defects.

- **Section 17 (Documents of which registration is compulsory)**: Requires compulsory registration of instruments creating, assigning, limiting, or extinguishing rights in immovable property valued above ₹100. Unregistered instruments cannot be used as evidence of title (Section 49, Registration Act).

- **Section 38 (Transfer by ostensible owner)**: A person who makes a representation (express or implied) that an ostensible owner is the true owner cannot deny the title of a transferee who took in good faith. Requires due diligence by the buyer to verify apparent ownership.

- **Section 41 (Transfer by person having authority to transfer)**: Protects a bona fide purchaser for value from the holder of a Power of Attorney, provided the purchaser acts in good faith. Due diligence of the GPA's validity and scope is therefore critical.

- **Section 52 (Lis Pendens)**: During the pendency of any suit involving an immovable property, a transfer of that property by any party to the suit is subject to the result of the suit. Litigation search is therefore essential to avoid purchasing a lis pendens-affected property.

- **Section 53 (Fraudulent Transfer)**: Transfers made to defeat or delay creditors are voidable. Due diligence should check for antecedent debts and creditor proceedings.

- **Section 54 (Sale)**: A contract for the sale of immovable property does not, by itself, create any interest in or charge on such property. Only a registered Sale Deed conveys title.

- **Section 58 (Mortgage)**: Defines various forms of mortgage; a mortgage by deposit of title deed (equitable mortgage) does not require registration but is valid. EC may not always capture equitable mortgages. Inquire specifically with the seller about any informal charges.

- **Section 100 (Charges)**: All charges on immovable property must be verified through the EC and revenue records.

### 8.2 Registration Act, 1908

- **Section 17**: Lists documents requiring compulsory registration, including sale deeds, gift deeds, and other instruments creating rights in immovable property.
- **Section 49**: An unregistered document required to be registered under Section 17 is inadmissible as evidence of the transaction it purports to affect. This confirms that only a registered Sale Deed conveys legal title.
- **Section 53A**: Protects part-performance of contract in certain circumstances, making it necessary to investigate whether any registered agreement to sell is outstanding against the property.

### 8.3 Karnataka Stamps Act, 1957 / Indian Stamp Act, 1899 (as applicable)

- Instruments not duly stamped cannot be admitted as evidence and cannot be acted upon unless the deficiency plus penalty is paid.
- In due diligence, verify that all documents in the title chain are properly stamped as per the rates in force at the time of execution.

### 8.4 Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016 (RERA)

- **Section 3**: Mandatory registration of new real estate projects.
- **Section 4**: Promoter must disclose title details, encumbrances, and approvals at the time of RERA registration.
- **Section 11(3)(a)**: Promoter must maintain separate bank accounts for each project (70% escrow rule).
- **Section 13**: Agreement for Sale to be executed (maximum 10% of total consideration may be collected before execution of Sale Agreement).
- **Section 14**: Construction must be as per sanctioned plans; any structural defect liability is on the promoter for 5 years post-possession.
- **Section 18**: Promoter is liable to refund amounts with interest and compensation for failure to give possession within agreed date.
- **Section 19**: Buyer rights, including right to receive all information, to receive timely possession, and to receive refund.
- **Section 31**: Complaint filing by any aggrieved person with K-RERA for violations of the Act.

### 8.5 Karnataka Land Revenue Act, 1964

- **Section 95**: Governs conversion of agricultural land to non-agricultural use. No construction on agricultural land is permissible without prior DC Conversion Order.
- **Section 136**: Specifies consequences of unauthorized occupation of government land.

### 8.6 Karnataka Land Reforms Act, 1961

- **Section 79A**: Restricts non-agriculturists from purchasing agricultural land (with limited exceptions). Section 79A was amended in 2020 to allow any person to purchase agricultural land (removing the income ceiling restriction) — but the buyer must still comply with ceiling limits under the Act.
- **Section 79B**: Prohibits grant of agricultural land to non-agriculturists (with certain exceptions). Check current judicial interpretation of Sections 79A/79B as amendments have been litigated.

### 8.7 Karnataka Apartment Ownership Act, 1972

- Governs the sale of apartments and defines undivided share (UDS) in land.
- Promoter must form an Apartment Owners' Association upon completion.
- Section 17A: Promoter must produce OC before demanding final payment.

### 8.8 SARFAESI Act, 2002 (for auctioned properties)

- Section 13: Empowers secured creditors to enforce security interest without court intervention.
- Rule 8 and Rule 9, Security Interest (Enforcement) Rules, 2002: Govern the procedure for taking possession and conducting public auction.
- Sale Certificate issued under SARFAESI must be registered: Section 17(1A), Registration Act (inserted by amendment).

### 8.9 FEMA, 1999 and RBI Master Directions

- Section 6(3)(i): Restricts acquisition and transfer of immovable property in India by persons resident outside India except as permitted by RBI.
- RBI Master Direction on Acquisition and Transfer of Immovable Property under FEMA must be verified where the seller is an NRI or foreign entity.

## 9. RED FLAGS — FINDINGS THAT SHOULD STOP A TRANSACTION

The following findings, if identified during due diligence, should give serious pause or require resolution before proceeding:

### 9.1 Title Defects

- **Gap in Title Chain**: A conveyance from a person who does not appear as an owner in the preceding documents — indicates possible forgery, unauthorized transfer, or incomplete chain.
- **GPA-Based Title**: If the seller's entire claim to title rests on a General Power of Attorney (not a registered Sale Deed), this is an extreme red flag. The Supreme Court in Suraj Lamp & Industries (2012) held that GPA transfers do not confer title.
- **Oral Partition**: Claims of title based on unregistered/undocumented oral family partitions, without supporting revenue records or a registered family settlement/partition deed.
- **Unregistered Sale Deed**: Any claim to title based on an unregistered or improperly stamped document.
- **Multiple Sellers with Disputed Shares**: Where property derives from a family and some heirs dispute the share of others.

### 9.2 Encumbrances and Charges

- **Live Mortgage / Charge in EC**: An EC showing a registered mortgage that has not been discharged by a registered Release Deed. A buyer who purchases a mortgaged property takes it subject to the mortgage, and the mortgagee lender can still enforce the security.
- **Attachment Orders**: EC showing an attachment by court order, income tax department, or enforcement agency. The property cannot be transferred until the attachment is lifted.
- **Prior Agreement to Sell**: EC revealing a registered Agreement to Sell to a third party — the property is already contractually committed to another buyer.
- **Lease Deed**: An EC showing a long-term registered lease may limit the seller's ability to deliver vacant possession.

### 9.3 Revenue Record Issues

- **B Kharab / Government Land Classification**: If the RTC classifies any portion of the land as "B Kharab" (government waste/forest/water body), that portion is government property and cannot be sold. Entire layouts built on government/kharab land have been demolished in Bangalore.
- **Bagair Hukum Entry (Unauthorized Occupation)**: Indicates that the occupant has no legal right; the government can evict and reclaim.
- **Gomala / Grazing Land**: Land classified as Gomala is village common land; its sale is illegal.
- **Lake Bed / Wetland / Storm Water Drain**: Bangalore has hundreds of lakes; properties encroaching on lake beds have been demolished by BBMP and the High Court of Karnataka has ordered regularization of many Bengaluru lakes. Verify the property is not on a lake bed, rejuvenation zone, or SWD.
- **Missing Mutation**: If the mutation of the latest Sale Deed in the seller's name is not recorded in the RTC, it may indicate a defect in the registration process or a disputed claim.
- **Agricultural Classification Without Conversion**: If the RTC still shows the land as agricultural (Judi — agricultural tax payable) without a DC Conversion Order, no lawful residential construction can be made.
- **Tenancy Rights / Occupancy Rights**: If the RTC shows a recorded tenant or occupancy right holder under the Karnataka Land Reforms Act, the land is encumbered by occupancy rights that cannot be overridden by the landowner's sale.

### 9.4 Regulatory Non-Compliance

- **Unapproved Layout**: If the layout in which the plot is situated has not received BDA/BBMP/BMRDA approval, it is an unauthorized layout. Banks will not finance such property; BBMP may not issue Khata or connections.
- **Missing or Absent OC**: An apartment without an Occupancy Certificate is technically unauthorized for habitation. This prevents bank financing, Khata issuance, and resale to financed buyers.
- **Deviations from Sanctioned Plan**: Building constructed with significant deviation from the approved plan (e.g., extra floors, encroachment on setbacks, road widening, increased FAR/FSI) is illegal construction subject to demolition notice.
- **RERA Unregistered Builder Project**: Selling or marketing a new project without RERA registration is a violation of Section 3 of the RERA Act. Buying from an unregistered project denies the buyer all statutory protections under RERA.
- **Expired RERA Registration**: If RERA registration has lapsed (project not completed and extension not sought), the builder is in default and the buyer's rights are at risk.

### 9.5 Pending Litigation

- **Lis Pendens (Suit Affecting Title)**: Any pending suit for title, partition, declaration, or injunction involving the property creates a statutory bar under Section 52, Transfer of Property Act. Transfer during the pendency of such suit is subject to the outcome of the litigation.
- **Writ Petitions in High Court**: Any writ petition challenging demolition notices, BDA acquisition, or similar government action relating to the property.
- **Insolvency / Bankruptcy of Seller**: If the seller is subject to insolvency proceedings under the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC), 2016, the resolution professional / liquidator holds control of assets; the seller cannot transfer the property independently.
- **DRT / SARFAESI Proceedings**: If a bank has filed a SARFAESI/DRT case against the property's mortgage, the property may be taken over by the bank; any purchase from the original owner after possession notice is void.
- **Income Tax Attachment**: IT department can attach a person's property for tax recovery; check for any attachment orders.

### 9.6 Document Authenticity Issues

- **Forged Documents**: In Bangalore, fraudulent Sale Deeds using impersonation (impersonating the original owner, forged notarized documents, fabricated documents) are a known risk, particularly for properties belonging to NRI owners or with absentee landlords.
  - Verification: Cross-check every document's registration number, date, and SRO against the IGR Karnataka records; obtain certified copies directly from the SRO rather than relying on photocopies provided by the seller.
  - BIOMETRIC AUTHENTICATION: From April 2024, Kaveri 2.0 requires biometric authentication for property registration, reducing forgery risk for future transactions.
- **Misrepresentation of Survey Number**: Properties where the title documents describe a different survey number than the physically identified land — requires physical survey and reconciliation.
- **Old Unregistered Documents Being Used as Links**: Some sellers present old unregistered "agreements" or "patta" documents as links in the title chain. These are legally invalid under Section 49, Registration Act.

### 9.7 Other Red Flags

- **Inadequate Stamp Duty on Earlier Documents**: If documents in the title chain were executed with insufficient stamp duty, the deficiency creates an instrument that cannot be admitted as evidence until the deficit is paid.
- **Power of Attorney as Seller**: If the person negotiating the sale is acting under a Power of Attorney and not the registered owner, verify the GPA is registered, valid, not revoked, and specifically authorises sale of the property at the agreed consideration.
- **Seller Under Legal Disability**: Seller who is a minor, person of unsound mind, or under guardianship cannot execute a valid Sale Deed without court approval.
- **Property in Sensitive Zone**: Property within 75m of a lake boundary, within the buffer zone of a protected forest, or within the prohibited area of a national monument cannot be developed and may be subject to demolition or acquisition.

## 10. CONSEQUENCES OF SKIPPING DUE DILIGENCE

### 10.1 Disputed Title / Loss of Ownership

**Scenario**: Buyer purchases a property from a seller who appears to hold title based on a Sale Deed. Unknown to the buyer, the seller had previously sold the property to another party by a registered Sale Deed that predates the current sale. The EC for the relevant period (if obtained) would have revealed this prior sale. Buyer skipped EC verification or relied on a short-period EC.

**Legal consequence**: Under the rule of "prior tempore, potior iure" (first in time, stronger in right), and under Section 48, Transfer of Property Act (providing for the priority of prior registrations for mortgages/charges), the prior registered transaction holds priority. Courts have consistently held that a subsequent purchaser with constructive notice of a prior registered deed cannot claim to be a bona fide purchaser.

**Real-world example in Karnataka**: Hundreds of apartment buyers in Bangalore have lost possession of properties where builders sold the same apartment to multiple buyers (double-selling fraud), or where the land title had disputes. RERA registration reduces this risk for new projects by mandating escrow accounts and requiring builder disclosure.

### 10.2 Illegal Construction / Demolition

**Scenario**: Buyer purchases an apartment from a builder at full price. The buyer does not verify the building plan approval or OC. After taking possession, BBMP issues a demolition notice because the building was constructed without plan approval or with gross deviations (extra floors, encroachment on setback, etc.).

**Legal consequence**: Under Section 321 of the Karnataka Municipal Corporations Act, 1976, BBMP has power to demolish unauthorized structures. Courts have upheld BBMP's demolition orders in dozens of cases (including the demolition of Jakkur lake encroachment buildings, Bellandur area layouts, etc.). The buyer loses both the property and the purchase consideration paid, with very limited legal recourse against the builder (particularly if the builder is insolvent or absconding).

**Bank loan consequences**: Banks refuse to sanction loans on properties without OC. If a buyer secured a loan on a fraudulent OC, the bank's security is compromised and the buyer may be held liable for misrepresentation.

### 10.3 Agricultural / Unapproved Layout Purchase

**Scenario**: Buyer purchases a plot in a "private layout" in Bangalore's outskirts without verifying BDA/BMRDA/BBMP approval of the layout. The layout was carved out of agricultural land without DC Conversion or BDA approval.

**Legal consequence**: (a) The plot cannot be registered as a non-agricultural property. (b) BBMP/BDA will not issue Khata or water/electricity connections. (c) No building plan will be sanctioned. (d) The land may be acquired by BDA for formation of an approved layout, with compensation at government guidance value (often far below market price). (e) The buyer cannot mortgage or resell the property through banks.

### 10.4 NRI / Forged Document Fraud

**Scenario**: Fraudsters impersonate an NRI owner using forged identity documents, produce a fraudulent Power of Attorney, and sell the property to an unsuspecting buyer.

**Legal consequence**: The sale is void ab initio (null from the beginning). The NRI's original ownership is not extinguished. Courts have consistently held that a fraudulent title is no title. The buyer loses the purchase consideration and may face a lengthy civil suit to recover money from the fraudster (often untraceable or absconded abroad). Criminal complaints under IPC Sections 420 (cheating), 467/468/471 (forgery) can be filed, but do not recover the money.

**Prevention**: Always verify the seller's title by obtaining certified copies of all chain documents directly from SRO; verify the identity of the seller (or PoA holder) with original identity documents; insist on biometric registration at the SRO.

### 10.5 Encumbrance / Bank Mortgage Not Discharged

**Scenario**: Seller took a home loan from a bank, mortgaged the property (registered mortgage deed), and later sold the property without disclosing the mortgage. Buyer does not run an Encumbrance Certificate.

**Legal consequence**: Under Section 58, Transfer of Property Act, the mortgage follows the property. The mortgagee bank can enforce the mortgage against the new buyer under the SARFAESI Act. Courts have upheld banks' rights to take possession of mortgaged property even from innocent subsequent buyers. The buyer must pay off the seller's loan to protect their own ownership.

**Additional risk**: If the seller used the same property to secure multiple loans from different lenders ("double financing"), the buyer may face claims from multiple secured creditors.

### 10.6 Home Loan Rejection

**Practical consequence**: Banks conduct their own legal due diligence before sanctioning a home loan. If the bank's panel advocate discovers a title defect (missing OC, unapproved layout, live encumbrance, lis pendens), the bank will reject the loan application. A buyer who has already paid a significant advance to the seller on the basis of a pending loan may face financial hardship and possible forfeiture of the advance (depending on the Sale Agreement terms).

### 10.7 Revenue Site (BBMP B-Khata) Complications

**Scenario**: Buyer purchases an apartment or house with B Khata (unauthorized property). Buyer discovers that banks refuse to finance B-Khata properties, resale is difficult (most buyers require A-Khata for loan-backed purchase), and BBMP connection applications are rejected.

**Long-term consequence**: The property is effectively illiquid — difficult to sell except to cash buyers at a discount. Regularization (where available under government schemes) involves payment of penalties and is not guaranteed.

### 10.8 Litigation Involvement

**Scenario**: Buyer purchases a property in which a partition suit was filed by an estranged co-owner two years before the sale. The suit was registered in the City Civil Court but not reflected in the Encumbrance Certificate (litigation may not appear in EC unless a court attachment order is specifically registered). Buyer did not conduct a litigation search.

**Legal consequence**: Under Section 52 (Lis Pendens), the transfer during the pendency of the suit is subject to the result of the suit. If the court ultimately partitions the property and awards a share to the estranged co-owner, the buyer may be bound by that outcome and lose a portion of the property purchased.

### 10.9 Total Loss of Investment in Builder Default

**Scenario**: Buyer invests ₹50 lakhs in an under-construction apartment. Builder has not registered with RERA and has used the funds without following the 70% escrow rule. Builder becomes insolvent, construction halts, and the project is stuck.

**Legal consequence without RERA protection**: Pre-RERA, buyers had very limited recourse — only consumer forum or civil court (slow, uncertain). Post-RERA, a registered project allows buyers to file complaints for refund with interest at prescribed rate (Section 18, RERA Act) or seek completion under RERA's supervision. However, if the project is unregistered, even RERA remedies are unavailable.

**Key lesson**: Verifying RERA registration before investing in any new project is non-negotiable and is the single most effective protection for a new apartment buyer.
