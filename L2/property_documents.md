Ownership must be proven through registered title documents such as sale deeds, gift deeds, or inheritance records. During title verification, Khata is used as a supporting document to confirm consistency across records, but it cannot replace a registered title deed.

A clear title requires a continuous chain of registered ownership documents, a clean encumbrance certificate, and matching municipal records. It should also be free from court cases, government acquisition notices, or unpaid statutory dues. Properties with clear titles are easier to sell, inherit, or mortgage and are readily accepted by banks and financial institutions for loans.

The Mother Deed fills this gap by validating the ownership lineage, making both documents necessary for clear title verification.
