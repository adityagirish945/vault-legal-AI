## Frequently Asked Questions

MODT cancellation is the formal legal process of removing the mortgage entry created when a home loan was taken. During loan sanction, banks record a Memorandum of Deposit of Title Deeds to secure the loan. Even after full repayment, this mortgage remains active in government records unless cancelled at the Sub-Registrar Office. MODT cancellation confirms that the bank no longer has any claim on the property. Without this step, the property is still shown as mortgaged in official records, which can create issues during resale, transfer, or loan applications.

Yes, MODT cancellation is mandatory after full home loan repayment. Loan closure alone only settles the financial obligation with the bank. It does not automatically remove the legal charge recorded in property records. If MODT is not cancelled, the property continues to reflect a bank mortgage during legal verification. This can delay or block property sale, gift, inheritance transfer, or redevelopment. Most buyers and banks insist on MODT cancellation before proceeding with transactions, making it an essential step for clear ownership.

MODT cancellation cannot be completed fully online. While some states allow online appointment booking or document uploads, the final process must be done offline at the Sub-Registrar Office. Physical presence is required for document verification, biometric authentication, and registration. As of now, states like Karnataka and Tamil Nadu do not support end-to-end online MODT cancellation. Borrowers must visit the same Sub-Registrar Office where the original MODT was registered to complete the process legally.

MODT cancellation must be initiated by the property owner whose name appears in the home loan and property documents. If the owner cannot attend personally, an authorized representative can complete the process with a valid authorization letter. Banks do not handle MODT cancellation on behalf of borrowers. The responsibility lies with the property owner to ensure the mortgage entry is legally closed after loan repayment. Completing this step protects the owner from future legal and financial complications.

MODT cancellation charges are paid by the borrower or property owner. These charges include government registration fees and nominal stamp duty as applicable under state rules. Banks generally do not cover these costs. The exact amount varies based on the state and registration slabs. Borrowers should verify applicable charges at the Sub-Registrar Office in advance to avoid delays during registration.

No, MODT charges are not refundable. The fees paid during MODT registration and cancellation are statutory charges collected by the government for recording and updating property records. Even if the loan is prepaid or closed early, these charges cannot be reclaimed. Borrowers should treat MODT charges as a one-time legal expense linked to home loan documentation and closure.

MODT cancellation usually takes between 10 and 20 working days. This includes time for collecting loan closure documents from the bank and completing registration at the Sub-Registrar Office. Delays may occur if documents are missing, drafts contain errors, or appointments are unavailable. Proper preparation and verification of documents can help complete the process faster.

MODT cancellation requires a loan closure certificate, bank NOC, MODT deed or release letter, property sale deed copy, identity proof, and address proof of the owner. If a representative attends, an authorization letter is also required. Carrying both originals and photocopies helps avoid rescheduling or rejection during registration.

If MODT is not cancelled, the property continues to show a mortgage entry in government records. This can cause loan rejection for buyers, negative legal opinions, and delays in property sale or transfer. In some cases, transactions may be completely blocked until MODT cancellation is completed. Ignoring this step often leads to avoidable legal disputes and reduced property marketability.

Yes, MODT is mandatory for most home loans secured through equitable mortgage. It allows banks to create a legal charge on the property without registering a full mortgage deed. Since MODT forms the basis of the bank’s security, its cancellation is equally important once the loan is repaid. Proper MODT closure ensures clear title and complete ownership rights.

## Frequently Asked Questions

Capital gains tax is the tax levied on the profit earned when selling a property in Bangalore. Only the difference between the selling price and the indexed purchase price is taxed, not the full sale amount.

Yes. Under Section 54, if you sell a residential property and buy another residential property within the prescribed time limits, you can claim full exemption on long-term capital gains, subject to conditions.

You can buy the new residential property one year before or two years after the sale, or construct a house within three years from the sale date to claim capital gains tax exemption.

Yes. Under Section 54F, if you sell land or a commercial property and invest the capital gains in a residential property, full exemption is available, provided you do not own more than one residential property at the time of sale.

Yes. You can invest in Section 54EC capital gains bonds such as NHAI, REC, or IRFC within six months of the sale. Up to ₹50 lakh can be exempt, and the bonds have a five-year lock-in period.

The Capital Gains Account Scheme (CGAS) is a special bank account where you can temporarily deposit your capital gains if you have not yet reinvested in a property or bonds. The funds must be used within the allowed timeframe to retain the tax exemption.

If you sell the newly purchased property before completing the minimum holding period, the capital gains exemption claimed earlier will be reversed and tax will become payable.

Yes. Sections 54D, 54G, and 54GA provide capital gains tax exemptions when industrial property is compulsorily acquired or when industries are relocated to rural areas or Special Economic Zones (SEZs), provided the gains are reinvested in eligible assets.
