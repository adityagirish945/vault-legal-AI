## How to Get 200 Units Free Electricity in Karnataka (Gruha Jyothi Scheme) | Vault PropTech

**📍 Source:** [https://www.vaultproptech.com/blogs/How-to-Get-200-Units-Free-Electricity-in-Karnataka](https://www.vaultproptech.com/blogs/How-to-Get-200-Units-Free-Electricity-in-Karnataka)

## Frequently Asked Questions

To get 200 units of free electricity in Bangalore, apply online through the Seva Sindhu portal or the BESCOM website. You need your RR number, Aadhaar card, and a mobile number. After entering the required details and submitting the form, you will receive an acknowledgement. Once approved, the benefit is automatically applied to your electricity bill. No physical visit or offline submission is required.

The Gruha Jyothi Scheme is a Karnataka government initiative that provides up to 200 units of free electricity every month for domestic households. The benefit is based on a consumer’s average electricity usage. It applies to homeowners and tenants and is available across Karnataka under BESCOM, GESCOM, HESCOM, MESCOM, and CESC. The scheme helps reduce monthly household expenses.

You can apply for the Gruha Jyothi Scheme online through the Seva Sindhu services portal or your local ESCOM website. Enter your RR number, Aadhaar details, address, and ownership or tenant status. Upload basic documents if required and submit the application. After submission, download the acknowledgement for reference. Approval is processed digitally, and benefits reflect in future bills.

There is no manual activation required for the Gruha Jyothi Scheme. Once your application is approved by the concerned ESCOM, the scheme is activated automatically. The free electricity benefit is applied directly to your monthly bill. Consumers only need to ensure their application details are correct and track the status online using the application number.

Yes, tenants are eligible to apply for the Gruha Jyothi Scheme in Karnataka. They can use the RR number of the rented property along with their Aadhaar and a self-declaration form. In most cases, the property owner’s signature is not required. This makes the scheme accessible to people living in rented houses across the state.

The documents required are minimal and simple. Applicants need an Aadhaar card, RR number from the electricity bill, address details, and a mobile number linked to Aadhaar. Tenants must provide a self-declaration confirming their residence. All documents are submitted online, making the process quick and paperless.

To check the Gruha Jyothi application status, visit the Seva Sindhu portal and enter your application or acknowledgement number. The system will show whether your application is approved, pending, or rejected. Status updates help applicants understand when the benefit will be reflected in their electricity bill.

The free electricity units are calculated using your average monthly consumption from the previous year, with an additional 10% buffer. For example, if your average usage is 180 units, you may receive up to 198 units free. This method ensures fair distribution based on actual consumption patterns.

On average, 200 units of electricity cost between ₹900 and ₹1,200, depending on the ESCOM and tariff slab. Under the Gruha Jyothi Scheme, this amount becomes zero for eligible households. The savings help reduce monthly expenses, especially for middle- and lower-income families.

No, the Gruha Jyothi Scheme does not apply to commercial or industrial electricity connections. It is strictly meant for domestic household connections. Shops, offices, and business establishments are not eligible for this benefit under the scheme.
